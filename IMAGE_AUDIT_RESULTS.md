# FightingBooks Image Audit Results

**Date:** February 16, 2026  
**Total Images Reviewed:** 235  
**Images with Issues:** 175  
**Clean Images:** 60

---

## Executive Summary

This audit reviewed all 235 educational animal images (47 animals × 5 images each) for species accuracy, anatomical correctness, and suitability for a children's educational book.

**CRITICAL FINDINGS:**
- **30 images depict mythological creatures** (Cerberus, Chimera, Dragon, Griffin, Hydra, Kraken, Manticore, Phoenix) — completely inappropriate for an educational wildlife book
- **Extensive AI-generation artifacts** — deformed limbs, extra body parts, merged anatomy, unnatural symmetry
- **Species misidentification** — many images show the wrong animal or hybrid creatures
- **Content concerns** — graphic predation scenes, blood, frightening imagery

## Issues by Category

### 1. Mythological Creatures (NOT Real Animals)
**Total: 30 images across 6 "animals"**

These should be **removed entirely** or the book should be clearly marketed as including mythological creatures.

- **Cerberus (5 images):** All show multi-headed dogs, menacing/demonic imagery

- **Hydra (5 images):** All show multi-headed snakes

## Recommendations

### Immediate Actions Required

1. **Remove or clearly label mythological creatures** (30 images)
   - Either remove these 6 "animals" entirely, OR
   - Create a separate "Mythological Creatures" section with clear labeling
   - Current presentation misleads children about real vs fictional animals

2. **Replace images with major anatomy defects** (68 images)
   - Extra limbs, missing limbs, deformed bodies
   - Wrong species entirely
   - These are educational accuracy failures

3. **Review content appropriateness** (12 images)
   - Blood/graphic predation scenes
   - Frightening/menacing imagery
   - May be too intense for target age group

### Quality Control Recommendations

1. **Verify all AI-generated images** against real photographs
2. **Use paleontology consultants** for dinosaur accuracy
3. **Species verification** by zoologists/wildlife experts
4. **Age-appropriate content review** by child development specialists
