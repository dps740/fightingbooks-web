## Meet the Fighters

### The Crocodile

The saltwater crocodile, or *Crocodylus porosus*, reigns as one of the largest reptiles on Earth. Found in coastal areas, rivers, and swamps across Southeast Asia and northern Australia, this creature is a master of stealth in its aquatic environment. Known for its incredible power and aggression, the crocodile is a fearsome predator with a reputation that commands respect.

### The Walrus

The walrus, scientifically known as *Odobenus rosmarus*, is a fascinating marine mammal that thrives in Arctic and sub-Arctic regions. With its iconic tusks and blubbery body, the walrus is a social creature often found in large herds. They primarily feed on clams and other sea creatures, showcasing their adaptability to the icy waters.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Up to 2,000 pounds
- **Length / Height:** Up to 12 feet long!
- **Top Speed:** Can swim up to 35 mph! That's like a speeding car on the highway!

**Weapons:**
- Tusks up to 3 feet long!
- Strong flippers
- Massive body weight

**Fighting Style:**
Walruses primarily use their size and strength in battle. When threatened, they can use their powerful tusks to fend off attackers. They often rely on their thick blubber and tough skin as defenses, diving deep into the water when necessary to escape danger.
