## Meet the Fighters

### The Crocodile

The saltwater crocodile, or *Crocodylus porosus*, is the largest living reptile, known for its ferocity and powerful predatory skills. Found in coastal areas, rivers, and swamps, these ancient creatures have been around since the time of the dinosaurs. With a reputation for being ruthless hunters, saltwater crocodiles are feared by many animals that share their habitat.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Up to 2,200 pounds
- **Length / Height:** Up to 23 feet long!
- **Top Speed:** Can swim up to 20 mph! That's as fast as a running dog!

**Weapons:**
- Powerful jaws with a bite force of 3,700 pounds!
- Sharp teeth
- Long tail

**Fighting Style:**
Crocodiles are known for their ambush tactics. They often lie in wait, camouflaged in the water, and then strike with incredible speed when a potential meal comes too close. They usually hunt alone, relying on their stealth and strength to overpower their prey.

### The Leopard

The leopard, or *Panthera pardus*, is a magnificent big cat that thrives in a variety of habitats, including savannas, forests, and grasslands. Known for their beautiful spotted coats and incredible climbing skills, leopards are agile and stealthy hunters. They are not only strong but also clever, often using their surroundings to their advantage when stalking prey.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Between 66 to 176 pounds
- **Length / Height:** Can be up to 6.25 feet long!
- **Top Speed:** Can run up to 36 mph! 

**Weapons:**
- Powerful jaws (up to 2 inches long)
- Sharp claws
- Agile body

**Fighting Style:**
Leopards are versatile hunters that use their speed and agility to chase down prey or climb trees to escape danger. They often stalk through the underbrush, using their spots for camouflage, and then launch a swift attack. Unlike crocodiles, leopards can fight effectively on land and have the ability to retreat to trees if needed.
