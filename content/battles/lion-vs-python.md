## Meet the Fighters

### The Lion

Lions, known scientifically as *Panthera leo*, are the majestic kings of the grasslands, savannas, and open woodlands in Africa and India. With their iconic manes and powerful presence, they are one of the most recognized big cats in the world. Lions are social animals that live in groups called prides, which makes them unique among felines.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Up to 420 pounds
- **Length / Height:** About 8 to 10 feet long from nose to tail
- **Top Speed:** Can run up to 50 miles per hour for short bursts
- **Bite Force / Strength:** 650 PSI

**Weapons:**
- Powerful jaws with a bite force of 650 PSI
- Sharp claws up to 1.5 inches long
- Strong legs for pouncing

**Fighting Style:**
Lions typically employ a combination of strength and teamwork in their hunting strategies. They often use stealth and patience to ambush their prey, but when it comes to a fight, they can also unleash raw power with their strong legs and sharp claws. When hunting, they usually work in coordination with their pride, but they can also fight solo when the need arises.

### The Python

The green python, scientifically known as *Morelia viridis*, is a stunning snake that thrives in the tropical rainforests of New Guinea and Australia. These vibrant green serpents are known for their beautiful scales and excellent climbing abilities. While they may seem like gentle giants, they are skilled hunters with a powerful grip.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Up to 50 pounds
- **Length / Height:** Up to 13 feet long
- **Top Speed:** Can strike at speeds of 3 mph
- **Bite Force / Strength:** Not specified but capable through constriction

**Weapons:**
- Strong muscles for constriction (up to 1 foot in diameter)
- Sharp teeth
- Camouflage for sneaky ambushes

**Fighting Style:**
Pythons are ambush predators, relying on their excellent camouflage to blend into their surroundings. They strike swiftly, using their sharp teeth to grip their prey before wrapping their powerful bodies around it to constrict. This constriction can be incredibly strong, making it difficult for their prey to escape.
