## Meet the Fighters

### The Giant Panda

The Giant Panda, scientifically known as *Ailuropoda melanoleuca*, is a bear species native to the misty mountain bamboo forests of central China. These fluffy giants are famous for their distinctive black and white coloring and their voracious appetite for bamboo. Despite their cuddly appearance, Giant Pandas are incredibly powerful and surprisingly agile!

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** 330 pounds
- **Length / Height:** Up to 6 feet long
- **Top Speed:** Can run up to 20 mph in short bursts
- **Bite Force / Strength:** 292 PSI

**Weapons:**
- Jaw muscles and teeth strong enough to crush thick bamboo stalks
- Sharp claws on powerful forearms for climbing and swiping
- A special sixth finger (pseudo-thumb) made from a modified wrist bone, giving them incredible grip strength

**Fighting Style:**
Giant Pandas typically rely on their strength and agility. They are not aggressive by nature but can defend themselves if threatened. They may swipe with their powerful claws or use their strong jaws to bite when necessary. Pandas are solitary animals, meaning they generally fight alone, rather than in packs.

### The T-Rex

The Tyrannosaurus Rex, often simply referred to as T-Rex, is one of the most famous dinosaurs that ever lived. This massive predator roamed the Earth during the late Cretaceous period, about 68 to 66 million years ago. Known for its intimidating size and powerful jaws, the T-Rex was the ultimate apex predator of its time.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Approximately 9 tons (18,000 pounds)
- **Length / Height:** About 40 feet long
- **Top Speed:** Up to 20 mph (approx.)
- **Bite Force / Strength:** 8,000 PSI

**Weapons:**
- Massive, powerful jaws capable of crushing bone
- Sharp, serrated teeth designed to tear flesh
- Strong legs for quick bursts of speed

**Fighting Style:**
The T-Rex would engage in combat with brute force and ferocity. With its powerful bite and sharp teeth, it would go for a lethal strike aimed at its opponent's vital areas. T-Rex was a solitary hunter, relying on its size and strength to overpower prey.
