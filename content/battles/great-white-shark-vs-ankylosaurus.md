## Meet the Fighters

### The Great White Shark

The Great White Shark, scientifically known as *Carcharodon carcharias*, is one of the most formidable predators in the ocean. Renowned for its size, speed, and sharp teeth, this carnivore is found in oceans worldwide, particularly in coastal waters. With a reputation as a top predator, it has earned a fearsome place in marine ecosystems.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** 2,400 pounds
- **Length / Height:** Up to 20 feet long!
- **Top Speed:** Can swim up to 25 miles per hour!
  
**Weapons:**
- Jaws with 300 sharp teeth, each up to 3 inches long!
- Powerful tail
- Speedy body

**Fighting Style:**
Great White Sharks are ambush predators, often stalking their prey before launching a surprise attack. They rely on their incredible speed and sharp teeth to deliver devastating bites. These sharks often hunt alone but are skilled at taking down large prey, making them fearsome opponents in any confrontation.

### The Ankylosaurus

The Ankylosaurus, known scientifically as *Ankylosaurus magniventris*, was a giant herbivore that roamed the forests and plains during the Late Cretaceous period. With its heavily armored body and massive tail, this dinosaur was a living tank, equipped to defend itself against predators of all sizes.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Up to 6 tons
- **Length / Height:** About 26 feet long!
- **Top Speed:** Top speed of 8 mph!

**Weapons:**
- Clubbed tail (up to 3 feet long)
- Spiny back
- Thick armor

**Fighting Style:**
Ankylosaurus had a unique defense strategy thanks to its hard, armor-plated skin and low center of gravity. When threatened, it could swing its powerful clubbed tail to deliver a crushing blow to predators. This dinosaur relied on its formidable defenses and striking tail rather than agility, making it a tough opponent to overcome.
