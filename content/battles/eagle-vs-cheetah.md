## Meet the Fighters

### The Eagle

The Bald Eagle, a majestic symbol of strength and freedom, thrives near large bodies of open water where it can easily hunt for fish. With its striking white head and tail contrasting against a dark brown body, this bird commands respect in the skies. Not only is the Bald Eagle known for its beauty, but it is also a top predator in its habitat.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** 3 to 6.3 kg (6.6 to 14 lbs)
- **Length / Height:** Wingspan of up to 7.5 feet
- **Top Speed:** Can dive at speeds of 30-40 mph
- **Bite Force / Strength:** 7

**Weapons:**
- Sharp talons (up to 2.5 inches long)
- Strong beak
- Keen eyesight

**Fighting Style:**
Eagles are solitary hunters, relying on their incredible eyesight to spot prey from high in the sky. Once they identify their target, they swoop down with impressive speed to catch their meal off guard. Their talons are their primary weapons, allowing them to grab and immobilize their prey.

### The Cheetah

The Cheetah, renowned for its incredible speed, roams the savannas and grasslands of Africa. Known as the "fast cat," this sleek feline is built for speed, with a lightweight body and long legs that make it the fastest land animal, capable of reaching speeds that leave other animals in the dust. Cheetahs are also well-known for their distinctive black tear marks that help reduce glare from the sun as they hunt.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** 50 to 65 kg (110 to 140 lbs)
- **Length / Height:** Up to 4.5 feet tall at the shoulder and 3.5 to 4.5 feet long
- **Top Speed:** 60 to 70 mph
- **Bite Force / Strength:** 7

**Weapons:**
- Sharp claws (up to 2 inches long)
- Powerful jaws
- Speedy legs

**Fighting Style:**
Cheetahs are expert sprinters. They use their remarkable speed to chase down prey, often accelerating from 0 to 60 mph in just three seconds! Cheetahs prefer to hunt alone or in small groups, using a combination of camouflage and stealth to get as close to their target as possible before unleashing their incredible speed.
