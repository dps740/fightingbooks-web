## Meet the Fighters

### The Grizzly Bear

The grizzly bear (Ursus arctos horribilis) roams the forests, mountains, and national parks of North America, earning a reputation as a formidable giant. With its enormous size and powerful presence, the grizzly is often seen as one of nature's most awe-inspiring creatures. Known for its intelligence and adaptability, this bear can thrive in various habitats, making it a true survivor.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Up to 1,500 pounds
- **Length / Height:** Up to 8 feet long
- **Top Speed:** Can run up to 35 mph

**Weapons:**
- Powerful claws (up to 4 inches long)
- Strong jaws
- Massive body weight

**Fighting Style:**
Grizzly bears typically fight using their overwhelming strength and size. They rely on powerful swipes with their claws and strong bites to overpower opponents. Grizzlies are solitary fighters and tend to use direct attacks rather than ambush tactics, showcasing their raw power.

### The Leopard

The leopard (Panthera pardus) dwells in diverse habitats, including savannas, forests, and grasslands across Africa and parts of Asia. This agile cat is renowned for its stealth and hunting prowess, making it one of the most skilled predators in the wild. With its stunning spotted coat, the leopard can blend into its surroundings, allowing for sneak attacks on unsuspecting prey.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Between 66 to 176 pounds
- **Length / Height:** Up to 6.25 feet long
- **Top Speed:** Can run up to 36 mph

**Weapons:**
- Powerful jaws (up to 2 inches long)
- Sharp claws
- Agile body

**Fighting Style:**
Leopards are tactical hunters, often employing stealth and speed to their advantage. They prefer ambush tactics, waiting patiently for the right moment to strike. If the situation demands, leopards can also engage in direct confrontations, relying on their agility and quick reflexes to evade and counterattack.
