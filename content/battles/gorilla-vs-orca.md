## Meet the Fighters

### The Gorilla

Gorillas are the largest primates on Earth, known for their impressive strength and intelligence. Residing in the tropical forests of Africa, these gentle giants are primarily herbivores, munching on leaves and fruits. Despite their fearsome appearance, gorillas are often peaceful creatures, living in family groups led by a dominant male. 

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Up to 400 pounds
- **Length / Height:** Up to 6 feet tall and 12 feet long when standing on all fours! That’s as tall as a door!
- **Top Speed:** Can run up to 20 miles per hour! That’s as fast as a speeding car in a school zone!

**Weapons:**
- Strong arms with a reach of 8 feet
- Powerful jaws
- Intimidating chest thump

**Fighting Style:**
Gorillas typically rely on their immense strength and size in a fight. They prefer to intimidate opponents with displays of power, like chest-thumping and roaring. When defending their territory or family, they can launch a fierce attack, using their long arms to strike and grapple. 

### The Orca

Orcas, also known as killer whales, are the largest members of the dolphin family and are renowned for their intelligence and complex social structures. Found in oceans worldwide, especially in colder waters, these apex predators are skilled hunters, using teamwork to capture prey. Orcas are known to eat a variety of marine animals, including fish, seals, and even whales!

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Up to 12,000 pounds
- **Length / Height:** Up to 32 feet long!
- **Top Speed:** Can swim up to 34.5 mph! That’s faster than most speedboats!

**Weapons:**
- Powerful jaws with 40-56 teeth, each up to 4 inches long
- Speedy swimming
- Echolocation to find prey

**Fighting Style:**
Orcas are incredibly intelligent hunters that often work in pods to coordinate attacks. They use echolocation to locate prey and can swim at high speeds to close in on their targets. Their powerful jaws and sharp teeth make them formidable opponents in a fight.
