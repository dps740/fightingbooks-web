## Meet the Fighters

### The Grizzly Bear

The Grizzly Bear, scientifically known as *Ursus arctos horribilis*, roams the forests, mountains, and national parks of North America. These massive creatures are famous for their strength, speed, and remarkable intelligence. With a powerful presence, they command respect in their territory and are often seen fishing for salmon or foraging for berries.

- **Weight:** Up to 1,500 pounds
- **Length / Height:** Up to 8 feet long! That's as long as a school bus!
- **Top Speed:** Can run up to 35 mph! That's faster than a galloping horse — NEVER try to outrun a grizzly!

**Weapons:**
- Powerful claws (up to 4 inches long)
- Strong jaws
- Massive body weight

**Fighting Style:**
Grizzly Bears are solitary fighters, relying on their brute strength and quick reflexes. They typically use their powerful front claws to swipe at opponents, while their strong jaws deliver deadly bites. Grizzlies often stand on their hind legs to intimidate attackers, showcasing their size before charging in for an assault.

### The Ankylosaurus

The Ankylosaurus, or *Ankylosaurus magniventris*, was a formidable herbivore that lived during the Late Cretaceous period. This dinosaur was found in forests and plains, where it munched on low-growing plants and ferns. Its impressively built body was covered in thick armor, making it resemble a walking tank!

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Up to 6 tons! That's like 3 cars!
- **Length / Height:** About 26 feet long!
- **Top Speed:** Top speed of 8 mph! That's like a slow running kid!

**Weapons:**
- Clubbed tail (up to 3 feet long)
- Spiny back
- Thick armor

**Fighting Style:**
Ankylosaurus relied heavily on its defensive capabilities, using its hard, armor-plated skin to withstand attacks. When threatened, it could swing its powerful tail like a club to deliver crushing blows to predators. This dinosaur was not a predator but rather a master of defense, capable of standing its ground against fierce attackers.
