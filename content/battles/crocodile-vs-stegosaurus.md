## Meet the Fighters

### The Crocodile

The Saltwater Crocodile, scientifically known as *Crocodylus porosus*, is a master predator found in coastal areas, rivers, and swamps. These formidable reptiles are known for their stealth and strength, making them one of the top carnivorous animals on the planet.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Up to 2,200 pounds
- **Length / Height:** Up to 23 feet long!
- **Top Speed:** Can swim up to 20 mph! That's as fast as a running dog!

**Weapons:**
- Powerful jaws with a bite force of 3,700 pounds!
- Sharp teeth
- Long tail

**Fighting Style:**
Crocodiles are ambush hunters; they lie still in the water, camouflaged, waiting for the perfect moment to strike. With a powerful tail and strong jaws, they can launch a quick attack, dragging prey underwater. They often fight solo, using their size and strength to overpower opponents.

### The Stegosaurus

The Stegosaurus, or *Stegosaurus ungulatus*, roamed the Earth during the Late Jurassic period, inhabiting dry, open woodlands and plains. This herbivorous giant is famous for its distinctive back plates and spiked tail, making it an unforgettable figure in dinosaur history.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** About 5 tons (approximately 10,000 pounds)
- **Length / Height:** About 30 feet long!
- **Top Speed:** Can run up to 5 miles per hour! That's like a slow jog!

**Weapons:**
- Tail spikes (up to 2 feet long)
- Plates on back
- Strong legs

**Fighting Style:**
Although primarily an herbivore, the Stegosaurus can defend itself fiercely. When threatened, it can swing its tail, known as a "thagomizer," to strike at attackers. Its large size and intimidating presence can deter many predators, but it relies more on defense than offense.
