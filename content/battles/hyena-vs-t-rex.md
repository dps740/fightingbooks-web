## Meet the Fighters

### The Hyena

The Spotted Hyena, scientifically known as *Crocuta crocuta*, roams the savannas, grasslands, and woodlands of Africa. Often misunderstood, these fascinating creatures are known for their loud, cackling laughs and strong social structures, living in clans that can number up to 80 members!

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** 90-190 pounds
- **Length / Height:** Up to 4.5 feet tall at the shoulder and 5.5 to 6.5 feet long! That's about as long as a small sedan!
- **Top Speed:** Can run up to 37 miles per hour! That's faster than a speeding car in a school zone!
- **Bite Force / Strength:** 1,100 PSI!

**Weapons:**
- Powerful jaws with a bite force of 1,100 PSI!
- Sharp claws
- Strong legs for running

**Fighting Style:**
Hyenas are skilled fighters, often using their powerful jaws to crush bones and tear flesh. They are social animals that tend to hunt in packs but can also take down smaller prey alone. Hyenas are strategic hunters, using ambush tactics and teamwork to outsmart larger animals.

### The T-Rex

The iconic Tyrannosaurus Rex, or T-Rex for short, was one of the largest land predators to ever roam the Earth during the late Cretaceous period. Known for its massive size and formidable bite, this dinosaur was a top predator in its ecosystem.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Approximately 9 tons (18,000 pounds)
- **Length / Height:** About 40 feet long and 12 feet tall at the hips!
- **Top Speed:** Estimated at 20-25 miles per hour (approx.)
- **Bite Force / Strength:** 12,800 PSI!

**Weapons:**
- Enormous, serrated teeth capable of crushing bones
- Powerful legs for swift movement
- Strong arms with two sharp claws

**Fighting Style:**
The T-Rex relied on its immense size and strength to overpower its prey. It would use a combination of speed (for a creature of its size), powerful jaws, and sharp teeth to deliver devastating bites. While it was primarily a solitary hunter, it could take down large animals with sheer force.
