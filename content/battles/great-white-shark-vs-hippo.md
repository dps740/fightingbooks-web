## Meet the Fighters

### The Great White Shark

The Great White Shark, known scientifically as *Carcharodon carcharias*, roams the oceans worldwide, particularly in coastal waters. Often feared as a ferocious predator, this shark is renowned for its impressive size and hunting prowess.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** 2,400 lbs
- **Length / Height:** Up to 20 feet long!
- **Top Speed:** Can swim up to 25 miles per hour!
- **Bite Force / Strength:** Stronger than a T-Rex!

**Weapons:**
- Jaws with 300 sharp teeth, each up to 3 inches long!
- Powerful tail
- Speedy body

**Fighting Style:**
Great White Sharks are ambush predators. They typically hunt alone, using stealth and speed to surprise their prey. Their tactics involve breaching the water's surface to catch seals or fish off guard, delivering a lethal bite before retreating to assess their catch.

### The Hippo

The Common Hippopotamus, or *Hippopotamus amphibius*, inhabits rivers and lakes in Africa. Despite its seemingly docile appearance, the hippo is one of the most dangerous animals on the continent, known for its territorial nature and powerful jaws.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Up to 4,000 lbs
- **Length / Height:** Up to 16.5 feet long!
- **Top Speed:** Can run up to 19 mph!
- **Bite Force / Strength:** Massive jaws with 20-inch teeth.

**Weapons:**
- Massive jaws with 20-inch teeth
- Thick skin
- Powerful legs

**Fighting Style:**
Hippos are known for their aggressive behavior, especially when protecting their territory or young. They typically fight by charging at threats, using their bulk and powerful jaws to inflict severe damage. Though primarily herbivorous, hippos can be formidable opponents when provoked.
