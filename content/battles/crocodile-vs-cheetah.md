## Meet the Fighters

### The Crocodile

The Saltwater Crocodile, or *Crocodylus porosus*, is a formidable reptile known for its sheer size and strength. Found in coastal areas, rivers, and swamps, this creature is an apex predator, ruling its aquatic domain. With its tough, armored skin and fierce reputation for ambush attacks, the crocodile is a creature that commands respect.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Up to 2,200 pounds
- **Length / Height:** Up to 23 feet long!
- **Top Speed:** Can swim up to 20 mph
- **Bite Force / Strength:** 3,700 pounds!

**Weapons:**
- Powerful jaws with a bite force of 3,700 pounds!
- Sharp teeth
- Long tail

**Fighting Style:**
Crocodiles are ambush predators that excel at stealth. They often lie motionless in the water, camouflaged by their surroundings, waiting for the perfect moment to strike. Their method of attack typically involves a sudden rush out of the water to seize prey with their powerful jaws, using their sharp teeth to hold on tightly. While they often hunt alone, they are not above using their size and strength to intimidate rivals.

### The Cheetah

The Cheetah, or *Acinonyx jubatus*, is renowned for its incredible speed and agility. Native to the savannas and grasslands of Africa, this feline is built for speed, with a lightweight body and long legs designed for quick bursts. Its reputation as the fastest land animal is well-deserved, as it can reach astonishing speeds in mere seconds, making it a master hunter.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** 110 to 140 pounds
- **Length / Height:** Up to 4.5 feet tall at the shoulder and 3.5 to 4.5 feet long
- **Top Speed:** 60 to 70 mph
- **Bite Force / Strength:** (approx.) 200 pounds!

**Weapons:**
- Sharp claws (up to 2 inches long)
- Powerful jaws
- Speedy legs

**Fighting Style:**
Cheetahs rely on their incredible speed and agility to catch prey. They typically hunt solo or in small groups, employing a strategy of quick bursts to chase down smaller animals like gazelles. Their keen eyesight allows them to spot potential prey from a distance, and they use their stealth to get as close as possible before launching into a high-speed chase. If they catch their target, it’s often over in a matter of seconds!
