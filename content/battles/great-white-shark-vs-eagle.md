## Meet the Fighters

### The Great White Shark

The Great White Shark, scientifically known as *Carcharodon carcharias*, is one of the ocean's top predators. These powerful fish inhabit oceans worldwide, particularly in coastal waters where they hunt for seals, fish, and even other sharks. Great Whites are known for their impressive size, speed, and fearsome reputation, making them one of the most iconic sharks in the world.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Over 2,400 pounds
- **Length / Height:** Up to 20 feet long
- **Top Speed:** Can swim up to 25 miles per hour
- **Bite Force / Strength:** Stronger than a T-Rex's bite!

**Weapons:**
- Jaws with 300 sharp teeth, each up to 3 inches long
- Powerful tail
- Speedy body

**Fighting Style:**
Great White Sharks are ambush predators. They often use surprise attacks to catch their prey off guard, breaching the water in a spectacular display to grab seals. Their impressive speed allows them to accelerate quickly, making them formidable hunters in the water.

Bald Eagles primarily hunt fish but will also scavenge or hunt small mammals and other birds when necessary.
