## Meet the Fighters

### The Great Horned Owl

The Great Horned Owl, scientifically known as *Bubo virginianus*, is one of the most widespread owls in the Americas! Found in forests, deserts, swamps, and even bustling cities, this bird is a master of adaptation. With its striking tufts resembling ears and powerful presence, it's no wonder this owl is often called the "tiger of the skies."

**Fighting Style:**
The Great Horned Owl is a stealthy ambush predator. It often hunts alone, using its impressive silent flight to surprise prey. With a unique ability to turn its head almost fully around, it can spot threats and prey alike in near-total darkness.

### The Triceratops

The Triceratops, or *Triceratops horridus*, roamed the open plains and forests of North America during the late Cretaceous period. Its name means "three-horned face," and it is known for its three iconic horns and large bony frill at the back of its head. This herbivore was a gentle giant, munching on tough plants but was also a formidable opponent when threatened.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** 12,000 pounds (or about 6 tons)
- **Length / Height:** 30 feet long
- **Top Speed:** 20 mph
- **Bite Force / Strength:** Not specified (but strong due to large beak and body weight)

**Weapons:**
- Three powerful horns, each up to 3 feet long
- A beak designed for biting tough vegetation
- Heavy body capable of charging at predators

**Fighting Style:**
The Triceratops is not a predator, but when faced with a threat, it can be incredibly defensive. It uses its massive size and powerful horns to fend off attackers. If provoked, this dinosaur can charge, using its weight and speed to intimidate and injure foes, showcasing a powerful combination of defense and aggression.
