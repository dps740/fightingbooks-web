### The Mandrill

The mandrill is the largest monkey in the world, boasting vibrant colors and a robust build. Found in the dense tropical rainforests of Central Africa—specifically in Cameroon, Gabon, and the Congo—these primates are known for their striking blue and red facial features, which become even more vivid when they’re excited or angry. Mandrills live in large troops, often numbering up to 800 individuals, which provides them safety in numbers.

The cheetah is a sleek, agile predator that reigns supreme over the savannas and grasslands of Africa.
