## Meet the Fighters

### The Polar Bear

The polar bear, known scientifically as *Ursus maritimus*, is the ultimate apex predator of the Arctic. With a habitat primarily on sea ice, these magnificent creatures are built for survival in some of the harshest conditions on Earth. Renowned for their strength and hunting prowess, polar bears are expert swimmers and can travel great distances in search of food.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Up to 1,500 pounds
- **Length / Height:** Up to 10 feet long!
- **Top Speed:** Can run up to 25 miles per hour!
- **Bite Force / Strength:** 1,200 PSI

**Weapons:**
- Powerful jaws with a bite force of 1,200 PSI
- Sharp claws up to 2 inches long
- Strong legs for powerful swimming

**Fighting Style:**
Polar bears are solitary hunters, employing stealth and strength to ambush their prey. They often stalk seals on the ice, using their keen sense of smell and camouflage to get as close as possible before launching a powerful attack. Their fighting style is direct and aggressive, relying on brute strength and sharp claws to overpower opponents.

### The Hippo

The common hippopotamus, or *Hippopotamus amphibius*, is a behemoth that calls the rivers and lakes of Africa home. These hefty creatures are known for their incredible size and aggressiveness. Despite their seemingly docile appearance, hippos are one of the most dangerous animals in Africa, capable of charging at impressive speeds and delivering lethal bite forces.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Up to 4,000 pounds
- **Length / Height:** Up to 16.5 feet long!
- **Top Speed:** Can run up to 19 mph!
- **Bite Force / Strength:** Massive jaws with 20-inch teeth

**Weapons:**
- Massive jaws with 20-inch teeth
- Thick skin
- Powerful legs

**Fighting Style:**
Hippos are known for their aggressive nature and territorial behavior, especially when it comes to protecting their young. They often engage in confrontations by displaying their massive mouths and charging at threats. In water, hippos can be surprisingly agile, using their size and strength to assert dominance over rivals.
