## Meet the Fighters

### The Crocodile

The Saltwater Crocodile, known scientifically as *Crocodylus porosus*, is the largest reptile on Earth. These ancient creatures inhabit coastal areas, rivers, and swamps, expertly adapting to both freshwater and saltwater environments. With a reputation as skilled ambush predators, they are known for their powerful jaws and stealthy hunting tactics.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Up to 2,200 pounds
- **Length / Height:** Up to 23 feet long
- **Top Speed:** Can swim up to 20 mph
- **Bite Force / Strength:** 3,700 pounds

**Weapons:**
- Powerful jaws with a bite force of 3,700 pounds
- Sharp teeth
- Long tail

**Fighting Style:**
Crocodiles are ambush predators, using their incredible camouflage to blend into their surroundings. They often lie in wait for unsuspecting prey, then launch a sudden attack with lightning speed. When in a fight, they utilize their strong jaws and tail to inflict damage while relying on their tough, armored skin for protection.

### The T-Rex

The Tyrannosaurus rex, a legendary dinosaur from the late Cretaceous period, dominated the land over 66 million years ago. This colossal predator roamed the forests of what is now North America, instilling fear in its prey with its massive size and powerful build. With a reputation as the ultimate carnivore, the T-Rex had the strength and speed to secure its place at the top of the food chain.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Up to 18,000 pounds
- **Length / Height:** About 40 feet long
- **Top Speed:** Approximately 20 mph
- **Bite Force / Strength:** Estimated around 8,000 pounds

**Weapons:**
- Massive jaws with a bite force of around 8,000 pounds
- Sharp, serrated teeth
- Strong hind legs for powerful movement

**Fighting Style:**
The T-Rex relied on its immense size and strength to intimidate and overpower its victims. It would often charge at its prey with sheer force, using its powerful bite and sharp teeth to deliver fatal wounds. Unlike the stealthy crocodile, the T-Rex was more of a direct attacker, showcasing its dominance through brute strength.
