## Meet the Fighters

### The Crocodile

The Saltwater Crocodile, known scientifically as *Crocodylus porosus*, is a powerful and intimidating predator that reigns over coastal areas, rivers, and swamps. With a reputation as one of the largest reptiles on Earth, this crocodile is a master of ambush, lurking just beneath the surface of the water until it’s time to strike.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Up to 2,200 pounds
- **Length / Height:** Up to 23 feet long
- **Top Speed:** Can swim up to 20 mph
- **Bite Force / Strength:** 3,700 pounds

**Weapons:**
- Powerful jaws with a bite force of 3,700 pounds
- Sharp teeth
- Long tail

**Fighting Style:**
Crocodiles are ambush predators, often hiding in shallow waters or among reeds. When prey comes too close, they strike with astonishing speed, using their strong jaws to capture and hold their victim. They can fight alone, relying on their size and strength, and are not afraid to take on large animals.

### The Wolf

The Gray Wolf, or *Canis lupus*, is a highly social and intelligent animal that thrives in forests, tundras, and grasslands. Renowned for its pack mentality, the wolf has a reputation for being a skillful hunter, working alongside its pack to take down large prey like deer and elk.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** 50-110 pounds
- **Length / Height:** Up to 6.5 feet long (including tail)
- **Top Speed:** Can run up to 40 mph
- **Bite Force / Strength:** 1,500 PSI

**Weapons:**
- Powerful jaws (up to 1,500 PSI)
- Sharp claws
- Keen sense of smell

**Fighting Style:**
Wolves are social animals that typically hunt in packs, using teamwork to outmaneuver and overpower their prey. They rely on speed, agility, and their sharp teeth to inflict damage. While they can take on larger opponents, they excel when working together, using strategy to increase their chances of success.
