## Meet the Fighters

### The Gorilla

Gorillas are the largest primates on Earth, residing in the tropical forests of Africa. Known for their impressive strength and intelligence, these gentle giants are herbivores, eating a variety of plants and fruits. A male gorilla can weigh up to a staggering 400 pounds! That’s like carrying a small child!

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** 400 pounds
- **Length / Height:** Up to 6 feet tall and 12 feet long when standing on all fours! That's as tall as a door!
- **Top Speed:** Can run up to 20 miles per hour! That's as fast as a speeding car in a school zone!

**Weapons:**
- Strong arms with a reach of 8 feet
- Powerful jaws
- Intimidating chest thump

**Fighting Style:**
Gorillas typically fight using their immense strength and reach. Though they are generally peaceful, if provoked, they can fiercely defend their territory and family.

### The Eagle

The bald eagle is the national bird of the United States, found near large bodies of open water with abundant food supplies. With its striking white head and powerful wings, this majestic bird is known for its hunting prowess. Bald eagles primarily feast on fish, but they won’t hesitate to take down smaller mammals or birds when the opportunity arises.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Approximately 14 pounds
- **Length / Height:** Wingspan of up to 7.5 feet! That's longer than a sedan (15 feet) laid on its side!
- **Top Speed:** Can dive at speeds of 30-40 mph! That's as fast as a car on the highway!

**Weapons:**
- Sharp talons (up to 2.5 inches long)
- Strong beak
- Keen eyesight

**Fighting Style:**
Bald eagles are agile fighters, relying on their exceptional flying skills and sharp talons to catch their prey. They often swoop down from high altitudes, using their speed and precision to attack. Their keen eyesight allows them to spot potential threats or prey from great distances, making them formidable aerial hunters.
