With their stunning orange coats and black stripes, they are perfectly camouflaged in their natural habitat.

- **Length / Height:** Up to 10 feet long including the tail! That's as long as a SCHOOL BUS!

**Fighting Style:**
Tigers are solitary hunters, preferring to stalk their prey silently, using their excellent camouflage to get as close as possible before leaping into action. They rely on their incredible strength and agility to overpower their targets, often using a powerful pounce to take them down quickly.

### The Electric Eel

The electric eel, or *Electrophorus electricus*, is not really an eel at all; it's a type of knifefish that inhabits the murky waters of the Amazon and Orinoco basins in South America. This remarkable creature can grow up to 8 feet long and weighs around 44 pounds. With its ability to generate electric shocks, the electric eel is one of nature's most fascinating predators.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Up to 44 pounds
- **Length / Height:** Up to 8 feet long and 44 pounds — as long as a couch!
- **Top Speed:** Slow swimmers at about 3 mph.

**Weapons:**
- Can discharge up to 860 VOLTS — enough to stun a horse or knock a human unconscious!
- Three separate electric organs make up 80% of their body — they're basically a living BATTERY!
- Can pulse electricity to make hidden prey twitch, revealing their hiding spots.

**Fighting Style:**
Electric eels use their electrical abilities to attack prey, shocking them into submission before swallowing them whole. They can also use their electricity for defense, sending out powerful shocks to deter any potential threats. When it comes to fighting, they are more about strategy and stealth rather than brute force.
