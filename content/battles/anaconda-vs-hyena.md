## Meet the Fighters

### The Anaconda

The Green Anaconda, known scientifically as *Eunectes murinus*, is one of the largest snakes in the world. Found in the swamps and rivers of South America, this massive serpent is known for its stealth and strength. With its impressive size, the Anaconda has earned a fearsome reputation as a dominant predator in its habitat.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Up to 500 pounds!
- **Length / Height:** Up to 30 feet long! That's longer than a school bus!
- **Top Speed:** Can swim at 1.5 mph, like a slow jog!

**Weapons:**
- Powerful muscles (30 feet long)
- Camouflage
- Strong jaws

**Fighting Style:**
The Anaconda is a master of ambush. It often lies in wait, blending seamlessly into its surroundings, before striking with lightning speed. Once it captures its prey, it coils around it with incredible strength, squeezing until the victim can no longer breathe. This solitary hunter relies on stealth and brute force rather than pack tactics.

### The Hyena

The Spotted Hyena, known scientifically as *Crocuta crocuta*, is a fascinating carnivore that roams the savannas, grasslands, and woodlands of Africa. Often misunderstood, these social animals are known for their intelligence and complex social structures, living in clans that can number up to 80 individuals.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Up to 190 pounds!
- **Length / Height:** 5.5 to 6.5 feet long! That's about as long as a small sedan!
- **Top Speed:** Can run up to 37 miles per hour! That's faster than a speeding car in a school zone!

**Weapons:**
- Powerful jaws with a bite force of 1,100 PSI!
- Sharp claws
- Strong legs for running

**Fighting Style:**
Hyenas are known for their highly social nature and often hunt in packs. They use teamwork to corner and exhaust their prey, employing stamina and strategic communication. When confronted, they can be surprisingly fierce, using their powerful jaws to inflict significant damage. Their intimidating laugh also serves to scare off threats, showcasing their unique approach to survival.
