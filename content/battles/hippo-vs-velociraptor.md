## Meet the Fighters

### The Hippo

The common hippopotamus, or hippo for short, is a gigantic mammal that resides in rivers and lakes across Africa. Known for their bulky bodies, hippos can weigh up to 4,000 pounds and are often considered one of the deadliest animals in the world due to their aggressive nature.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** 4,000 pounds
- **Length / Height:** Up to 16.5 feet long
- **Top Speed:** Can run up to 19 mph
- **Bite Force / Strength:** Not specified

**Weapons:**
- Massive jaws with 20-inch teeth
- Thick skin
- Powerful legs

**Fighting Style:**
Hippos are solitary fighters, but they can be extremely aggressive, especially when defending their territory or young. They often use their large jaws to bite down on threats, showcasing their incredible bite strength. Even in water, hippos can be surprisingly quick and agile, making them formidable opponents.

### The Velociraptor

The velociraptor, scientifically known as Velociraptor mongoliensis, was a small but mighty dinosaur that roamed the dry, open areas of what is now Mongolia. With its sharp claws and keen hunting skills, this carnivorous creature was well-equipped for survival in a harsh environment.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** (approx.) 33 pounds
- **Length / Height:** About 6.5 feet long
- **Top Speed:** Top speed of 24 mph
- **Bite Force / Strength:** Not specified

**Weapons:**
- Clawed toes (up to 3 inches long)
- Sharp teeth
- Speed and agility

**Fighting Style:**
Velociraptors were known for their pack hunting behavior, often working together to take down larger prey. Their speed and agility allowed them to quickly dodge attacks and strike with their sharp claws and teeth. They relied on quick reflexes to outmaneuver their opponents, making them effective hunters.
