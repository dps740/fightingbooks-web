## Meet the Fighters

### The Gorilla

Gorillas are the gentle giants of the jungle, dominating the tropical forests of Africa. These incredible primates can weigh up to 400 pounds and stand as tall as 6 feet! Despite their massive size, gorillas are known for their gentle behavior and social structures. They are herbivores, munching on a variety of leaves, fruit, and flowers, and are famous for their intelligent social interactions.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** 400 pounds
- **Length / Height:** Up to 6 feet tall and 12 feet long when standing on all fours! That's as tall as a door!
- **Top Speed:** Can run up to 20 miles per hour! That's as fast as a speeding car in a school zone!

**Weapons:**
- Strong arms with a reach of 8 feet
- Powerful jaws
- Intimidating chest thump

**Fighting Style:**
Gorillas are known for their powerful strikes and incredible brute strength. They typically fight solo, using their strong arms and weight to intimidate or overpower opponents. When threatened, a gorilla may charge, pounding its chest to assert dominance while delivering powerful blows with its long arms.

### The Anaconda

The green anaconda is a true aquatic powerhouse, thriving in the swamps and rivers of South America. They can grow to an astonishing 30 feet in length and weigh over 500 pounds! Anacondas are carnivorous, known for their ability to hunt a wide range of prey, including fish, birds, and even capybaras. With their impressive camouflage and stealthy movements, these snakes are among the most formidable predators in their environment.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** 500 pounds
- **Length / Height:** Up to 30 feet long! That's longer than a SCHOOL BUS!
- **Top Speed:** Can swim at 1.5 mph, like a slow jog!

**Weapons:**
- Powerful muscles (30 feet long)
- Camouflage
- Strong jaws

**Fighting Style:**
Anacondas are ambush predators, relying on their exceptional camouflage to blend into their surroundings. When they strike, they wrap around their prey and constrict, using their immense strength to suffocate it. They are patient hunters, often lying in wait for the perfect moment to strike.
