## Meet the Fighters

### The Triceratops

The Triceratops, known scientifically as *Triceratops horridus*, roamed the open plains and forests of North America during the late Cretaceous period. This dinosaur is famous for its three massive horns and a large bony frill protecting its neck. Triceratops was not just a gentle giant; it had a tough reputation among predators, using its formidable weapons to defend itself.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** 12,000 pounds (about 12 tons)
- **Length / Height:** 30 feet long
- **Top Speed:** 20 mph

**Weapons:**
- Horns up to 3 feet long!
- Beak for biting
- Heavy body for charging

**Fighting Style:**
Triceratops often fought defensively, using its horns for thrusting and its heavy body for charging at threats. When threatened, it would lower its head and prepare to charge, using its horns to jab at attackers. This dinosaur preferred to face its opponents head-on, showcasing its strength and size in battle.

### The Stegosaurus

The Stegosaurus, scientifically named *Stegosaurus ungulatus*, lived in dry, open woodlands and plains during the late Jurassic period. Known for its distinctive double row of large plates along its back, this dinosaur was both a sight to behold and a clever survivor. While it had a reputation for being slow and cumbersome, Stegosaurus had some surprising tricks up its sleeve!

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** 5,000 pounds (about 5 tons)
- **Length / Height:** 30 feet long
- **Top Speed:** 5 mph

**Weapons:**
- Tail spikes (up to 2 feet long)
- Plates on back
- Strong legs

**Fighting Style:**
Stegosaurus relied heavily on its spiked tail, known as a "thagomizer," to defend itself against predators. When threatened, it would swing its tail to deliver powerful blows. The large plates on its back were not just for show; they may have helped with thermoregulation and intimidation. While not as fast as Triceratops, Stegosaurus was adept at using its unique defenses to deter attackers.
