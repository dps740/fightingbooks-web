## Meet the Fighters

### The Great White Shark

The Great White Shark, scientifically known as *Carcharodon carcharias*, is one of the most formidable creatures in the ocean. Found in coastal waters around the world, these apex predators are famous for their size and hunting prowess. With a reputation that sends shivers down the spines of even the bravest swimmers, Great Whites are known for their sharp teeth and incredible speed.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Over 2,400 pounds
- **Length / Height:** Up to 20 feet long!
- **Top Speed:** Can swim up to 25 miles per hour!
- **Bite Force / Strength:** Stronger than a T-Rex!

**Weapons:**
- Jaws with 300 sharp teeth, each up to 3 inches long!
- Powerful tail
- Speedy body

**Fighting Style:**
Great White Sharks are ambush predators. They typically strike by swimming swiftly toward their prey from below, using their powerful tails to propel themselves with incredible speed. They often attack solo, relying on their size and strength to overpower their victims, which include seals and fish.

### The Leopard

The Leopard, or *Panthera pardus*, is a sleek and stealthy big cat found in a variety of habitats, including savannas, forests, and grasslands. Known for their beautiful spotted coats and incredible climbing skills, Leopards are skilled hunters that can adapt to many environments. With their strong muscles and agility, they are among the most elusive predators in the animal kingdom.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Between 66 to 176 pounds
- **Length / Height:** Up to 6.25 feet long!
- **Top Speed:** Can run up to 36 mph!
- **Bite Force / Strength:** Powerful jaws (up to 2 inches long)

**Weapons:**
- Powerful jaws (up to 2 inches long)
- Sharp claws
- Agile body

**Fighting Style:**
Leopards are known for their stealthy hunting techniques. They can stalk their prey silently, often waiting for the perfect moment to pounce. Using their sharp claws and powerful jaws, they can quickly take down animals much larger than themselves. They also have the ability to climb trees, allowing them to escape danger or store their prey away from scavengers.
