### The Walrus

The walrus, or *Odobenus rosmarus*, is a massive marine mammal found in the chilly Arctic and sub-Arctic regions. Known for their long tusks and thick blubber, walruses are social creatures that often gather in large groups. While they may look sluggish on land, they are surprisingly agile in the water and can swim at impressive speeds.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Up to 2,000 pounds
- **Length / Height:** Up to 12 feet long
- **Top Speed:** Can swim up to 35 mph
- **Bite Force / Strength:** (approx.) Stronger than average predators

**Weapons:**
- Tusks up to 3 feet long
- Strong flippers for powerful swimming
- Massive body weight for intimidation and defense

**Fighting Style:**
Walruses typically rely on their size and tusks for defense. When threatened, they can use their tusks to jab at opponents or employ their mass to push adversaries away. They can be aggressive when protecting their young or territory, using their strong flippers to maneuver effectively in their aquatic environment.
