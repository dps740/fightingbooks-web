## Meet the Fighters

### The Lion

The lion, known scientifically as *Panthera leo*, is the quintessential symbol of strength and bravery in the animal kingdom. These magnificent creatures primarily inhabit grasslands, savannas, and open woodlands in Africa and India. Often referred to as the "king of the jungle," lions are social animals that live in groups called prides, showcasing a unique blend of teamwork and individual prowess.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Up to 420 pounds
- **Length / Height:** About 8 to 10 feet long from nose to tail
- **Top Speed:** Up to 50 miles per hour for short bursts
- **Bite Force / Strength:** 650 PSI

**Weapons:**
- Powerful jaws with a bite force of 650 PSI
- Sharp claws up to 1.5 inches long
- Strong legs for pouncing

**Fighting Style:**
Lions are known for their powerful ambush tactics, often working in groups to take down larger prey. They rely on their incredible strength and speed, using stealth to get close before launching a surprise attack. While they can fight alone, their teamwork allows them to dominate their rivals in the wild.

### The Hyena

The spotted hyena, or *Crocuta crocuta*, is a fascinating and often misunderstood creature. These animals thrive in savannas, grasslands, and woodlands across Africa. Often seen as scavengers, hyenas are actually skilled hunters with a reputation for their intelligence and complex social structures, living in clans that can include up to 80 members!

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Up to 190 pounds
- **Length / Height:** 5.5 to 6.5 feet long
- **Top Speed:** Up to 37 miles per hour
- **Bite Force / Strength:** 1,100 PSI

**Weapons:**
- Powerful jaws with a bite force of 1,100 PSI
- Sharp claws
- Strong legs for running

**Fighting Style:**
Hyenas are known for their pack mentality, often hunting and fighting in groups. They use their incredible stamina and agility to outmaneuver their opponents. When challenged, hyenas can be surprisingly aggressive, relying on their powerful bite and strategic teamwork to defend their territory or food.
