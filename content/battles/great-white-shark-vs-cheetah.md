## Meet the Fighters

### The Great White Shark

The Great White Shark, scientifically known as *Carcharodon carcharias*, reigns supreme in the oceans worldwide, especially in coastal waters. With its intimidating size and sharp teeth, this carnivorous predator is notorious for being a powerful hunter. These majestic creatures can be found patrolling the waters, always on the lookout for their next meal.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Over 2,400 pounds
- **Length / Height:** Up to 20 feet long!
- **Top Speed:** Can swim up to 25 miles per hour! That's faster than a speeding car!

**Weapons:**
- Jaws with 300 sharp teeth, each up to 3 inches long!
- Powerful tail
- Speedy body

**Fighting Style:**
Great White Sharks are ambush predators. They often use surprise attacks, lurking near the surface before launching themselves toward their prey with explosive speed. They rely on their massive size and powerful jaws to deliver a bone-crushing bite, making them fearsome opponents in the water.

### The Cheetah

The Cheetah, or *Acinonyx jubatus*, is the fastest land animal, found primarily in the savannas and grasslands of Africa. Known for its incredible speed and agility, the Cheetah is a master of the chase, able to sprint after its prey in short, explosive bursts. With its distinctive black tear marks under its eyes, this feline is built for speed and stealth.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** 110 to 140 pounds
- **Length / Height:** Up to 4.5 feet tall at the shoulder and 3.5 to 4.5 feet long
- **Top Speed:** Top speed of 60 to 70 mph! That's faster than a school bus on the highway!

**Weapons:**
- Sharp claws (up to 2 inches long)
- Powerful jaws
- Speedy legs

**Fighting Style:**
Cheetahs rely on their incredible speed to chase down prey, often sprinting at full speed for short distances. They are solitary hunters, focusing on quick, strategic attacks. With their sharp claws and powerful jaws, they can quickly take down smaller animals, but they need to be careful not to overexert themselves.
