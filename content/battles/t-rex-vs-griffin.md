## Meet the Fighters

### The T-Rex

The Tyrannosaurus rex, often dubbed the "T-Rex," roamed the Earth about 68 million years ago, primarily in what is now North America. With its massive frame and fearsome reputation, the T-Rex is a symbol of prehistoric power, known for being one of the largest carnivores of its time.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** 9 tons
- **Length / Height:** 40 feet long
- **Top Speed:** 20 miles per hour
- **Bite Force / Strength:** 12,800 pounds of bite force

**Weapons:**
- Powerful jaws with serrated teeth
- Muscular legs for swift movement
- Sharp claws on its tiny forearms

**Fighting Style:**
The T-Rex is a solitary predator that relies on its immense size and strength to overpower its prey. It employs a direct approach in combat, charging with powerful strides and utilizing its formidable bite to inflict damage. While its small arms may seem a disadvantage, they are strong enough to hold onto prey during a struggle.

### The Griffin

The Griffin, a mythical creature with the body of a lion and the head and wings of an eagle, is often found in tales of ancient ruins and mountainous regions. Known as a guardian of treasures, the Griffin is celebrated for its strength, courage, and majestic flight.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** (approx.) 1,200 pounds
- **Length / Height:** About 13 feet long and 6 feet tall at the shoulder
- **Top Speed:** Can fly up to 30 miles per hour
- **Bite Force / Strength:** (approx.) 1,000 pounds of bite force

**Weapons:**
- Sharp claws (up to 6 inches long)
- Powerful beak
- Talons for gripping

**Fighting Style:**
Griffins are agile fighters that combine aerial prowess with ground tactics. They swoop down from the sky to surprise their foes, using their sharp claws and beak to deliver quick, devastating strikes. Their ability to fly also provides them with a strategic advantage, allowing them to evade attacks and reposition quickly.
