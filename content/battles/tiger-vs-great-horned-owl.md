## Meet the Fighters

### The Tiger

The tiger, or Panthera tigris, reigns as the largest wild cat in the world! These magnificent creatures inhabit tropical forests, grasslands, and swamps across Asia. Known for their striking orange coats adorned with dark stripes, tigers are not only beautiful but also efficient hunters that stalk their prey with incredible skill.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** 500 to 660 pounds
- **Length / Height:** Up to 10 feet long including the tail! That's as long as a SCHOOL BUS!
- **Top Speed:** Can run up to 30 miles per hour! That's as fast as a speeding CAR!

**Weapons:**
- Powerful jaws with a bite force of 1,000 PSI
- Sharp claws up to 3 inches long
- Strong legs for pouncing

**Fighting Style:**
Tigers are solitary hunters, relying on stealth and strength to take down their prey. They often ambush from a distance before pouncing with their powerful legs and razor-sharp claws. Tigers are patient and cunning, waiting for the perfect moment to strike.

### The Great Horned Owl

The great horned owl, or Bubo virginianus, is a master of the night skies. This adaptable bird can be found in a variety of habitats, including forests, deserts, swamps, and even bustling cities across North and South America. With their recognizable tufts that resemble horns, great horned owls are both fierce and wise, known for their impressive hunting skills.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Up to 5.5 pounds
- **Length / Height:** Body is 2 feet long with a wingspan up to 5 FEET — wider than most adults are tall!
- **Top Speed:** Can dive at 40 mph and fly at 40 mph — as fast as a car in a neighborhood!

**Weapons:**
- Talons grip with 300 PSI of crushing force — same as a large dog's BITE!
- Silent flight feathers with serrated edges eliminate ALL sound — prey never hears them coming!
- Can rotate head up to 270 degrees — almost a full circle — spotting prey in near-total darkness!

**Fighting Style:**
Great horned owls are stealthy aerial hunters that rely on their silent flight and keen eyesight. They often hunt at night, swooping down on unsuspecting prey. When threatened, they can display fierce aggression, using their powerful talons to defend themselves.
