## Meet the Fighters

### The Lion

The lion, scientifically known as *Panthera leo*, is a powerful symbol of strength and bravery. These magnificent creatures are found in grasslands, savannas, and open woodlands across Africa and a small region in India. Known for their social structures, lions live in groups called prides, where they display teamwork in hunting and defending their territory.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Up to 420 pounds
- **Length / Height:** About 8 to 10 feet long from nose to tail
- **Top Speed:** 50 miles per hour (for short bursts)
- **Bite Force / Strength:** 650 PSI

**Weapons:**
- Powerful jaws with a bite force of 650 PSI
- Sharp claws up to 1.5 inches long
- Strong legs for pouncing

**Fighting Style:**
Lions are known for their brute strength and strategic hunting prowess. They typically rely on teamwork to hunt down prey, using ambush tactics to surprise unsuspecting animals. While lions can fight solo, their coordinated efforts in a pride give them a significant advantage. When challenged, a lion will use its powerful jaws and claws to assert dominance and defend its territory.

### The Cheetah

The cheetah, or *Acinonyx jubatus*, is famed for its incredible speed and agility, making it a master of the chase. Found in the savannas and grasslands of Africa, these sleek felines are built for speed, with a lightweight frame and long legs. Cheetahs are solitary or live in small groups, relying on their speed and stealth to catch smaller prey like gazelles and impalas.

**Physical Stats:**

Cheetahs rely on their lightning-fast speed to hunt, accelerating from 0 to 60 mph in just three seconds! They use their keen eyesight to spot prey from a distance and then sprint at incredible speeds to catch it. In a confrontation, a cheetah would use its agility and quick reflexes to dodge attacks, relying on its speed to escape rather than engage in a prolonged fight.
