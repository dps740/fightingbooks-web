## Meet the Fighters

### The Hammerhead Shark

The Hammerhead Shark is a remarkable creature known for its distinctive hammer-shaped head. These fascinating sharks roam warm tropical and temperate oceans worldwide, from vibrant coral reefs to the deep blue sea. With their incredible hunting prowess and unique anatomy, hammerheads are both fearsome predators and social creatures, often seen traveling in schools.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Up to 1,000 pounds
- **Length / Height:** Up to 20 feet long
- **Top Speed:** 25 mph in bursts

**Weapons:**
- Wide hammer head that pins prey to the ocean floor—like using a spatula!
- Rows of razor-sharp serrated teeth that constantly regrow
- Incredible speed bursts for ambush attacks

**Fighting Style:**
The Hammerhead Shark employs a combination of speed and strategy when hunting. They often ambush their prey, using their hammer head to pin it down, making it easier to bite. While they can hunt alone, they are also known to travel in schools of up to 500 sharks, providing safety in numbers and a coordinated attack strategy.

### The T-Rex

The Tyrannosaurus rex, or T-Rex, is one of the most iconic dinosaurs to ever roam the Earth. Known as the "king of the dinosaurs," this massive predator lived during the late Cretaceous period, around 68 to 66 million years ago. With powerful legs built for speed and incredibly strong jaws, the T-Rex was a formidable hunter on land.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Up to 9 tons (18,000 pounds)
- **Length / Height:** About 40 feet long and 12 feet tall at the hips
- **Top Speed:** Estimated 20-25 mph

**Weapons:**
- Enormous jaws with sharp, serrated teeth that could crush bone
- Strong, muscular legs for powerful movement
- Long, powerful tail for balance and defense

**Fighting Style:**
The T-Rex relied on its immense power and size to dominate its prey. With a mix of speed and strength, it could chase down smaller dinosaurs or ambush larger ones. Its massive jaws could deliver a bone-crushing bite, making it one of the most dangerous terrestrial predators of its time.
