## Meet the Fighters

### The Anaconda

The Green Anaconda, known scientifically as *Eunectes murinus*, is one of the largest snakes in the world. These colossal creatures inhabit the swamps and rivers of South America, where they are perfectly adapted to their environment. Renowned for their immense size and strength, Anacondas are top predators, striking fear into the hearts of smaller animals and even some larger ones!

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Over 500 pounds!
- **Length / Height:** Up to 30 feet long! That's longer than a school bus!
- **Top Speed:** Can swim at 1.5 mph, like a slow jog!

**Weapons:**
- Powerful muscles (30 feet long)
- Camouflage
- Strong jaws

**Fighting Style:**
Anacondas are ambush predators. They prefer to lie in wait, camouflaged in the water, until unsuspecting prey comes close. With a swift strike, they wrap their powerful bodies around their target, squeezing until it can no longer breathe. Anacondas typically hunt alone, relying on their sheer size and strength to overpower their foes.

### The Electric Eel

The Electric Eel, or *Electrophorus electricus*, is a fascinating fish that thrives in the murky freshwater rivers and swamps of the Amazon and Orinoco basins. Despite its name, it is not a true eel but belongs to a unique group of knifefish. Known for its remarkable ability to generate electricity, the Electric Eel is a master of surprise, using its shocks to incapacitate prey and defend itself from predators.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Up to 44 pounds!
- **Length / Height:** Up to 8 feet long— as long as a couch!
- **Top Speed:** Slow swimmers at about 3 mph.

**Weapons:**
- Can discharge up to 860 volts—enough to stun a horse!
- Three separate electric organs make up 80% of their body—like a living battery!
- Can pulse electricity to reveal hidden prey—like sonar but with lightning!

**Fighting Style:**
Electric Eels are not your typical fighters. They rely on their electric shocks to stun or paralyze prey, allowing them to swallow their catch whole. These fish can also use their electricity defensively, delivering shocks to deter any would-be attackers. Rather than engage in physical combat, Electric Eels prefer to zap their enemies from a safe distance.
