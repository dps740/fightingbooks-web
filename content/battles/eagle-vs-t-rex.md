## Meet the Fighters

### The Eagle

The Bald Eagle, a symbol of strength and freedom, soars across the skies of North America. Found near large bodies of water, these birds are expert hunters, relying on their sharp eyesight to spot fish swimming below the surface. With their impressive wingspan and powerful talons, Bald Eagles are formidable hunters in their own right.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** 3-6.3 kg (6.6-14 lbs)
- **Length / Height:** Wingspan of up to 7.5 feet!
- **Top Speed:** Can dive at speeds of 30-40 mph!
- **Bite Force / Strength:** Not specified (approx. 100-200 psi)

**Weapons:**
- Sharp talons (up to 2.5 inches long)
- Strong beak
- Keen eyesight

**Fighting Style:**
Bald Eagles are known for their aerial prowess. They often hunt solo and use ambush tactics, diving down at incredible speeds to catch their prey by surprise. Their sharp talons are designed for grabbing and holding onto their catch, while their powerful beaks can tear into flesh.

### The T-Rex

The Tyrannosaurus Rex, one of the largest carnivorous dinosaurs to roam the Earth, ruled the land during the late Cretaceous period. With its massive size and fearsome reputation, the T-Rex was an apex predator that instilled fear in other dinosaurs. Its powerful legs and sharp teeth made it a fearsome hunter in its prehistoric habitat.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Approximately 8,000 kg (17,600 lbs)
- **Length / Height:** About 40 feet long!
- **Top Speed:** Estimated 20-25 mph (though some say it could sprint faster!)
- **Bite Force / Strength:** 8,000 psi

**Weapons:**
- Enormous teeth (up to 12 inches long)
- Powerful legs
- Strong jaws

**Fighting Style:**
The T-Rex relied on brute force and intimidation. As a solitary hunter, it would often chase down prey, using its powerful legs to reach high speeds. Once within striking distance, it would use its massive jaws to deliver a bone-crushing bite, capable of taking down large dinosaurs with ease.
