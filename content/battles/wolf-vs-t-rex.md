## Meet the Fighters

### The Wolf

The Gray Wolf, scientifically known as Canis lupus, roams the forests, tundras, and grasslands of North America and beyond. These social creatures are famed for their pack mentality, hunting together to take down large prey like deer and elk. With a reputation for being both fierce and intelligent, wolves are remarkable survivors in the wild.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** 50-110 pounds
- **Length / Height:** Up to 6.5 feet long (including tail) - ABOUT AS LONG AS A SCHOOL BUS!
- **Top Speed:** Can run up to 40 mph - FASTER THAN A SPRINTING HUMAN!

**Weapons:**
- Powerful jaws (up to 1,500 PSI)
- Sharp claws
- Keen sense of smell

**Fighting Style:**
Wolves are strategic fighters, often utilizing teamwork to take down larger animals. They rely on speed and stealth, using their incredible sense of smell to track prey. While they typically hunt in packs, wolves are also capable of taking on smaller opponents solo when necessary.

### The T-Rex

The Tyrannosaurus rex, a fearsome predator that lived about 68 to 66 million years ago, was one of the largest land carnivores to ever walk the Earth. With its towering height and powerful legs, the T-Rex dominated the Late Cretaceous period. Renowned for its fearsome bite and massive size, this dinosaur is often depicted as the ultimate predator in popular culture.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Approximately 9 tons (18,000 pounds)
- **Length / Height:** About 40 feet long and over 12 feet tall at the hips
- **Top Speed:** Estimated at 20-25 mph (approx.)
- **Bite Force / Strength:** Over 8,000 PSI

**Weapons:**
- Massive jaws with serrated teeth
- Powerful legs for charging
- Strong tail for balance and swatting away threats

**Fighting Style:**
The T-Rex used its immense size and strength to overpower opponents. With a powerful bite capable of crushing bone, it relied on brute force rather than agility. While it may not have been the fastest dinosaur, its massive legs and sharp claws made it a formidable predator.
