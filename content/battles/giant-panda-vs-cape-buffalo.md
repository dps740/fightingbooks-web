## Meet the Fighters

### The Giant Panda

The giant panda, known scientifically as *Ailuropoda melanoleuca*, is a beloved bear species native to the misty mountain bamboo forests of central China. These gentle giants are famous for their black-and-white fur and strong affinity for bamboo, which makes up the majority of their diet. Despite their adorable appearance, giant pandas are powerful creatures capable of defending themselves when necessary.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Up to 330 pounds
- **Length / Height:** Up to 6 feet long
- **Top Speed:** Can run up to 20 mph in short bursts
- **Bite Force / Strength:** 292 PSI

**Weapons:**
- Jaw muscles and teeth strong enough to crush thick bamboo stalks
- Sharp claws on powerful forearms for climbing and swiping
- A special sixth finger (pseudo-thumb) made from a modified wrist bone

**Fighting Style:**
Giant pandas are not naturally aggressive, but if they feel threatened, they can use their size and strength to fight back. They tend to rely on their powerful jaws and sharp claws to defend themselves. Although they usually avoid confrontations, pandas can become formidable when provoked!

### The Cape Buffalo

The cape buffalo, scientifically known as *Syncerus caffer*, roams the grasslands and savannas of Africa. Often called the "Black Death," these massive animals can weigh as much as a small car and are notorious for their tough and aggressive nature. Cape buffaloes are social animals that often travel in herds, providing them with safety in numbers.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Up to 2,000 pounds

**Fighting Style:**

Buffaloes also work together in herds to fend off predators, using their impressive horns and sheer mass to intimidate and overpower opponents.
