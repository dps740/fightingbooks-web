## Meet the Fighters

### The Lion

The lion, scientifically known as *Panthera leo*, roams the grasslands, savannas, and open woodlands of Africa and India. Often called the "king of the jungle," lions are social creatures that live in groups called prides, making them formidable hunters.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** 420 pounds
- **Length / Height:** About 8 to 10 feet long from nose to tail
- **Top Speed:** 50 miles per hour
- **Bite Force / Strength:** 650 PSI

**Weapons:**
- Powerful jaws
- Sharp claws up to 1.5 inches long
- Strong legs for pouncing

**Fighting Style:**
Lions are skilled hunters that often rely on teamwork and strategy. They typically ambush their prey, using their speed and strength to deliver a quick and powerful attack. When hunting in a pride, they coordinate their movements to encircle their target, making them a fearsome force in the animal kingdom.

### The Triceratops

The Triceratops, or *Triceratops horridus*, was a herbivorous dinosaur that lived in North America during the late Cretaceous period. Known for its impressive size and distinctive three horns, this dinosaur roamed the open plains and forests, munching on tough plants and shrubs.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** 12,000 pounds
- **Length / Height:** 30 feet long
- **Top Speed:** 20 miles per hour

**Weapons:**
- Three powerful horns up to 3 feet long
- Beak for biting
- Heavy body for charging

**Fighting Style:**
Triceratops are not only known for their size but also their defensive tactics. When threatened, they can charge at predators, using their horns to deliver formidable blows. Their large bony frill acts as a shield, helping to protect them from attacks while they defend themselves.
