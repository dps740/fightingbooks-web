## Meet the Fighters

### The Polar Bear

The polar bear, known scientifically as *Ursus maritimus*, roams the icy realms of the Arctic, primarily hunting on sea ice. These majestic creatures are renowned for their strength and size, making them the largest land carnivores on Earth.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Up to 1,500 pounds
- **Length / Height:** Up to 10 feet long!
- **Top Speed:** Can run up to 25 miles per hour!
- **Bite Force / Strength:** 1,200 PSI

**Weapons:**
- Powerful jaws with a bite force of 1,200 PSI
- Sharp claws up to 2 inches long
- Strong legs for powerful swimming

**Fighting Style:**
Polar bears are solitary hunters that rely on stealth and brute strength. They often ambush their prey, using their incredible sense of smell to detect seals from nearly a mile away. In a fight, they utilize their massive size and powerful claws, striking with speed and tenacity.

### The Giant Panda

The giant panda, scientifically known as *Ailuropoda melanoleuca*, calls the misty bamboo forests of central China its home. These bears might look cute and cuddly, but they are surprisingly powerful and well-equipped for survival in their mountainous habitat.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Up to 330 pounds
- **Length / Height:** Up to 6 feet long
- **Top Speed:** Can run up to 20 miles per hour!
- **Bite Force / Strength:** 292 PSI

**Weapons:**
- Jaw muscles and teeth capable of crushing thick bamboo stalks with a bite force of 292 PSI
- Sharp claws on powerful forearms for climbing and swiping
- A special sixth finger (pseudo-thumb) that enhances grip strength

**Fighting Style:**
Giant pandas are not aggressive by nature but can defend themselves fiercely if threatened. They often rely on their climbing abilities to escape danger, but in a confrontation, they can use their strong jaws and claws to swipe at opponents.
