## Meet the Fighters

### The Electric Eel

The Electric Eel (scientific name: *Electrophorus electricus*) is a fascinating creature found in the murky freshwater rivers and swamps of the Amazon and Orinoco basins in South America. Despite its name, this eel is actually a fish and can grow up to an astonishing 8 feet long! Known for its ability to produce powerful electric shocks, the Electric Eel is a master at subduing its prey and defending itself from threats.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** 44 pounds
- **Length / Height:** Up to 8 feet
- **Top Speed:** 3 mph

**Weapons:**
- Can discharge up to 860 volts—enough to stun a horse!
- Three separate electric organs make up 80% of its body.
- Can pulse electricity to reveal hidden prey, like sonar but with lightning!

**Fighting Style:**
The Electric Eel employs a stealthy approach in combat, often ambushing its prey. It uses its electric shocks to incapacitate fish, amphibians, and even small mammals. When threatened, it can unleash a series of shocks to deter attackers, making it an effective solo fighter.

### The Triceratops

The Triceratops (scientific name: *Triceratops horridus*) roamed the open plains and forests of North America during the Late Cretaceous period. This iconic herbivore was known for its three powerful horns and large bony frill, which provided both defense and a striking appearance. Weighing around 12 tons, the Triceratops was a force to be reckoned with!

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** 12,000 pounds (or 12 tons)
- **Length / Height:** 30 feet long
- **Top Speed:** 20 mph

**Weapons:**
- Three formidable horns, each up to 3 feet long!
- A beak designed for biting tough plants.
- A heavy body capable of charging at predators.

**Fighting Style:**
The Triceratops uses its impressive weight and speed to its advantage. It can charge at threats, using its horns to defend itself from predators or rivals. With a powerful bite and strong legs, the Triceratops is well-equipped for close combat and can stand its ground against attackers.
