## Meet the Fighters

### The Gorilla

The Gorilla is a giant primate known for its impressive size and intelligence. Native to the tropical forests of Africa, these gentle giants are mostly herbivores and live in family groups. With their massive arms and powerful presence, gorillas are often considered one of the strongest animals on land!

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Up to 400 pounds!
- **Length / Height:** Up to 6 feet tall and 12 feet long when standing on all fours! That's as tall as a door!
- **Top Speed:** Can run up to 20 miles per hour! That's as fast as a speeding car in a school zone!

**Weapons:**
- Strong arms with a reach of 8 feet
- Powerful jaws
- Intimidating chest thump

**Fighting Style:**
Gorillas typically fight using their immense strength and reach. They are not known for aggression but will defend their territory and family if threatened. They may charge or swing their arms to intimidate opponents, using their size to their advantage in a direct confrontation.

### The Walrus

The Walrus is a massive marine mammal often found lounging on ice floes in the Arctic and sub-Arctic regions. With their thick blubber and impressive tusks, walruses are well-equipped for survival in their harsh habitat. They are social creatures, often seen in groups, and they love to feast on clams and other sea creatures!

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Up to 2,000 pounds, that's like a small car!
- **Length / Height:** Up to 12 feet long!
- **Top Speed:** Can swim up to 35 mph! That's like a speeding car on the highway!

**Weapons:**
- Tusks up to 3 feet long!
- Strong flippers
- Massive body weight

**Fighting Style:**
Walruses primarily use their tusks for defense and dominance displays. When threatened, they can charge at predators with their massive size. In fights, they may rely on their weight and tusks to fend off attackers, using their strong flippers to maneuver both in and out of water.
