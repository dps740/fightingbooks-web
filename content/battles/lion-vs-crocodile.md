## Meet the Fighters

### The Lion

The lion, known scientifically as *Panthera leo*, roams the grasslands, savannas, and open woodlands of Africa and India. These magnificent creatures are social animals that live in groups called prides. Their reputation as powerful hunters and majestic rulers of the animal kingdom is well-deserved.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Up to 420 pounds
- **Length / Height:** About 8 to 10 feet long from nose to tail
- **Top Speed:** 50 miles per hour for short bursts
- **Bite Force / Strength:** 650 PSI

**Weapons:**
- Powerful jaws with a bite force of 650 PSI
- Sharp claws up to 1.5 inches long
- Strong legs for pouncing

**Fighting Style:**
Lions often hunt in groups, using their strength in numbers to take down large prey. When fighting, a lion employs its powerful legs to pounce on its opponent while delivering crushing bites with its powerful jaws. They can also utilize their sharp claws for slashing attacks, making them formidable opponents in close combat.

### The Crocodile

The saltwater crocodile, or *Crocodylus porosus*, is a master of stealth found in coastal areas, rivers, and swamps. As the largest reptile on Earth, this ancient predator has a fearsome reputation, lurking just below the water's surface, waiting for the perfect moment to strike.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Up to 2,200 pounds
- **Length / Height:** Up to 23 feet long
- **Top Speed:** Can swim up to 20 miles per hour
- **Bite Force / Strength:** 3,700 pounds!

**Weapons:**
- Powerful jaws with a bite force of 3,700 pounds
- Sharp teeth
- Long, powerful tail

**Fighting Style:**
Crocodiles are ambush predators that rely on stealth and surprise. They can remain submerged for long periods, using their camouflage to blend into the water. When attacking, they lunge with incredible speed, using their powerful jaws to grab and hold onto prey. Their long tail can also be utilized for powerful strikes, making them a double threat.
