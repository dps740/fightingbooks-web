## Meet the Fighters

### The Tiger

The tiger, known scientifically as *Panthera tigris*, is a majestic hunter that roams the tropical forests, grasslands, and swamps of Asia. With its striking orange coat and black stripes, the tiger is not just a symbol of beauty but also a fierce predator revered in many cultures around the world.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Up to 660 pounds!
- **Length / Height:** Up to 10 feet long including the tail! That's as long as a SCHOOL BUS!
- **Top Speed:** Can run up to 30 miles per hour! That's as fast as a speeding CAR!

**Weapons:**
- Powerful jaws with a bite force of 1,000 PSI
- Sharp claws up to 3 inches long
- Strong legs for pouncing

**Fighting Style:**
Tigers are solitary hunters and prefer to stalk their prey silently through the underbrush. They use their incredible camouflage to ambush unsuspecting animals, relying on stealth and a powerful leap to pounce on their target. Once engaged, they use their strong jaws and razor-sharp claws to deliver devastating blows.

### The Gorilla

The gorilla, scientifically known as *Gorilla gorilla*, is the largest primate on Earth, living in the lush tropical forests of Africa. Known for their intelligence and social behavior, gorillas are gentle giants but can be incredibly powerful when threatened.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Up to 400 pounds!
- **Length / Height:** Up to 6 feet tall and 12 feet long when standing on all fours! That's as tall as a door!
- **Top Speed:** Can run up to 20 miles per hour! That's as fast as a speeding car in a school zone!

**Weapons:**
- Strong arms with a reach of 8 feet
- Powerful jaws
- Intimidating chest thump

**Fighting Style:**
Gorillas primarily rely on their size and strength to defend themselves. They can display aggressive behavior by thumping their chests, which serves as both a warning and a way to establish dominance. When fighting, they use their powerful arms to strike, grapple, and push their opponent back, leveraging their impressive reach to keep threats at bay.
