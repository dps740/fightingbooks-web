## Meet the Fighters

### The Grizzly Bear

The grizzly bear, scientifically known as *Ursus arctos horribilis*, is a heavy-hitter in the animal kingdom. Found in forests, mountains, and national parks across North America, these bears are known for their strength and ferocity. Grizzlies are omnivorous and can be seen foraging for berries, catching fish, or even hunting down small mammals.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Up to 1,500 pounds
- **Length / Height:** Up to 8 feet long! That's as long as a school bus!
- **Top Speed:** Can run up to 35 mph! That's faster than a galloping horse — NEVER try to outrun a grizzly!

**Weapons:**
- Powerful claws (up to 4 inches long)
- Strong jaws
- Massive body weight

**Fighting Style:**
Grizzly bears are solitary fighters, relying on their brute strength and fearsome claws to take down prey. They usually attack directly, using their powerful limbs and speed for short bursts. They can also be defensive, standing their ground against threats while using their incredible sense of smell to detect danger from afar.

### The Dragon

The fire-breathing dragon, known scientifically as *Draco Ignis*, is a legendary creature that has captured the imaginations of people for centuries. These majestic beasts dwell in mountains and caves, often depicted as fierce protectors of treasure. With their carnivorous diets, they feast on large animals, including deer and even other dragons!

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Around 1,500 pounds
- **Length / Height:** 30 feet long (about the length of a school bus!)
- **Top Speed:** Top speed of 60 mph! That's as fast as a car on the highway!

**Weapons:**
- Fire Breath (up to 1,000°F)
- Sharp claws (2 feet long)
- Tail swipe (5 feet wide)

**Fighting Style:**
Dragons are aerial combatants, using their ability to fly to gain a height advantage over their opponents. They often attack from the air with their fire breath before swooping down to strike with their sharp claws or powerful tail. Their camouflage also allows them to blend into rocky terrain, making them stealthy and unpredictable in battle.
