The tiger, known scientifically as *Panthera tigris*, is the largest cat species in the world and a true apex predator. Found in tropical forests, grasslands, and swamps across Asia, tigers are renowned for their striking orange fur with black stripes and their stealthy hunting abilities. ### The Stegosaurus

The stegosaurus, scientifically known as *Stegosaurus ungulatus*, roamed the Earth during the late Jurassic period, around 150 million years ago. This iconic dinosaur is famous for its massive size and the distinctive bony plates that run along its back. Living in dry, open woodlands and plains, the stegosaurus was a herbivore, munching on plants, leaves, and ferns to satisfy its enormous appetite.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** About 5 tons (10,000 pounds)
- **Length / Height:** About 30 feet long!
- **Top Speed:** Can run up to 5 miles per hour! That's like a slow jog!

**Weapons:**
- Tail spikes (up to 2 feet long)
- Plates on back
- Strong legs

**Fighting Style:**
Stegosaurus typically relied on its impressive size and armor for defense rather than aggression. However, when threatened, it could use its powerful tail, equipped with sharp spikes known as a "thagomizer," to deliver devastating blows to attackers. Stegosaurus often stood its ground, using its intimidating size to deter potential predators.
