## Meet the Fighters

### The Great White Shark

The Great White Shark, scientifically known as *Carcharodon carcharias*, is a magnificent creature that roams the oceans worldwide, especially in coastal waters. Renowned for its size and predatory prowess, the Great White has earned a fearsome reputation as one of the ocean's top predators.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Over 2,400 pounds
- **Length / Height:** Up to 20 feet long!
- **Top Speed:** Can swim up to 25 miles per hour!
  
**Weapons:**
- Jaws with 300 sharp teeth, each up to 3 inches long!
- Powerful tail
- Speedy body

**Fighting Style:**
The Great White Shark typically relies on stealth and surprise. It often ambushes its prey from below, using its speed and powerful jaws to deliver a devastating bite. This solitary hunter uses its acute senses to detect even the faintest traces of blood in the water, making it a formidable opponent.

### The Orca

The Orca, or *Orcinus orca*, is also known as the killer whale. These remarkable animals inhabit oceans worldwide, particularly in colder waters. Orcas are not only impressive hunters but also highly intelligent and social creatures that live in family groups called pods.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Up to 12,000 pounds
- **Length / Height:** Up to 32 feet long!
- **Top Speed:** Can swim up to 34.5 miles per hour!

**Weapons:**
- Powerful jaws with 40-56 teeth, each up to 4 inches long
- Speedy swimming
- Echolocation to find prey

**Fighting Style:**
Orcas are known for their highly coordinated hunting tactics, especially when they hunt in pods. They use sophisticated communication and echolocation to track prey, often working together to outsmart and overpower larger animals. Their intelligence and social structure give them an edge in battles.
