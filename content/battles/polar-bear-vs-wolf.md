## Meet the Fighters

### The Polar Bear

The polar bear, known scientifically as *Ursus maritimus*, reigns as the king of the Arctic. Adapted to life on sea ice, these massive carnivores are primarily found in the icy waters of the Arctic regions, where they hunt for seals and other marine animals. With their thick white fur and layer of blubber, polar bears are not only powerful but also perfectly suited to their frigid habitat.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Up to 1,500 pounds
- **Length / Height:** Up to 10 feet long!
- **Top Speed:** Can run up to 25 miles per hour
- **Bite Force / Strength:** 1,200 PSI

**Weapons:**
- Powerful jaws with a bite force of 1,200 PSI
- Sharp claws up to 2 inches long
- Strong legs for powerful swimming

**Fighting Style:**
Polar bears are solitary hunters, relying on their immense size and strength to overpower prey. They use their sharp claws and powerful jaws to deliver crushing bites. Polar bears often ambush seals resting on the ice, using stealth and patience to get close before pouncing.

### The Wolf

On the other side of the battle, we have the gray wolf, scientifically known as *Canis lupus*. These intelligent pack animals thrive in forests, tundras, and grasslands, showcasing their adaptability and social behavior. Wolves are known for their teamwork when hunting, often working together to take down larger prey like deer and elk.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** 50-110 pounds
- **Length / Height:** Up to 6.5 feet long (including tail)
- **Top Speed:** Can run up to 40 miles per hour
- **Bite Force / Strength:** 1,500 PSI

**Weapons:**
- Powerful jaws with a bite force of 1,500 PSI
- Sharp claws
- Keen sense of smell

**Fighting Style:**
Wolves are known for their strategic hunting tactics, often using their speed and agility to outmaneuver prey. When fighting, they rely on their pack mentality, coordinating attacks and flanking their opponent to wear them down. However, a lone wolf will fight with speed and agility, using quick bites and evasive maneuvers.
