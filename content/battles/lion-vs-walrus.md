## Meet the Fighters

### The Lion

The lion (Panthera leo) is a majestic predator known for its power and social behavior. Roaming the grasslands, savannas, and open woodlands of Africa and India, lions are the epitome of strength and teamwork, often hunting in groups called prides. Their reputation as fierce hunters and the “king of the jungle” makes them one of the most iconic animals on the planet.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Up to 420 pounds
- **Length / Height:** About 8 to 10 feet long from nose to tail
- **Top Speed:** Can run up to 50 miles per hour for short bursts
- **Bite Force / Strength:** 650 PSI

**Weapons:**
- Powerful jaws with a bite force of 650 PSI
- Sharp claws up to 1.5 inches long
- Strong legs designed for pouncing

**Fighting Style:**
Lions are strategic hunters that rely on teamwork and stealth. They often stalk their prey quietly before launching a sudden ambush. While they can hunt solo, they are more effective in groups, coordinating their attacks to take down larger animals. Their powerful legs and sharp claws give them an advantage when it comes to striking.

### The Walrus

The walrus (Odobenus rosmarus) is a massive marine mammal known for its impressive size and long tusks. Found in the icy waters of the Arctic and sub-Arctic regions, walruses have a unique charm and a social nature, often gathering in large groups. Their blubbery bodies and formidable tusks make them a sight to behold in the frigid ocean.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Up to 2,000 pounds
- **Length / Height:** Up to 12 feet long
- **Top Speed:** Can swim up to 35 mph
- **Bite Force / Strength:** Not specified (but strong due to massive body weight)

**Weapons:**
- Tusks up to 3 feet long
- Strong flippers
- Massive body weight

**Fighting Style:**
Walruses are known for their impressive defense mechanisms rather than aggressive fighting styles. They primarily use their massive size and powerful tusks to fend off threats. In the water, their strong flippers allow them to navigate quickly, and they can dive deep to escape danger. Though they are not typically aggressive, they can be fierce when protecting their territory.
