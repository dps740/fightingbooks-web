## Meet the Fighters

### The Lion

The lion, known scientifically as *Panthera leo*, reigns supreme in the grasslands, savannas, and open woodlands of Africa and India. Often referred to as the “King of the Jungle,” this majestic big cat is not only admired for its striking mane but also feared for its incredible hunting prowess. Lions are social creatures, living in groups called prides, which adds to their reputation as formidable predators.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Up to 420 pounds
- **Length / Height:** About 8 to 10 feet long from nose to tail
- **Top Speed:** Can run up to 50 miles per hour for short bursts
- **Bite Force / Strength:** 650 PSI

**Weapons:**
- Powerful jaws with a bite force of 650 PSI
- Sharp claws up to 1.5 inches long
- Strong legs for pouncing

**Fighting Style:**
Lions are known for their strategic hunting tactics, often working in teams to take down larger prey. They can ambush their targets, using the element of surprise, or engage in direct confrontations. When fighting, a lion will typically rely on its strength and speed, using powerful swipes with its claws and biting down hard with its strong jaws.

### The Giant Panda

The giant panda, scientifically known as *Ailuropoda melanoleuca*, resides in the misty mountain bamboo forests of central China, particularly in the provinces of Sichuan, Shaanxi, and Gansu. While these bears are often perceived as cute and cuddly, they possess incredible strength and resilience. Pandas are primarily herbivores, spending most of their time munching on bamboo, but they are more than capable of defending themselves when needed.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Up to 330 pounds
- **Length / Height:** Up to 6 feet long
- **Top Speed:** Can run up to 20 miles per hour in short bursts
- **Bite Force / Strength:** 292 PSI

**Weapons:**
- Jaw muscles and teeth strong enough to crush thick bamboo stalks
- Sharp claws on powerful forearms for climbing and swiping
- A special sixth finger (pseudo-thumb) for incredible grip strength

**Fighting Style:**
Giant pandas are not aggressive by nature, but they can defend themselves fiercely when threatened. Their fighting style mainly relies on their powerful forearms, using swipes and strikes against potential threats. They can also climb trees quickly to escape danger, which adds an extra layer of defense.
