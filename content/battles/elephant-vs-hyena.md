## Meet the Fighters

### The Elephant

The African Elephant, scientifically known as *Loxodonta africana*, is the largest land animal on Earth. These gentle giants inhabit savannas, forests, and grasslands across Africa, where they are often seen wandering in herds. Known for their intelligence and social bonds, elephants are revered in many cultures but also face threats from poaching and habitat loss.

**Fighting Style:**
Elephants generally use their size and strength to defend themselves rather than attacking. When threatened, they can charge at intruders, using their tusks and massive bodies to push enemies away. ### The Hyena

The Spotted Hyena, known scientifically as *Crocuta crocuta*, is a highly social carnivore found in the savannas, grasslands, and woodlands of Africa. With a reputation for being scavengers, hyenas are often misunderstood; they are skilled hunters and can take down prey much larger than themselves. Living in clans that can number up to 80, these animals have a complex social structure and communicate with each other using distinctive calls.

**Weapons:**

**Fighting Style:**
Hyenas are known for their cunning tactics and pack mentality. They often hunt in groups, using teamwork to outsmart their prey. When defending themselves, they can be aggressive, using their powerful jaws and sharp claws to inflict damage. Their intimidating laugh can also scare off potential threats.
