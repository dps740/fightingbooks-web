## Meet the Fighters

### The Wolf

The Gray Wolf, or Canis lupus, roams diverse habitats, including forests, tundras, and grasslands. Known for their incredible social structure, these animals often hunt in packs, making them formidable predators. With their keen senses and strong pack mentality, wolves are respected and revered in many cultures.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Up to 110 lbs
- **Length / Height:** Up to 6.5 feet long (including tail) - ABOUT AS LONG AS A SCHOOL BUS!
- **Top Speed:** Can run up to 40 mph - FASTER THAN A SPRINTING HUMAN!

**Weapons:**
- Powerful jaws (up to 1,500 PSI)
- Sharp claws
- Keen sense of smell

**Fighting Style:**
Wolves are strategic fighters, often relying on teamwork to take down prey. They are known for their ambush tactics and can launch coordinated attacks on larger animals. While they can fight solo, their strength lies in their pack, allowing them to create distractions and flank their opponents.

### The Hyena

The Spotted Hyena, scientifically known as Crocuta crocuta, primarily inhabits the savannas, grasslands, and woodlands of Africa. Often misunderstood due to their eerie laugh, hyenas are intelligent and resourceful hunters. Their social structures, known as clans, can include up to 80 members, making them a powerful force in the animal kingdom.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Up to 190 lbs
- **Length / Height:** Up to 4.5 feet tall at the shoulder and 5.5 to 6.5 feet long! That's about as long as a small sedan!
- **Top Speed:** Can run up to 37 miles per hour! That's faster than a speeding car in a school zone!

**Weapons:**
- Powerful jaws with a bite force of 1,100 PSI!
- Sharp claws
- Strong legs for running

**Fighting Style:**
Hyenas are tenacious fighters, often using their powerful jaws to crush bone and defend against threats. They typically hunt in packs, utilizing their numbers to intimidate and overwhelm prey. Their unique laugh and vocalizations serve both as communication and a means to assert dominance over competitors.

## The Battle

The sun sets over the African savanna, casting long shadows as the stage is set for the ultimate showdown. On one side, a pack of Gray Wolves, sleek and agile, eyes the horizon. The battle intensifies as the wolves dart in and out, trying to land quick bites while avoiding the hyenas' powerful jaws. The wolves, realizing they cannot win a direct confrontation, decide to use their intelligence. They initiate a distraction, causing a few hyenas to chase after them. This allows a couple of wolves to sneak around and attempt to isolate a weaker hyena. However, the hyenas are clever; they use their numbers to their advantage, surrounding the lone wolf and launching a coordinated attack.

As the dust settles, the hyenas emerge victorious, having successfully defended their territory against the cunning wolves. Although both animals fought fiercely, the sheer weight advantage and pack strategy of the hyenas tipped the scale in their favor.

While both animals are strong, the hyena is generally considered stronger due to its larger size and powerful bite force. The wolf, however, has a higher bite force, which can also be very damaging in a fight.

**What are the odds in a Wolf vs Hyena fight?**
