## Meet the Fighters

### The Walrus

The walrus, scientifically known as *Odobenus rosmarus*, is a large marine mammal found in the icy waters of the Arctic and sub-Arctic regions. With its distinctive tusks and sociable nature, this creature is known for its ability to gather in large groups, often lounging on ice floes. Walruses are primarily carnivorous, feasting on clams, mollusks, and other sea creatures, making them impressive predators in their icy habitats.

**Fighting Style:**

In a confrontation, a walrus may charge at its opponent, using its blubber to absorb blows while delivering powerful strikes with its tusks.

### The Stegosaurus

The stegosaurus, known scientifically as *Stegosaurus ungulatus*, roamed the Earth during the late Jurassic period. This iconic dinosaur is instantly recognizable due to its distinctive row of bony plates along its back and its spiked tail. The stegosaurus inhabited dry, open woodlands and plains, munching on a diet of plants, leaves, and ferns. Despite its massive size, the stegosaurus had a surprisingly tiny brain for its size!

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** About 5 tons (as much as 2 cars!)
- **Length / Height:** About 30 feet long!
- **Top Speed:** Can run up to 5 miles per hour! That's like a slow jog!
- **Bite Force / Strength:** 7 (out of 10)

**Weapons:**

**Fighting Style:**
The stegosaurus's primary defense mechanism is its impressive tail, armed with spikes that can inflict serious injury on any predator. When threatened, this dinosaur would likely adopt a defensive stance, swinging its tail to ward off attackers while using its armored plates as additional protection. Although not particularly fast, its sheer size and strength make it an intimidating opponent in any battle.
