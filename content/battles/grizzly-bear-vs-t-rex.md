## Meet the Fighters

### The Grizzly Bear

The Grizzly Bear, scientifically known as *Ursus arctos horribilis*, is a formidable presence in the forests and mountains of North America. Renowned for its strength and ferocity, this bear can weigh up to 1,500 pounds and measure about 8 feet long—making it as long as a school bus! Grizzly Bears are omnivorous, feasting on a diverse diet that includes berries, fish, and even small mammals.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Up to 1,500 pounds
- **Length / Height:** Up to 8 feet long
- **Top Speed:** Can run up to 35 mph

**Fighting Style:**

When threatened, they often stand tall to intimidate rivals, displaying their massive stature before charging in with fierce speed.

### The T-Rex

The Tyrannosaurus rex, or T-Rex for short, was one of the largest land predators to ever walk the Earth. This dinosaur roamed during the late Cretaceous period and could reach a staggering weight of around 9 tons (that’s 18,000 pounds!). With a length of up to 40 feet, the T-Rex was not just big, but incredibly powerful, with a bite force that is estimated to be among the strongest in history.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Up to 18,000 pounds
- **Length / Height:** Up to 40 feet long
- **Top Speed:** Estimated at 20-25 mph
- **Bite Force / Strength:** Not specified (but very high)

**Weapons:**
- Massive, serrated teeth
- Strong hind legs for powerful attacks
- Size and weight advantage

**Fighting Style:**
T-Rex was a dominant predator with a fighting style built on brute strength. It would use its powerful legs to charge at prey, delivering devastating bites with its massive teeth. With an impressive sense of smell, it could track down its next meal from miles away. The T-Rex often relied on its size to overpower opponents, using its weight to crush any threats.
