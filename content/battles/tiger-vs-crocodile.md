## Meet the Fighters

### The Tiger

The tiger, scientifically known as *Panthera tigris*, is one of the largest and most powerful cats in the world. Found in tropical forests, grasslands, and swamps across Asia, these magnificent creatures are not just beautiful; they're also apex predators. With their striking orange fur and black stripes, tigers are experts at blending into their environments, making them formidable hunters.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** 220 to 660 pounds
- **Length / Height:** Up to 10 feet long including the tail! That's as long as a SCHOOL BUS!
- **Top Speed:** Can run up to 30 miles per hour! That's as fast as a speeding CAR!
- **Bite Force / Strength:** 1,000 PSI

**Weapons:**
- Powerful jaws with a bite force of 1,000 PSI
- Sharp claws up to 3 inches long
- Strong legs for pouncing

**Fighting Style:**
Tigers are solitary hunters that prefer to ambush their prey. They stalk quietly through the underbrush, using their incredible camouflage to get as close as possible before launching a powerful leap. With their strength and agility, they can take down large animals, making them fierce fighters when threatened.

### The Crocodile

Enter the saltwater crocodile, known scientifically as *Crocodylus porosus*. Found in coastal areas, rivers, and swamps, this reptile is the largest living reptile on Earth. With a reputation for being fierce and territorial, crocodiles are equipped with an arsenal of weaponry that makes them one of the most dangerous predators in the water.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Up to 2,200 pounds
- **Length / Height:** Up to 23 feet long!
- **Top Speed:** Can swim up to 20 mph! That's as fast as a running dog!
- **Bite Force / Strength:** 3,700 PSI

**Weapons:**
- Powerful jaws with a bite force of 3,700 pounds!
- Sharp teeth
- Long tail

**Fighting Style:**
Crocodiles are ambush predators that lie in wait for their prey, using their camouflage to blend into the water. When an unsuspecting animal comes close, they strike with lightning speed, using their powerful jaws to clamp down on their target. Their thick, armored skin provides excellent protection, making them tough opponents in a fight.

**What are the odds in a Tiger vs Crocodile fight?**
