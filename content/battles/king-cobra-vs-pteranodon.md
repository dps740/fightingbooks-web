### The Pteranodon

Enter the Pteranodon (scientific name: Pteranodon longiceps), a flying reptile that ruled the skies during the Late Cretaceous period, around 70 million years ago. With a wingspan of up to 33 feet, the Pteranodon was a master of flight, soaring over coastal areas in search of fish and squid. Though not a dinosaur, it remains one of the most recognized prehistoric creatures and a symbol of ancient aviation.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Estimated at around 44 pounds (20 kg) (approx.)
- **Length / Height:** About 20 feet long — longer than a car but shorter than a school bus!

**Weapons:**

**Fighting Style:**
The Pteranodon primarily uses its size and speed to evade predators while hunting for fish. When threatened, it can quickly take to the skies, using its impressive wings to escape danger. If forced to defend itself, it can use its sharp beak and claws for attack, but it generally prefers to avoid conflicts altogether.
