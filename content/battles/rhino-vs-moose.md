## Meet the Fighters

### The Rhino

The rhino, specifically the White Rhino, is an impressive herbivore found in the grasslands and savannas of Africa. With its enormous size and tough exterior, the rhino has earned a reputation as a formidable presence in its habitat. Despite their somewhat clumsy appearance, these animals are surprisingly agile and can be quite aggressive when threatened.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** 5,000 lbs
- **Length / Height:** Up to 13 feet long!
- **Top Speed:** 30 mph!

**Weapons:**
- HORN: Up to 3 feet long!
- THICK SKIN
- POWERFUL LEGS

**Fighting Style:**
Rhinos are known for their direct and aggressive fighting style. When threatened, they charge at their opponents using their powerful legs and sharp horn as weapons. Rhinos usually fight solo, relying on their strength and size to intimidate and overpower foes.

### The Moose

The moose, or Alces alces, is a majestic creature commonly found in the forests and wetlands of North America, Europe, and parts of Asia. With its impressive height and massive antlers, the moose is both a sight to behold and a powerful contender in any animal battle. These gentle giants are known for their herbivorous diet, munching on leaves, twigs, and even aquatic plants.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** 1,500 lbs
- **Length / Height:** 6 to 7.5 feet tall at the shoulder!
- **Top Speed:** 35 mph!

**Weapons:**
- Antlers up to 6 feet wide!
- Strong legs
- Kicking power

**Fighting Style:**
Moose typically defend themselves using their large antlers and powerful legs. They can kick with great force, making them a dangerous opponent when threatened. Moose often stand their ground, using their size to intimidate attackers while delivering powerful strikes if necessary.
