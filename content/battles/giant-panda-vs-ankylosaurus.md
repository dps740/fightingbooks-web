## Meet the Fighters

### The Giant Panda

The giant panda, scientifically known as *Ailuropoda melanoleuca*, is a bear native to the misty mountain bamboo forests of central China, specifically in the provinces of Sichuan, Shaanxi, and Gansu. These majestic creatures are famous for their striking black and white fur and their diet that consists almost entirely of bamboo. Despite their cuddly appearance, giant pandas possess incredible strength that is often underestimated.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** 330 pounds
- **Length / Height:** Up to 6 feet long
- **Top Speed:** Can run up to 20 mph in short bursts
- **Bite Force / Strength:** 292 PSI

**Weapons:**

- Sharp claws on powerful forearms for climbing and swiping
- A special sixth finger (pseudo-thumb) that enhances grip strength

**Fighting Style:**
Giant pandas are primarily solitary animals and prefer to keep to themselves. However, when threatened, they can be surprisingly aggressive. Their fighting style relies on their powerful bite and sharp claws, which they use to defend themselves or compete for resources. They may attack by swiping at their opponent or delivering a powerful bite, often using their climbing skills to get an advantageous position.

### The Ankylosaurus

The Ankylosaurus, scientifically referred to as *Ankylosaurus magniventris*, roamed the Earth during the Late Cretaceous period. This tank-like dinosaur could reach lengths of about 26 feet and weighed anywhere from 4 to 6 tons! Known for its heavily armored body and clubbed tail, the Ankylosaurus was an herbivore that primarily munched on low-growing plants and ferns. Its robust defenses made it one of the most well-protected dinosaurs of its time.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Up to 13,000 pounds (6 tons)

- **Top Speed:** 8 mph
- **Bite Force / Strength:** Not specified

**Fighting Style:**
Ankylosaurus had a unique fighting style defined by its defensive posture. When threatened, it would often remain low to the ground, displaying its thick armor. If an attacker approached, it could swing its clubbed tail with incredible force, making it a formidable opponent. This dinosaur relied more on defense than offense, using its natural armor and tail as its primary means of protection.
