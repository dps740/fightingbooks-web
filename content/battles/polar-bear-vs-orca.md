## Meet the Fighters

### The Polar Bear

The polar bear, scientifically known as *Ursus maritimus*, reigns supreme in the frigid Arctic regions, primarily relying on sea ice for survival. Known for their impressive size and majestic presence, polar bears are often seen as symbols of the wild, embodying both beauty and strength.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Up to 1,500 pounds
- **Length / Height:** Up to 10 feet long!
- **Top Speed:** Can run up to 25 miles per hour! That's as fast as a car in a school zone!

**Weapons:**
- Powerful jaws with a bite force of 1,200 PSI
- Sharp claws up to 2 inches long
- Strong legs for powerful swimming

**Fighting Style:**
Polar bears are solitary hunters, using stealth and strength to ambush their prey, primarily seals. They rely on their powerful limbs to swim and navigate icy waters, while their keen sense of smell helps them locate food from nearly a mile away. In a fight, polar bears may use their weight and claws to overpower their opponent.

### The Orca

The orca, or *Orcinus orca*, is not only one of the ocean's most formidable predators but also one of the most intelligent creatures in the marine world. These incredible animals can be found in oceans worldwide, especially thriving in colder waters. Known for their striking black and white coloration, orcas are often referred to as "killer whales" for their apex predator status.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Up to 12,000 pounds
- **Length / Height:** Up to 32 feet long!
- **Top Speed:** Can swim up to 34.5 mph! That's faster than most speedboats!

**Weapons:**
- Powerful jaws with 40-56 teeth, each up to 4 inches long
- Speedy swimming
- Echolocation to find prey

**Fighting Style:**
Orcas are highly social animals that often hunt in pods, using teamwork and communication to strategize their attacks. They are known for their remarkable intelligence, which they employ to outsmart both prey and potential threats. In a confrontation, orcas might use their speed and agility to dodge attacks while delivering powerful bites.
