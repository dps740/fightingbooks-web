## Meet the Fighters

### The Eagle

The Bald Eagle, a symbol of strength and freedom, thrives near large bodies of water where it expertly hunts for fish. With its striking white head and tail contrasted against a dark brown body, this bird is not just a pretty sight—it’s a fierce predator with impressive skills.

- **Length / Height:** Wingspan of up to 7.5 feet
- **Top Speed:** 30-40 mph when diving
- **Bite Force / Strength:** N/A

**Weapons:**
- Sharp talons (up to 2.5 inches long)
- Strong beak
- Keen eyesight

**Fighting Style:**
The Bald Eagle utilizes its incredible speed and agility, often employing a strategy of surprise attacks from above. It swoops down, using its talons to grasp prey, and can even carry off small mammals if they venture too close to its nest. This solitary hunter prefers to strike quickly and efficiently rather than engage in prolonged battles.

### The Hyena

The Spotted Hyena, often misunderstood and underestimated, prowls the savannas and grasslands of Africa. With a reputation for being the "laughing" predator, these creatures are highly social and often hunt in packs, giving them a tactical advantage over solitary foes.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** 40-86 kg (88-190 lbs)
- **Length / Height:** Up to 4.5 feet tall at the shoulder and 5.5 to 6.5 feet long
- **Top Speed:** 37 mph
- **Bite Force / Strength:** 1,100 PSI

**Weapons:**
- Powerful jaws with a bite force of 1,100 PSI
- Sharp claws
- Strong legs for running

**Fighting Style:**
Hyenas are clever and tenacious fighters, often using teamwork to overpower prey. They can intimidate with their loud, haunting laughs and will not hesitate to defend their territory fiercely. Though they can hunt alone, they are more effective in packs, where they coordinate attacks and take advantage of their numbers.
