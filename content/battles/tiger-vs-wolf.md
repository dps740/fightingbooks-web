## Meet the Fighters

### The Tiger

Tigers are the largest wild cats on the planet, boasting a powerful physique that strikes fear into the hearts of their prey. Found in tropical forests, grasslands, and swamps across Asia, these magnificent creatures are not just beautiful—they're also formidable hunters. Tigers are solitary animals, relying on their stealth and strength to take down their food. 

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** 220 to 660 pounds! That's like 2 to 3 average-sized people!
- **Length / Height:** Up to 10 feet long including the tail! That's as long as a SCHOOL BUS!
- **Top Speed:** Can run up to 30 miles per hour! That's as fast as a speeding CAR!
- **Bite Force / Strength:** 1,000 PSI

**Weapons:**
- Powerful jaws with a bite force of 1,000 PSI
- Sharp claws up to 3 inches long
- Strong legs for pouncing

**Fighting Style:**
Tigers are stealthy ambush predators. They stalk their prey through dense foliage, using their camouflage to blend into the environment. Once close enough, they launch a powerful pounce, using their strength to overpower their target. Unlike wolves, tigers fight alone, relying on their sheer power and ferocity.

### The Wolf

Wolves, specifically gray wolves, are social creatures that thrive in packs. Their habitats range from forests to tundras and grasslands, showcasing their adaptability and intelligence. Known for their loyalty and strong family bonds, wolves hunt cooperatively, using teamwork to take down larger prey. 

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** 50 to 110 pounds, similar to a child!
- **Length / Height:** Up to 6.5 feet long (including tail) - ABOUT AS LONG AS A SCHOOL BUS!
- **Top Speed:** Can run up to 40 mph - FASTER THAN A SPRINTING HUMAN!
- **Bite Force / Strength:** 1,500 PSI

**Weapons:**
- Powerful jaws with a bite force of 1,500 PSI
- Sharp claws
- Keen sense of smell

**Fighting Style:**
Wolves are known for their pack mentality, often hunting and fighting in a group. Their strategy typically involves surrounding their target, using speed and agility to outmaneuver their prey. While they can fight solo, they are most effective when working together as a team, coordinating their attacks to wear down opponents.
