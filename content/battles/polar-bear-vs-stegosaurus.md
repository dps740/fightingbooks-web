## Meet the Fighters

### The Polar Bear

The Polar Bear, scientifically known as *Ursus maritimus*, roams the icy realms of the Arctic, primarily on sea ice. With its majestic white fur and powerful physique, it has earned its reputation as the world’s largest land carnivore. These incredible animals rely heavily on their hunting skills to catch seals, fish, and even birds, making them apex predators of their environment.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Up to 1,500 pounds
- **Length / Height:** Up to 10 feet long
- **Top Speed:** Can run up to 25 miles per hour
- **Bite Force / Strength:** 1,200 PSI

**Weapons:**
- Powerful jaws with a bite force of 1,200 PSI
- Sharp claws up to 2 inches long
- Strong legs for powerful swimming

**Fighting Style:**
Polar Bears are known for their ambush hunting style, often relying on stealth and patience. They typically hunt alone, using their keen sense of smell to locate seals beneath the ice. When it comes to a fight, they leverage their size and strength, using powerful swipes of their claws and their formidable bite.

### The Stegosaurus

The Stegosaurus, or *Stegosaurus ungulatus*, thrived in the dry, open woodlands and plains during the late Jurassic period. This iconic dinosaur is recognized for its distinctive back plates and spiked tail, making it one of the most recognizable prehistoric creatures. Though it was an herbivore, the Stegosaurus was no pushover; it used its impressive size and defensive features to deter predators.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** About 10,000 pounds
- **Length / Height:** About 30 feet long
- **Top Speed:** Can run up to 5 miles per hour

**Weapons:**
- Tail spikes (up to 2 feet long)
- Plates on back
- Strong legs

**Fighting Style:**
Stegosaurs primarily relied on their size and armor for defense rather than aggressive fighting tactics. If threatened, they would use their powerful tails, capable of delivering painful strikes with their tail spikes, known as a "thagomizer." This dinosaur’s fighting style is mostly about intimidation and defense, rather than offense.
