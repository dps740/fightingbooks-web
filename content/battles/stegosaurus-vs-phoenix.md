## Meet the Fighters

### The Stegosaurus

The Stegosaurus, scientifically known as *Stegosaurus ungulatus*, roamed the dry, open woodlands and plains during the late Jurassic period. This iconic dinosaur is easily recognized by its unique double row of bony plates along its back and its spiked tail, used for defense. Known as a gentle giant, the Stegosaurus was a herbivore, munching on plants, leaves, and ferns to sustain its massive body.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** 5 tons
- **Length / Height:** About 30 feet long!
- **Top Speed:** Can run up to 5 miles per hour!
  
**Weapons:**
- Tail spikes (up to 2 feet long)
- Plates on back
- Strong legs

**Fighting Style:**
The Stegosaurus primarily relies on its impressive size and defensive capabilities. In a fight, it would use its long, spiked tail, known as a "thagomizer," to swing at opponents, while its armored plates provide crucial protection against attacks. Though not a predator, it could stand its ground against threats, using its weight and strength to intimidate.

### The Phoenix

The Phoenix is a legendary bird from mythology, known for its stunning plumage and fiery existence. Residing in fiery mountains and mystical realms, this creature has captivated imaginations for centuries. The Phoenix is said to feed on sunlight and absorb energy from flames, making it a symbol of rebirth and immortality.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** About 200 pounds
- **Length / Height:** About 15 feet long! 
- **Top Speed:** Can fly up to 100 mph!

**Weapons:**
- Fiery talons (1 foot long)
- Blazing beak
- Fire breath

**Fighting Style:**
The Phoenix is a fierce aerial fighter, utilizing its speed and agility in the skies. It swoops down on opponents with its fiery talons and can unleash scorching fire breath. When in danger, the Phoenix can retreat into the skies, making it hard to catch. Its ability to be reborn from ashes provides a unique advantage, as it can return to the fray even after defeat.
