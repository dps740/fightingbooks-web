## Meet the Fighters

### The Elephant

The African elephant (Loxodonta africana) roams across the savannas, forests, and grasslands of Africa. Known for their intelligence, social behavior, and impressive size, elephants are the largest land animals on Earth. These gentle giants are herbivores, spending their days munching on grass, leaves, fruits, and bark.

**Fighting Style:**
Elephants are known for their brute strength. In a confrontation, they use their tusks and trunk to deliver powerful blows. They can charge at high speeds, using their enormous size to intimidate foes. Elephants usually fight solo, standing their ground against threats, relying on their sheer weight and size to fend off attackers.

### The Anaconda

The green anaconda (Eunectes murinus) is a giant snake native to the swamps and rivers of South America. Famous for being one of the largest snakes in the world, anacondas are known for their remarkable ability to blend into their aquatic surroundings. These carnivorous reptiles can take down prey much larger than themselves, making them formidable predators.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Can weigh over 500 pounds! That's like a small car!
- **Length / Height:** Up to 30 feet long! That's longer than a SCHOOL BUS!
- **Top Speed:** Can swim at 1.5 mph, like a slow jog!

**Weapons:**
- Powerful muscles (30 feet long)
- Camouflage
- Strong jaws

**Fighting Style:**
Anacondas are ambush predators, often relying on stealth and camouflage to surprise their prey. They strike quickly to wrap around their target, using their powerful muscles to constrict and suffocate it. Anacondas are patient hunters, waiting for the right moment to strike, and they can stay submerged for long periods, making them deadly in water.
