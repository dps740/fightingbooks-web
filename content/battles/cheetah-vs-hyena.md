## Meet the Fighters

### The Cheetah

The cheetah, known scientifically as *Acinonyx jubatus*, roams the savannas and grasslands of Africa. Renowned for being the fastest land animal, these magnificent cats are built for speed, with slender bodies and long legs. Their reputation as expert hunters is well-deserved, as they rely on their incredible acceleration to catch prey.

- **Top Speed:** 60 to 70 mph! That's faster than a school bus on the highway!
- **Bite Force / Strength:** Not specifically provided

**Weapons:**
- Sharp claws (up to 2 inches long)
- Powerful jaws
- Speedy legs

**Fighting Style:**
Cheetahs are ambush hunters, relying on their exceptional speed and agility to chase down prey. They prefer to hunt solo, using their keen eyesight to spot potential meals from a distance. When they are ready to strike, they accelerate rapidly, often reaching speeds of 60 mph within seconds. However, they can only maintain this pace for short bursts, typically around 20-30 seconds.

### The Hyena

The spotted hyena, or *Crocuta crocuta*, is a fascinating and often misunderstood predator found in the savannas, grasslands, and woodlands of Africa. With a reputation for scavenging and hunting in packs, hyenas are social animals that work together to overpower their prey. Their laughter-like vocalizations add a unique twist to their already intriguing persona.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** 90 to 190 pounds
- **Length / Height:** Up to 4.5 feet tall at the shoulder and 5.5 to 6.5 feet long! That's about as long as a small sedan!
- **Top Speed:** Can run up to 37 miles per hour! That's faster than a speeding car in a school zone!
- **Bite Force / Strength:** 1,100 PSI!

**Weapons:**
- Powerful jaws with a bite force of 1,100 PSI!
- Sharp claws
- Strong legs for running

**Fighting Style:**
Hyenas are known for their pack behavior, often hunting in groups of up to 80 members. Their fighting style is strategic and relies on teamwork. They use their powerful jaws to crush bones and tear flesh, making them efficient scavengers and hunters. When challenged, they can be aggressive and will often intimidate larger predators by working together.
