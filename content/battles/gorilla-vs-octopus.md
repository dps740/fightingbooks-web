## Meet the Fighters

### The Gorilla

The gorilla, known scientifically as *Gorilla gorilla*, reigns as one of the largest primates on the planet. These gentle giants inhabit the tropical forests of Africa, where they are often seen foraging for fruits and leaves. While they are famous for their strength and size, gorillas are also known for their intelligence and social behaviors, living in tight-knit family groups.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Up to 400 pounds! That's like carrying a small child!
- **Length / Height:** Up to 6 feet tall and 12 feet long when standing on all fours! That's as tall as a door!
- **Top Speed:** Can run up to 20 miles per hour! That's as fast as a speeding car in a school zone!

**Weapons:**
- Strong arms with a reach of 8 feet
- Powerful jaws
- Intimidating chest thump

**Fighting Style:**
Gorillas are generally peaceful creatures, but when threatened, they can exhibit aggressive behavior. They rely on their size and strength, using powerful punches and chest thumps to intimidate foes. In a fight, a gorilla may charge directly at its opponent, utilizing its long reach to deliver devastating blows.

### The Octopus

The common octopus, or *Octopus vulgaris*, is a remarkable creature found in oceans and seas around the world. This intelligent mollusk is famous for its ability to camouflage itself and its incredible problem-solving skills. With a soft body and eight flexible arms, the octopus is a master of stealth and adaptation in its aquatic environment.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Up to 22 pounds! That's like a medium-sized dog!
- **Length / Height:** Up to 3.5 feet long! That's about the length of a school bus!
- **Top Speed:** Can jet through water at 25 mph! That's faster than most people can swim — by A LOT!

**Weapons:**
- Beak (1 inch)
- Ink cloud
- Camouflage

**Fighting Style:**
The octopus engages in a unique fighting style that emphasizes agility and cunning. It can quickly escape using its ink cloud, confusing enemies while it darts away. If pressed into battle, it can wrap its arms around opponents and use its beak to deliver sharp bites. Its ability to camouflage also allows it to surprise adversaries, striking when they least expect it.
