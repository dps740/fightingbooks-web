## Meet the Fighters

### The Hippo

The Common Hippopotamus, or hippo for short, is known as one of the largest land mammals on Earth. These magnificent creatures inhabit rivers and lakes across Africa, spending most of their time submerged to keep cool. Despite their seemingly lazy demeanor, hippos are incredibly territorial and can be very aggressive. Their size and strength make them a formidable presence in their watery home.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Up to 4,000 pounds
- **Length / Height:** Up to 16.5 feet long
- **Top Speed:** Can run up to 19 mph
- **Bite Force / Strength:** (approx.) 1,800 psi

**Weapons:**
- Massive jaws with 20-inch teeth
- Thick skin
- Powerful legs

**Fighting Style:**
Hippos primarily fight to defend their territory and young. They use their immense size and powerful jaws to intimidate and attack rivals. Their fighting style is direct and aggressive, often charging at threats with surprising speed, despite their bulk. Hippos can hold their breath underwater for up to five minutes, allowing them to ambush potential threats from below the surface.

### The Cheetah

The Cheetah, scientifically known as Acinonyx jubatus, is the fastest land animal, capable of reaching incredible speeds. These sleek felines are found in the savannas and grasslands of Africa, where they rely on their speed and agility to catch prey. With their distinctive black tear marks under their eyes and characteristic slender bodies, cheetahs are built for one thing: speed!

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** 110 to 140 pounds
- **Length / Height:** Up to 4.5 feet tall at the shoulder and 3.5 to 4.5 feet long
- **Top Speed:** 60 to 70 mph
- **Bite Force / Strength:** (approx.) 475 psi

**Weapons:**
- Sharp claws (up to 2 inches long)
- Powerful jaws
- Speedy legs

**Fighting Style:**
Cheetahs are skilled hunters that primarily rely on their exceptional speed to chase down prey. They use a combination of stealth and explosive bursts of speed to catch small to medium-sized animals like gazelles. In a fight, a cheetah will likely attempt to evade and outmaneuver its opponent, striking quickly when the opportunity arises.
