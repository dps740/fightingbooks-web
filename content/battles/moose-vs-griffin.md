## Meet the Fighters

### The Moose

The moose, scientifically known as *Alces alces*, is the largest member of the deer family and can be found roaming the forests and wetlands of North America, Europe, and Asia. With its striking antlers and long legs, this herbivore is a true gentle giant, but don’t let its calm demeanor fool you—when threatened, a moose can be surprisingly fierce!

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** 1,200 pounds
- **Length / Height:** 6 to 7.5 feet tall at the shoulder
- **Top Speed:** Can run up to 35 mph

**Weapons:**
- Antlers up to 6 feet wide!
- Strong legs
- Kicking power

**Fighting Style:**
Moose tend to fight with brute force rather than speed. When challenged, they will stand their ground and use their massive size and antlers to intimidate or physically confront their opponent. They can kick fiercely with their strong legs, making them formidable in a one-on-one battle.

### The Griffin

The griffin, a legendary creature from myth and folklore, boasts the majestic body of a lion and the head and wings of an eagle. Often depicted as a guardian of treasures, this creature is said to dwell in mountainous regions and ancient ruins. Its reputation for strength and courage makes it a fearsome opponent in any battle.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** 1,000 pounds (approx.)
- **Length / Height:** About 13 feet long and 6 feet tall at the shoulder
- **Top Speed:** Can fly up to 30 mph

**Weapons:**
- Sharp claws (up to 6 inches long)
- Powerful beak
- Talons for gripping

**Fighting Style:**
Griffins are agile fighters, using their ability to fly to their advantage. They can swoop down from above, using their sharp claws and powerful beak to attack. Their tactical flying allows them to evade attacks while striking with precision, making them a challenging opponent for ground-based animals.
