## Meet the Fighters

### The Gorilla

Gorillas are the largest primates on Earth and are known for their incredible strength and gentle nature. Found in the tropical forests of Africa, these majestic creatures live in family groups and are highly intelligent. Their reputation as peaceful herbivores is well-earned, but don’t let that fool you; a gorilla can be a fierce protector when threatened!

- **Weight:** Up to 400 pounds!
- **Length / Height:** Up to 6 feet tall and 12 feet long when standing on all fours! That's as tall as a door!
- **Top Speed:** Can run up to 20 miles per hour! That's as fast as a speeding car in a school zone!

**Weapons:**
- Strong arms with a reach of 8 feet
- Powerful jaws
- Intimidating chest thump

**Fighting Style:**
Gorillas are typically non-aggressive, using their strength to intimidate rather than attack outright. When they do fight, they rely on their powerful arms for swinging strikes and their weight to overpower an opponent. They are known to fight solo, using brute strength and agility to defend themselves or their family.

### The Alligator

The American alligator is a reptilian powerhouse that rules the swamps, rivers, and lakes of the southeastern United States. Known for their fearsome look and impressive hunting skills, alligators are stealthy predators that can be surprisingly fast. With a diet consisting of fish, birds, and even deer, they strike fear into the hearts of many animals in their environment.

**Weapons:**

**Fighting Style:**
Alligators are ambush predators, often lying in wait for their prey before launching a surprise attack. They use their powerful jaws to grip and drag their prey underwater. When engaged in a fight, they can use their teeth and tail effectively, showcasing a combination of strength and cunning.
