## Meet the Fighters

### The T-Rex

The T-Rex, or Tyrannosaurus rex, roamed the Earth during the late Cretaceous period around 68 to 66 million years ago. This massive predator was the top of the food chain, dominating its habitat in what is now North America. With its intimidating size and fearsome reputation, the T-Rex is often depicted as the ultimate dinosaur and a symbol of prehistoric might.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** 12,000 pounds
- **Length / Height:** 40 feet long
- **Top Speed:** 20 mph

**Weapons:**
- Powerful jaws with sharp teeth
- Strong legs for ambushing
- Massive tail for balance and swatting enemies

**Fighting Style:**
The T-Rex was an ambush predator, relying on its strength and surprise to take down prey. It would often use its powerful legs to sprint short distances, closing the gap between itself and its target. When engaged in a fight, the T-Rex would use its massive jaws to deliver bone-crushing bites and its tail to knock opponents off balance.

### The Dragon

Meet the Fire-Breathing Dragon, or Draco Ignis! This legendary creature is often found soaring high above mountains or lurking in caves. Dragons are commonly portrayed as majestic yet fearsome beings capable of breathing fire and wreaking havoc on anything that threatens them. Their mythical status has made them a favorite in stories and folklore around the world.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** 1,500 pounds
- **Length / Height:** 30 feet long (about the length of a school bus!)
- **Top Speed:** 60 mph

**Weapons:**
- Fire Breath (up to 1,000°F)

- Tail Swipe (5 feet wide)

**Fighting Style:**
Dragons are known for their aerial abilities, using the skies to their advantage. They can dive down swiftly to attack with their fire breath or sharp claws. In a battle, a dragon would likely rely on its ability to fly, swooping down to unleash its fiery breath while using its claws and tail swipe to defend against ground-based attackers.
