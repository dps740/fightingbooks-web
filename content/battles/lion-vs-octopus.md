## Meet the Fighters

### The Lion

Lions, known scientifically as *Panthera leo*, are majestic creatures that roam the grasslands, savannas, and open woodlands of Africa and India. With their golden manes and reputation as fearsome hunters, they are often called the "king of the jungle," even though they primarily inhabit savannas.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Up to 420 pounds
- **Length / Height:** About 8 to 10 feet long from nose to tail
- **Top Speed:** Up to 50 miles per hour for short bursts

**Weapons:**
- Powerful jaws with a bite force of 650 PSI
- Sharp claws up to 1.5 inches long
- Strong legs for pouncing

**Fighting Style:**
Lions are social animals that often fight in groups called prides. Their hunting tactics usually involve teamwork, where they ambush prey or chase it down with their impressive speed. When it comes to fighting, they rely on their powerful jaws and claws to overpower their opponent.

### The Octopus

The common octopus, or *Octopus vulgaris*, is an amazing marine animal found in oceans and seas around the world. Known for its intelligence and adaptability, this creature can change colors and textures to blend into its surroundings, making it a master of camouflage.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Up to 33 pounds (approx.)
- **Length / Height:** Up to 3.5 feet long
- **Top Speed:** Can jet through water at 25 mph

**Weapons:**
- Beak (1 inch)
- Ink cloud
- Camouflage

**Fighting Style:**
Octopuses are solitary hunters and use their intelligence to outsmart prey. They can squirt an ink cloud to confuse and escape from predators, while their ability to camouflage allows them to hide effectively. When they attack, they use their beak to pierce shells and capture prey.
