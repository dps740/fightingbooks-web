## Meet the Fighters

### The Great Horned Owl

The Great Horned Owl, scientifically known as *Bubo virginianus*, is one of the most adaptable and widespread owls in the Americas. Found in forests, deserts, swamps, and even bustling cities, this nocturnal hunter is renowned for its distinctive tufts resembling horns. With a fierce reputation, this owl often hunts anything from small mammals to other birds, including its own kind!

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Up to 5.5 pounds
- **Length / Height:** 2 feet long; wingspan up to 5 feet
- **Top Speed:** Can dive at 40 mph and fly at 40 mph

**Fighting Style:**
The Great Horned Owl is a solitary hunter that relies on stealth and surprise. Using its incredible night vision and silent flight, it ambushes unsuspecting prey, striking with lethal precision. When threatened, it may show aggressive displays or even attack larger animals, using its strength and talons to defend itself.

### The Walrus

The Walrus, known scientifically as *Odobenus rosmarus*, is a fascinating marine mammal found in the icy waters of the Arctic and sub-Arctic regions. Recognizable by its long tusks and whiskered face, the walrus is a social creature that often congregates in large groups. Although it primarily feeds on clams and other mollusks, it has a strong presence in its habitat and is not to be underestimated!

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Up to 2,000 pounds
- **Length / Height:** Up to 12 feet long
- **Top Speed:** Can swim up to 35 mph

**Weapons:**
- Tusks that can measure up to 3 feet long!
- Strong flippers that aid in swimming and mobility
- Massive body weight that provides significant physical advantage

**Fighting Style:**
Walruses typically rely on their size and strength during confrontations. They can use their tusks for defense or to establish dominance within their social groups. When threatened, a walrus may dive deep to escape or use its bulk to intimidate potential predators. They rarely engage in direct combat unless necessary.
