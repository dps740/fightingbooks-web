## Meet the Fighters

### The Hippo

The Common Hippopotamus, or hippo for short, is a semi-aquatic mammal found in rivers and lakes throughout Africa. Known for their massive size and aggressive nature, hippos are often underestimated due to their seemingly slow movements. But don’t be fooled! These enormous beasts can be surprisingly swift on land and in water.

### The Wolf

The Gray Wolf, or simply wolf, is a social carnivore that thrives in forests, tundras, and grasslands. Famous for their pack mentality, wolves are skilled hunters that work together to take down prey much larger than themselves. With their keen senses and agility, wolves are known for their cunning tactics in both hunting and defending their territory.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** 50-110 pounds
- **Length / Height:** Up to 6.5 feet long (including tail)
- **Top Speed:** Can run up to 40 mph
- **Bite Force / Strength:** Up to 1,500 PSI

**Weapons:**
- Powerful jaws (up to 1,500 PSI)

**Fighting Style:**
Wolves are strategic fighters that often rely on their pack for support. They use their speed and agility to outmaneuver larger opponents, striking swiftly and retreating before the enemy can retaliate. Wolves are also skilled at using their environment for camouflage, allowing them to launch surprise attacks against larger foes.
