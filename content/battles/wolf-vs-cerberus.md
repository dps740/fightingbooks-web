## Meet the Fighters

### The Wolf

The Gray Wolf, or Canis lupus, is a highly intelligent and social predator found in forests, tundras, and grasslands across the world. Known for their strong pack dynamics and vocal howling, these wolves are skilled hunters that work together to bring down prey. Their reputation as fierce yet family-oriented animals makes them both respected and feared in the wild.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Up to 110 lbs
- **Length / Height:** Up to 6.5 feet long (including tail) - ABOUT AS LONG AS A SCHOOL BUS!
- **Top Speed:** Can run up to 40 mph - FASTER THAN A SPRINTING HUMAN!

**Weapons:**
- Powerful jaws (up to 1,500 PSI)
- Sharp claws
- Keen sense of smell

**Fighting Style:**
Wolves are strategic fighters that often rely on their pack to corner and ambush prey. They use teamwork to outmaneuver opponents, leveraging their speed and agility. A lone wolf may attack directly, but in a pack, they create a coordinated assault that overwhelms their target.

### The Cerberus

Cerberus is a mythological creature that serves as the fearsome guardian of the gates of Hades, ensuring that no one escapes the Underworld. This three-headed beast, often depicted with a serpent for a tail, is a towering presence with a reputation for being intimidating and relentless. Its role in Greek mythology makes Cerberus a powerful symbol of the afterlife and the dangers that lurk beyond.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** (approx.) 500 lbs
- **Length / Height:** About 30 feet long! That's almost as long as a school bus!
- **Top Speed:** Can run up to 25 miles per hour! That's faster than the fastest human sprinter!

**Weapons:**
- Three fierce heads, each about 3 feet wide
- Sharp claws
- Poisonous breath

**Fighting Style:**
Cerberus fights with brute strength and intimidation. Each of its three heads can bite at the same time, making it a formidable opponent. Its thick fur and fearsome growl add to its defenses, while its ability to scare off intruders makes it a truly terrifying foe.
