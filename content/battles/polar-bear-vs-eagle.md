## Meet the Fighters

### The Polar Bear

The polar bear, known scientifically as *Ursus maritimus*, is the largest land carnivore and is primarily found in the frigid Arctic regions. These powerful animals are well-adapted to life on the sea ice, where they hunt for seals, their main source of food. Fierce and formidable, polar bears are often seen as symbols of strength in the wild.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Up to 1,500 pounds
- **Length / Height:** Up to 10 feet long
- **Top Speed:** Can run up to 25 miles per hour
- **Bite Force / Strength:** 1,200 PSI

**Weapons:**
- Powerful jaws with a bite force of 1,200 PSI
- Sharp claws up to 2 inches long
- Strong legs for powerful swimming

**Fighting Style:**
Polar bears are solitary hunters, using their massive size and strength to overpower their prey. They rely on a combination of stealth and brute force, often ambushing seals by breaking through the ice or waiting patiently by breathing holes. When it comes to fighting, polar bears can use their powerful claws and strong jaws to deliver devastating blows.

### The Eagle

The bald eagle, or *Haliaeetus leucocephalus*, is a majestic bird of prey known for its impressive wingspan and sharp hunting skills. These birds typically inhabit areas near large bodies of water, where they can find abundant food supplies like fish. With their keen eyesight and incredible flying abilities, eagles are often seen as symbols of freedom and strength.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Up to 14 pounds
- **Length / Height:** About 3.5 feet long with a wingspan of up to 7.5 feet
- **Top Speed:** Can dive at speeds of 30-40 miles per hour
- **Bite Force / Strength:** Not provided (but strong for their size)

**Weapons:**
- Sharp talons (up to 2.5 inches long)
- Strong beak
- Keen eyesight

**Fighting Style:**
Eagles are agile fighters, relying on their speed and aerial prowess to outmaneuver opponents. They often swoop down from high altitudes to strike their prey with precision, using their sharp talons to capture and kill. In a confrontation, an eagle would likely use its agility and sharp eyesight to avoid attacks while trying to land a powerful strike.
