## Meet the Fighters

### The Tiger

The tiger, known scientifically as *Panthera tigris*, is a magnificent carnivore that roams the tropical forests, grasslands, and swamps of Asia. Revered as the largest cat species, tigers are solitary hunters with a reputation for being both stealthy and powerful.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** 220 to 660 pounds
- **Length / Height:** Up to 10 feet long including the tail! That's as long as a SCHOOL BUS!
- **Top Speed:** Can run up to 30 miles per hour! That's as fast as a speeding CAR!

**Weapons:**
- Powerful jaws with a bite force of 1,000 PSI
- Sharp claws up to 3 inches long
- Strong legs for pouncing

**Fighting Style:**
Tigers are solitary hunters who rely on stealth and ambush tactics to catch their prey. They use their powerful legs to pounce swiftly and silently, often stalking their target before launching an attack. In a fight, a tiger would likely employ its size and strength, using surprise to its advantage.

### The Mandrill

The mandrill, scientifically known as *Mandrillus sphinx*, is the largest monkey in the world and an expert at navigating the dense tropical rainforests of Central Africa, including regions in Cameroon, Gabon, and the Congo. With its vibrant colors and social nature, the mandrill is both fascinating and fierce!

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** 77 pounds
- **Length / Height:** Males are up to 3 feet tall
- **Top Speed:** Can run 25 mph through dense jungle

**Weapons:**
- Canine fangs up to 2.5 INCHES long — longer than a lion's relative to body size!
- Incredibly powerful arms that can tear bark off trees with bare hands
- Males flash their enormous fangs and vivid colors as a terrifying threat display!

**Fighting Style:**
Mandrills are known for their social behavior, often living in large troops of up to 800 individuals. However, when it comes to defending themselves, they can be quite aggressive. In a fight, mandrills would use their agility and speed to dodge attacks, while their sharp fangs would serve as an effective weapon against larger foes.
