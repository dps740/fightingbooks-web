## Meet the Fighters

### The Gorilla

Gorillas are the largest primates on Earth, native to the tropical forests of Africa. These gentle giants are known for their immense strength and intelligence, living in family groups led by a dominant male. Despite their intimidating size, gorillas are primarily herbivores, munching on a variety of plants, leaves, and fruits.

- **Length / Height:** Up to 6 feet tall and 12 feet long when standing on all fours! That's as tall as a door!

**Weapons:**

**Fighting Style:**
Gorillas are known for their sheer power and size, utilizing their impressive arms to swing and strike. When threatened, they often display aggressive behavior, including chest thumping and roaring, to intimidate opponents. While they may not be aggressive hunters, they are formidable fighters when defending their territory or family.

### The Wolf

Gray wolves are social carnivores that roam forests, tundras, and grasslands across North America and Eurasia. Known for their pack mentality, wolves hunt together to take down larger prey. Their reputation as cunning hunters makes them both feared and respected in the animal kingdom.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** 50-110 pounds
- **Length / Height:** Up to 6.5 feet long (including tail) - ABOUT AS LONG AS A SCHOOL BUS!
- **Top Speed:** Can run up to 40 mph - FASTER THAN A SPRINTING HUMAN!
- **Bite Force / Strength:** Up to 1,500 PSI

**Weapons:**
- Powerful jaws (up to 1,500 PSI)
- Sharp claws
- Keen sense of smell

**Fighting Style:**
Wolves rely on their agility and speed in battles. They often attack in packs, using coordinated strategies to outwit and overpower their prey. When on their own, wolves can be stealthy hunters, using their keen senses to ambush from a distance or chase down their target.
