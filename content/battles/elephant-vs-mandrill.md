## Meet the Fighters

### The Elephant

The African elephant, known scientifically as *Loxodonta africana*, is the largest land mammal on Earth. These gentle giants roam the savannas, forests, and grasslands of Africa, where they play a crucial role in their ecosystem. Elephants are known for their intelligence, social structures, and remarkable memory, making them one of the most revered animals in the wild.

**Fighting Style:**
Elephants typically use their size and strength to intimidate opponents. In the wild, they might charge at threats, using their tusks to push or stab. While they usually fight alone, they can also work together in herds to protect one another.

### The Mandrill

The mandrill, scientifically known as *Mandrillus sphinx*, is an extraordinary monkey that calls the dense tropical rainforests of Central Africa its home. With their vibrant faces and striking colors, mandrills are not just visually impressive; they are also highly social animals that live in large troops, sometimes exceeding 800 individuals. This social structure helps them fend off predators and establish dominance.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Up to 77 pounds

**Fighting Style:**
Mandrills rely on their speed and agility to evade predators and engage in fights. When threatened, they often display their impressive fangs and bright colors to intimidate rivals. In confrontations, they can use their powerful arms to strike and grapple, showcasing their strength and dexterity. Their ability to climb allows them to escape or reposition quickly during skirmishes.
