## Meet the Fighters

### The Moose

The moose (scientific name: *Alces alces*) is an impressive creature found in the forests and wetlands of North America, Europe, and parts of Asia. Known for their towering height and massive antlers, moose are the largest members of the deer family. These gentle giants are herbivores, munching on leaves, twigs, and even aquatic plants. With their massive bodies and unique adaptations, moose are truly a sight to behold!

**Fighting Style:**

When faced with danger, they may charge head-on, relying on their size to scare off potential threats.

### The T-Rex

The Tyrannosaurus rex, one of the most famous dinosaurs, roamed the Earth around 68 to 66 million years ago. This colossal predator was a top carnivore in its ecosystem, known for its massive head, powerful jaws, and keen hunting skills. T-Rex inhabited what is now North America and is renowned for its size, with a reputation that has made it a staple in popular culture. Its bone-crushing bite force and fearsome presence made it a fearsome hunter during the Late Cretaceous period.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** 9 tons (18,000 pounds)
- **Length / Height:** 40 feet long
- **Top Speed:** Up to 20 mph
- **Bite Force / Strength:** 12,800 psi

**Weapons:**
- Massive, powerful jaws
- Sharp teeth up to 12 inches long
- Strong legs and tail for balance

**Fighting Style:**
The T-Rex was a fierce predator that relied on its strength and speed to take down prey. With its powerful jaws and razor-sharp teeth, T-Rex could crush bones and tear flesh with ease. It often used ambush tactics, sneaking up on prey before launching a surprise attack. When engaged in battle, T-Rex displayed relentless aggression, using its powerful legs to chase down its target and its enormous bite to deliver a fatal blow.
