## Meet the Fighters

### The Gorilla

The gorilla, scientifically named *Gorilla gorilla*, is the largest primate on Earth. Found in the tropical forests of Africa, these gentle giants are known for their incredible strength and intelligent social behavior. Gorillas live in groups, called troops, led by a dominant male known as a silverback. Their reputation as peaceful herbivores often disguises their impressive fighting abilities when threatened.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Up to 400 pounds
- **Length / Height:** Up to 6 feet tall and 12 feet long when standing on all fours! That's as tall as a door!
- **Top Speed:** Can run up to 20 miles per hour! That's as fast as a speeding car in a school zone!

**Weapons:**
- Strong arms with a reach of 8 feet
- Powerful jaws
- Intimidating chest thump

**Fighting Style:**
Gorillas primarily rely on their sheer size and strength to defend themselves. In a fight, they may use their powerful arms to deliver devastating blows while intimidating opponents with loud chest thumps. They prefer to fight solo unless protecting their troop, where teamwork becomes crucial.

### The Jaguar

The jaguar, or *Panthera onca*, reigns as the third largest big cat in the world. Native to the rainforests, swamps, and grasslands of Central and South America, jaguars are renowned for their stealth and strength. These solitary hunters have a reputation for being fierce and powerful, often taking down prey much larger than themselves.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Up to 250 pounds
- **Length / Height:** Up to 6 feet long! That's as long as a school bus is tall!
- **Top Speed:** Can run up to 50 miles per hour! That's faster than a speeding car in a school zone!

**Weapons:**
- Powerful jaws with a bite force of 1,500 PSI
- Sharp claws
- Stealthy approach

**Fighting Style:**
Jaguars are ambush predators, often relying on their camouflage and stealth to surprise their prey. When they strike, they deliver a powerful bite that can crush bones, allowing them to take down larger animals. They are quick and agile, making them formidable opponents in a fight.
