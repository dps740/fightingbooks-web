## Meet the Fighters

### The Jaguar

The jaguar (*Panthera onca*) is a magnificent big cat found in the rainforests, swamps, and grasslands of Central and South America. Famous for its beautiful rosettes and impressive hunting skills, the jaguar is a solitary creature that often ambushes its prey from the shadows. With its powerful physique and stealthy approach, this feline is a master of its environment.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Up to 250 pounds
- **Length / Height:** Up to 6 feet long! That's as long as a school bus is tall!
- **Top Speed:** Can run up to 50 miles per hour! That's faster than a speeding car in a school zone!
- **Bite Force / Strength:** 1,500 PSI

**Weapons:**
- Powerful jaws with a bite force of 1,500 PSI
- Sharp claws
- Stealthy approach

**Fighting Style:**
Jaguars are known for their ambush tactics. They use their incredible stealth to sneak up on unsuspecting prey, often striking from above or behind. With their powerful jaws, they can crush the skulls of their victims, making them deadly hunters in their own right.

### The T-Rex

The Tyrannosaurus rex, often known simply as T-Rex, is one of the most famous dinosaurs that roamed the Earth during the late Cretaceous period. This massive predator was an apex hunter, dominating its environment with its sheer size and strength. Fossil evidence suggests that T-Rex could reach lengths of over 40 feet, making it an intimidating presence.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Up to 9 tons (18,000 pounds)
- **Length / Height:** Up to 40 feet long!
- **Top Speed:** Estimated at 20 miles per hour (approx.)
- **Bite Force / Strength:** 8,000 PSI

**Weapons:**
- Massive, serrated teeth designed for crushing bone
- Powerful hind limbs for running and attacking
- Strong tail for balance and possibly defense

**Fighting Style:**
T-Rex was a powerful predator that primarily relied on its massive size and enormous bite force to overpower its prey. With its strong legs, it could charge at high speeds, delivering devastating bites to any creature unfortunate enough to cross its path. T-Rex often hunted alone, using its keen sense of smell to track down food.
