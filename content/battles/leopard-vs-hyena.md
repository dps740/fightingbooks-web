## Meet the Fighters

### The Leopard

The leopard, scientifically known as *Panthera pardus*, is a stunning feline that roams the savannas, forests, and grasslands of Africa. With its beautiful spotted coat and incredible climbing ability, it is known as one of the most adaptable big cats. Leopards have a reputation for being solitary hunters, relying on their stealth to take down prey.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** 66 to 176 pounds
- **Length / Height:** Up to 6.25 feet long
- **Top Speed:** Can run up to 36 mph
- **Bite Force / Strength:** (Not provided)

**Weapons:**
- Powerful jaws (up to 2 inches long)
- Sharp claws
- Agile body

**Fighting Style:**
Leopards are ambush predators, often stalking their prey before launching a surprise attack. They rely on their speed and agility to outmaneuver opponents, striking quickly and then using their powerful claws and jaws to immobilize their target. While they typically hunt alone, they are adept at defending their territory and can be fierce in combat.

### The Hyena

The spotted hyena, or *Crocuta crocuta*, is a social carnivore found in savannas, grasslands, and woodlands across Africa. Known for their distinctive laugh and remarkable hunting skills, hyenas are often misunderstood as scavengers, but they are skilled hunters in their own right, often taking down large prey in packs.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** 90 to 190 pounds (average weight of around 130 pounds)
- **Length / Height:** Up to 5.5 to 6.5 feet long
- **Top Speed:** Can run up to 37 mph
- **Bite Force / Strength:** 1,100 PSI

**Weapons:**
- Powerful jaws with a bite force of 1,100 PSI
- Sharp claws
- Strong legs for running

**Fighting Style:**
Hyenas are pack hunters and often work together to bring down prey. They use their intelligence and teamwork in fights, surrounding and outmaneuvering their opponents. While they can fight individually, their strength lies in their numbers, and they are known for their ability to intimidate rivals with their loud vocalizations.
