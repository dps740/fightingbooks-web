## Meet the Fighters

### The Triceratops

The Triceratops, scientifically known as *Triceratops horridus*, roamed the open plains and forests of North America around 68 to 66 million years ago. Known for its impressive size and distinctive frilled head adorned with three formidable horns, this herbivorous dinosaur was a heavyweight in the ancient world.

- **Length / Height:** 30 feet long
- **Top Speed:** 20 mph

**Weapons:**

- Beak for biting

**Fighting Style:**
Triceratops was a powerful beast that relied on its massive size and strength to defend itself from predators. It often fought solo, using its horns and sturdy body to charge at attackers. Its defensive strategy included positioning itself with its frill facing threats, ready to strike with its horns if necessary.

### The Pteranodon

The Pteranodon, characterized by its awe-inspiring wingspan of up to 33 feet, was a formidable flying reptile that soared above coastal areas and open skies during the Late Cretaceous period. Though not a dinosaur, this creature was an expert fisherman, diving into the water to catch fish and squid with its long beak.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** 1,000 pounds
- **Length / Height:** 20 feet long
- **Top Speed:** 25 mph

**Weapons:**
- Beak (up to 3 feet long)
- Wings
- Claws

**Fighting Style:**
Pteranodon used its speed and agility to fight rather than brute strength. It could swoop down from the sky to catch prey or evade predators. If threatened, it would often take to the air, using its camouflage against the sky to escape danger or attack from above.
