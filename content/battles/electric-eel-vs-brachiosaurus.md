This slippery predator is infamous for its ability to generate electric shocks, using this power to stun prey and defend itself from threats.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** 44 pounds
- **Length / Height:** Up to 8 feet long
- **Top Speed:** 3 mph 

**Weapons:**

- Three separate electric organs make up 80% of its body, turning it into a living battery!
- Can pulse electricity to make hidden prey twitch, revealing their hiding spots!

**Fighting Style:**
The Electric Eel primarily relies on stealth and surprise attacks. It can ambush its prey by using its electrifying capabilities to incapacitate them before swallowing them whole. While it may not be fast, its ability to deliver a powerful shock gives it a unique advantage in underwater combat.

### The Brachiosaurus

The Brachiosaurus, scientifically known as Brachiosaurus altithorax, was a giant that roamed the forests and floodplains during the Late Jurassic period. Standing tall with its long neck and massive body, this herbivore was famous for its ability to reach high into trees to munch on leaves and branches. With a size that rivals that of a school bus, this gentle giant was not just big but also incredibly impressive.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** 62 tons
- **Length / Height:** 85 feet long and 40 feet tall
- **Top Speed:** 10 mph 

**Weapons:**
- Long neck (up to 30 feet) for reaching high foliage
- Strong legs capable of supporting its massive weight
- Massive body for defense against predators

**Fighting Style:**
While generally a peaceful creature, the Brachiosaurus can defend itself if threatened. Its sheer size makes it a formidable opponent, and it can use its long neck to reach high and knock down threats. It’s not a fighter by nature, but if cornered, it can use its bulk to intimidate or push away attackers.
