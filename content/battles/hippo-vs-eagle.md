## Meet the Fighters

### The Hippo

The Common Hippopotamus, known scientifically as *Hippopotamus amphibius*, is a semi-aquatic mammal that primarily inhabits rivers and lakes across Africa. These enormous creatures can be found in groups, known as pods, and are infamous for their territorial nature. Despite their bulky appearance, hippos are incredibly agile, both in water and on land.

- **Length / Height:** Up to 16.5 feet long

Their thick skin serves as natural armor, making them difficult to injure. Hippos often rely on brute strength and intimidation during confrontations.

### The Eagle

The Bald Eagle, or *Haliaeetus leucocephalus*, is a bird of prey that thrives near large bodies of open water, where it can find abundant food supplies and tall trees for nesting. As the national bird of the United States, it is celebrated for its impressive wingspan and striking white head. Bald Eagles are known for their sharp eyesight and unmatched hunting skills.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Approximately 15 lbs
- **Length / Height:** About 3.5 feet long

- Strong beak
- Keen eyesight

**Fighting Style:**
Eagles are aerial hunters that use their incredible vision to spot prey from high altitudes. They dive at exceptional speeds, using their sharp talons to snatch up smaller animals or fish. In a confrontation, Bald Eagles would likely rely on agility, speed, and their ability to evade attacks from larger creatures like hippos.
