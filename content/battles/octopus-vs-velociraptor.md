## Meet the Fighters

### The Octopus

The common octopus (Octopus vulgaris) is a fascinating creature found in oceans and seas around the world. Known for its intelligence and ability to escape from predators and captivity alike, the octopus is a master of disguise and defense. With its soft body and impressive capabilities, this cephalopod has a reputation for being a clever survivor.

- **Bite Force / Strength:** (approx.) 50 psi

**Weapons:**

**Fighting Style:**

When threatened, it can release an ink cloud to escape, while its beak allows it to crack open shells of prey. Octopuses are solitary hunters, relying on stealth and surprise rather than brute force.

### The Velociraptor

The velociraptor (Velociraptor mongoliensis) roamed the dry, open areas like deserts and plains during the Late Cretaceous period. This small but fierce dinosaur is often depicted as a clever and agile predator, known for its ability to hunt in packs. With its sharp claws and keen senses, the velociraptor was a formidable opponent in its time.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** (approx.) 15-33 lbs
- **Length / Height:** About 6.5 feet long! That's like a small car! (A sedan is ~15 feet)!
- **Top Speed:** Top speed of 24 mph! That's faster than a human sprinter!
- **Bite Force / Strength:** (approx.) 70 psi

**Weapons:**

- Sharp teeth
- Speed and agility

**Fighting Style:**
The velociraptor is a skilled hunter that relies on its speed and agility to outmaneuver prey. It often hunted in packs, using teamwork to overwhelm larger animals. With quick reflexes and a powerful sickle-shaped claw, the velociraptor strikes with precision, making it a dangerous opponent in a fight.
