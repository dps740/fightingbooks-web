## Meet the Fighters

### The Leopard

The leopard (*Panthera pardus*) is one of nature's most fascinating hunters. Found in savannas, forests, and grasslands, this elusive feline is known for its striking spotted coat and incredible climbing abilities. Leopards are skilled and stealthy predators, often seen lurking in trees or dense foliage, waiting for the perfect moment to pounce on their unsuspecting prey.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Up to 176 pounds
- **Length / Height:** Up to 6.25 feet long
- **Top Speed:** Can run up to 36 mph

**Weapons:**
- Powerful jaws (up to 2 inches long)
- Sharp claws
- Agile body

**Fighting Style:**
Leopards typically employ a stealthy approach, using their camouflage to stalk prey before launching surprise attacks. They prefer to hunt alone and rely on their speed and agility to overpower smaller animals. When faced with larger foes, they may use their climbing skills to escape or to launch an ambush from above.

### The Triceratops

The triceratops (*Triceratops horridus*) roamed the open plains and forests of North America during the late Cretaceous period, around 68 million years ago. With its three impressive horns and large bony frill, the triceratops was an unmistakable sight. This herbivore primarily feasted on tough plants and shrubs, using its beak-like mouth to munch on foliage.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** About 12,000 pounds
- **Length / Height:** About 30 feet long
- **Top Speed:** 20 mph

**Weapons:**
- Horns up to 3 feet long
- Beak for biting
- Heavy body for charging

**Fighting Style:**
When threatened, triceratops could charge at predators with remarkable speed for their size. Their three powerful horns and large frill provided both offensive and defensive capabilities. Triceratops often stood their ground, using their size and strength to intimidate or fend off attackers.
