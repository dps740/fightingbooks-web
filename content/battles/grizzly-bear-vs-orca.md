## Meet the Fighters

### The Grizzly Bear

The Grizzly Bear, scientifically known as *Ursus arctos horribilis*, roams the forests, mountains, and national parks of North America. This massive creature is known for its strength and fierce reputation, capturing the imagination of wildlife enthusiasts everywhere!

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Up to 1,500 pounds
- **Length / Height:** Up to 8 feet long
- **Top Speed:** Can run up to 35 mph

**Weapons:**
- Powerful claws (up to 4 inches long)
- Strong jaws
- Massive body weight

**Fighting Style:**
Grizzly Bears are solitary fighters known for their brute strength and powerful strikes. When threatened, they often charge at their opponents, using their impressive speed and massive size to intimidate. Their primary tactic is to deliver devastating blows with their powerful paws while utilizing their sharp claws for slashing.

### The Orca

The Orca, or *Orcinus orca*, is a magnificent marine mammal found in oceans around the world, particularly in colder waters. Known as the "killer whale," this top predator commands respect among its aquatic peers, employing intelligence and teamwork to hunt.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Up to 12,000 pounds
- **Length / Height:** Up to 32 feet long
- **Top Speed:** Can swim up to 34.5 mph

**Weapons:**
- Powerful jaws with 40-56 teeth, each up to 4 inches long
- Speedy swimming
- Echolocation to find prey

**Fighting Style:**
Orcas are highly intelligent hunters that often use their strength and speed in coordinated attacks. They work in pods, strategizing to outsmart and trap their prey, whether it’s fish or even larger marine mammals like seals. Their echolocation allows them to pinpoint their targets with remarkable accuracy, making them efficient predators.
