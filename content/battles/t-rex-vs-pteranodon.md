## Meet the Fighters

### The T-Rex

The Tyrannosaurus Rex, often simply called T-Rex, is one of the most iconic dinosaurs known to science. This fearsome predator roamed the lush landscapes of North America during the Late Cretaceous period, around 68 to 66 million years ago. With its powerful jaws and massive size, the T-Rex was a top predator, feared by many creatures of its time.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** 9 tons
- **Length / Height:** 40 feet long
- **Top Speed:** 20 mph
- **Bite Force / Strength:** 12,800 pounds

**Weapons:**
- Massive jaws filled with serrated teeth
- Strong legs for powerful attacks
- Sturdy tail for balance and defense

**Fighting Style:**
The T-Rex is a solitary hunter, often using its size and strength to intimidate or overpower its prey. It relies on ambush tactics, staying low and hidden until the moment it strikes. With its strong bite force, the T-Rex can crush bones and tear flesh, making it a formidable opponent.

### The Pteranodon

The Pteranodon is a remarkable flying reptile that ruled the skies during the Late Cretaceous period, around 70 million years ago. With its impressive wingspan, reaching up to 33 feet, it soared over coastal areas and open waters, hunting for fish and squid. Although it’s not a dinosaur, the Pteranodon is one of the most fascinating creatures of the prehistoric world.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** 44 pounds
- **Length / Height:** Wingspan of up to 33 feet
- **Top Speed:** 25 mph
- **Bite Force / Strength:** (approx.) 250 pounds

**Weapons:**
- Beak (up to 3 feet long)
- Wings
- Claws

**Fighting Style:**
The Pteranodon uses its speed and agility to evade predators, often engaging in aerial acrobatics to escape danger. While it primarily hunts fish, it has sharp claws and a long beak that could inflict damage if necessary. It prefers to fly high, using its keen eyesight to spot threats from above.
