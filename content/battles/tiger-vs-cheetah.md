## Meet the Fighters

### The Tiger

The tiger, scientifically known as *Panthera tigris*, is a magnificent predator that roams the tropical forests, grasslands, and swamps of Asia. With its striking orange coat and bold black stripes, this apex predator is not just a symbol of strength, but also a master of stealth, often lurking silently in the shadows as it stalks its prey.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** 220 to 660 pounds
- **Length / Height:** Up to 10 feet long including the tail! That's as long as a SCHOOL BUS!
- **Top Speed:** Can run up to 30 miles per hour! That's as fast as a speeding CAR!
- **Bite Force / Strength:** 1,000 PSI

**Weapons:**
- Powerful jaws with a bite force of 1,000 PSI
- Sharp claws up to 3 inches long
- Strong legs for pouncing

**Fighting Style:**
Tigers are solitary hunters, using their incredible camouflage to ambush unsuspecting prey. They rely on their strength and powerful muscles to deliver crushing blows, often pouncing from a distance to tackle their targets. Their fighting tactics involve stealth and surprise, making them formidable opponents.

### The Cheetah

The cheetah, or *Acinonyx jubatus*, is a sleek and agile hunter found on the savannas and grasslands of Africa. Known for its incredible speed and unique appearance—with distinctive black tear marks under its eyes—this feline is built for one thing: speed. Cheetahs are social animals and often hunt in small groups, but their primary weapon is their unmatched ability to accelerate quickly.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** 110 to 140 pounds
- **Length / Height:** Up to 4.5 feet tall at the shoulder and 3.5 to 4.5 feet long. That's about the length of a small sedan!
- **Top Speed:** Top speed of 60 to 70 mph! That's faster than a school bus on the highway!
- **Bite Force / Strength:** (approx.) 450 PSI

**Weapons:**
- Sharp claws (up to 2 inches long)
- Powerful jaws
- Speedy legs

**Fighting Style:**
Cheetahs rely on their astonishing speed and agility to chase down prey. They can accelerate from 0 to 60 mph in just 3 seconds! When it comes to fighting, they prefer to avoid confrontation, using their speed to evade danger. However, if cornered, they can use their quick turns and powerful claws to defend themselves.
