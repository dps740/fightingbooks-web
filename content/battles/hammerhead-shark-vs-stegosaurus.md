## Meet the Fighters

### The Hammerhead Shark

The hammerhead shark, scientifically known as *Sphyrna mokarran*, is a sleek predator found in warm tropical and temperate oceans worldwide. With its iconic hammer-shaped head, this shark has a reputation for being a fierce hunter, especially known for its ability to detect prey hidden in the sand using its amazing sensory system.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Up to 1,000 pounds
- **Length / Height:** Up to 20 feet long
- **Top Speed:** 25 mph in bursts

**Weapons:**
- Wide hammer head that can pin prey to the ocean floor
- Rows of razor-sharp serrated teeth that constantly regrow
- Incredible speed bursts for ambush attacks

**Fighting Style:**
Hammerhead sharks are solitary hunters that prefer to ambush their prey. They use their unique head shape to pin down stingrays and other fish, making them highly effective predators. With their incredible speed and agility, they can strike quickly, making them fearsome opponents in the water.

### The Stegosaurus

The stegosaurus, known scientifically as *Stegosaurus ungulatus*, was a massive herbivorous dinosaur that roamed dry, open woodlands and plains during the late Jurassic period. With its iconic double row of large plates along its back and a spiked tail, this dinosaur has a reputation as one of the most recognizable dinosaurs of all time.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** About 5 tons (approximately 10,000 pounds)
- **Length / Height:** About 30 feet long
- **Top Speed:** Can run up to 5 miles per hour

**Weapons:**
- Tail spikes (up to 2 feet long) used for defense
- Armored plates that protect its body
- Strong legs for stability and power

**Fighting Style:**
The stegosaurus primarily relied on its defensive capabilities rather than aggressive tactics. When threatened, it would use its powerful tail spikes, known as a "thagomizer," to fend off predators. With its large size and intimidating appearance, the stegosaurus could deter many would-be attackers simply by being present.
