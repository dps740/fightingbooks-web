## Meet the Fighters

### The Tiger

Tigers are magnificent big cats known for their striking orange coats and bold black stripes. Found primarily in tropical forests, grasslands, and swamps across Asia, they are solitary hunters that dominate their territories. With their incredible strength and agility, tigers are feared predators, stalking their prey with stealth and precision.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Up to 660 pounds!
- **Length / Height:** Up to 10 feet long including the tail! That's as long as a SCHOOL BUS!
- **Top Speed:** Can run up to 30 miles per hour! That's as fast as a speeding CAR!

**Weapons:**
- Powerful jaws with a bite force of 1,000 PSI
- Sharp claws up to 3 inches long
- Strong legs for pouncing

**Fighting Style:**
Tigers are expert ambush predators. They rely on their camouflage and stealth to stalk their prey, waiting patiently until they can launch a powerful pounce. Tigers typically hunt alone, using their strength and agility to overpower animals much larger than themselves.

### The Orca

Orcas, also known as killer whales, are highly intelligent and social marine mammals that inhabit oceans worldwide, especially in colder waters. These majestic creatures are not actually whales but are the largest members of the dolphin family. Orcas are known for their striking black and white coloration and are top predators in the ocean, feeding on fish, seals, and even whales!

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Up to 12,000 pounds!
- **Length / Height:** Up to 32 feet long!
- **Top Speed:** Can swim up to 34.5 mph! That's faster than most speedboats!

**Weapons:**
- Powerful jaws with 40-56 teeth, each up to 4 inches long
- Speedy swimming
- Echolocation to find prey

**Fighting Style:**
Orcas are known for their teamwork and intelligence. They often hunt in pods, coordinating their attacks to take down larger prey. Their echolocation allows them to navigate and communicate effectively underwater, making them formidable opponents. When faced with threats, orcas can use their speed and agility to evade or counterattack.
