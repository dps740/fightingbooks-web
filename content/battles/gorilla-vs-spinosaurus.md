## Meet the Fighters

### The Gorilla

Gorillas are the largest living primates, found in the tropical forests of Africa. These intelligent and social creatures are known for their strength and gentle demeanor, often living in groups called troops. Despite their fierce appearance, gorillas are mainly herbivores, munching on leaves, stems, and fruits.

- **Weight:** Up to 400 pounds!
- **Length / Height:** Up to 6 feet tall and 12 feet long when standing on all fours! That's as tall as a door!
- **Top Speed:** Can run up to 20 miles per hour! That's as fast as a speeding car in a school zone!

**Weapons:**
- Strong arms with a reach of 8 feet
- Powerful jaws
- Intimidating chest thump

**Fighting Style:**
Gorillas typically rely on their immense strength and size to defend themselves. They can use their long arms to strike or intimidate opponents. When threatened, a gorilla might charge, beating its chest to assert dominance and scare off attackers. While they usually prefer peaceful resolutions, they can be fierce when necessary.

- **Top Speed:** Could move at about 12 mph! That's about as fast as a human sprinter — pretty quick for a 7-ton dinosaur!

**Weapons:**

- Huge sail on its back

**Fighting Style:**
Spinosaurus is a powerful predator that likely used its long, crocodile-like snout to catch fish and other prey. It could also utilize its massive size and strength to overpower opponents. In a fight, Spinosaurus would likely rely on its powerful bite and sharp claws, striking swiftly and effectively.
