## Meet the Fighters

### The Wolf

The Gray Wolf, known scientifically as *Canis lupus*, roams the forests, tundras, and grasslands of North America, Europe, and Asia. These social animals are famous for their pack mentality, often hunting in groups to take down larger prey. With their incredible sense of smell and sharp instincts, wolves have earned a reputation as one of nature's top predators.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** 50-110 pounds
- **Length / Height:** Up to 6.5 feet long (including tail) - ABOUT AS LONG AS A SCHOOL BUS!
- **Top Speed:** Can run up to 40 mph - FASTER THAN A SPRINTING HUMAN!

**Weapons:**
- Powerful jaws (up to 1,500 PSI)
- Sharp claws
- Keen sense of smell

**Fighting Style:**
Wolves primarily rely on teamwork when hunting, using their keen senses and speed to outmaneuver prey. They often ambush their targets, using stealth to get close before launching an attack. In a fight, a wolf will use its powerful jaws and sharp claws to defend itself or take down an opponent.

### The Octopus

The Common Octopus, or *Octopus vulgaris*, is a master of disguise that thrives in oceans and seas worldwide. With its ability to change colors and textures, this intelligent creature is known for its problem-solving skills and agility. Octopuses are fascinating animals that can escape predators and traps, making them one of the ocean's most enigmatic inhabitants.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Up to 22 pounds
- **Length / Height:** Up to 3.5 feet long! That's about the length of a SCHOOL BUS! (35 feet)!
- **Top Speed:** Can jet through water at 25 mph! That's faster than most people can swim — by A LOT!

**Weapons:**
- Beak (1 inch)
- Ink cloud
- Camouflage

**Fighting Style:**
Octopuses are strategic fighters, often relying on their ability to blend into their surroundings to avoid detection. When threatened, they can release an ink cloud to create a smokescreen, allowing them to escape. If cornered, they use their beak to bite and their arms to grapple with predators.
