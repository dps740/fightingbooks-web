### The Moose

The Moose, known for its massive size and distinctive antlers, is the largest member of the deer family. These gentle giants inhabit forests and wetlands across North America and parts of Europe and Asia. While they may look calm and docile, moose can be surprisingly aggressive, especially when provoked.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Approx. 272 to 700 kg (600 to 1,500 lbs)
- **Length / Height:** 6 to 7.5 feet tall at the shoulder
- **Top Speed:** Can run up to 35 mph

**Weapons:**
- Antlers up to 6 feet wide

- Kicking power

**Fighting Style:**
Moose are generally non-aggressive but can defend themselves fiercely if threatened. They rely on their immense size and powerful legs to stomp at predators. During fights, especially in mating season, moose will use their antlers to clash with rivals. Their size allows them to intimidate many potential threats.
