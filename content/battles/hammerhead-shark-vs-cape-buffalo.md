## Meet the Fighters

### The Hammerhead Shark

The hammerhead shark, scientifically known as *Sphyrna mokarran*, is a fascinating predator of the ocean. Found in warm tropical and temperate waters worldwide, this shark is easily recognized by its unique hammer-shaped head, which not only looks cool but also serves a vital purpose in hunting. With a reputation for being aggressive and skilled hunters, hammerheads have earned their place as one of the ocean's top predators.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Up to 1,000 pounds
- **Length / Height:** Up to 20 feet long
- **Top Speed:** 25 mph in bursts

**Weapons:**
- Wide hammer head that pins prey to the ocean floor — like using a spatula!
- Rows of razor-sharp serrated teeth that constantly regrow
- Incredible speed bursts for ambush attacks

**Fighting Style:**
Hammerhead sharks are known for their ambush tactics. They rely on their speed and agility to surprise prey, often using their unique head shape to pin down smaller fish like stingrays. They can hunt both solo and in schools, which can consist of up to 500 sharks, providing safety in numbers and overwhelming their targets.

### The Cape Buffalo

The cape buffalo, scientifically known as *Syncerus caffer*, roams the grasslands and savannas of Africa. Known for their strength and resilience, these animals are often referred to as "Black Death" due to their fierce nature and ability to take down predators. With a formidable presence, cape buffalos are not to be underestimated!

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Up to 2,000 pounds
- **Length / Height:** Up to 11.5 feet long and about 5.5 feet tall at the shoulder
- **Top Speed:** Can run up to 35 mph

**Weapons:**
- Massive horns that can reach up to 3.5 feet long
- Powerful legs for charging and defending
- Thick skin that provides natural protection

**Fighting Style:**
Cape buffalos are known for their herd behavior, which helps them defend against predators. When threatened, they will often charge at high speeds to scare off attackers. Their horns are a crucial weapon, allowing them to engage in head-to-head confrontations with enemies. If a buffalo feels cornered, it will not hesitate to fight back fiercely!
