## Meet the Fighters

### The Gorilla

Gorillas are the largest primates on Earth, roaming the tropical forests of Africa. These gentle giants are known for their social behavior and strong family bonds. Despite their intimidating size and strength, gorillas are primarily herbivores, munching on leaves, fruits, and flowers. 

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Up to 400 pounds!
- **Length / Height:** Up to 6 feet tall and 12 feet long when standing on all fours! That's as tall as a door!
- **Top Speed:** Can run up to 20 miles per hour! That's as fast as a speeding car in a school zone!

**Weapons:**
- Strong arms with a reach of 8 feet
- Powerful jaws
- Intimidating chest thump

**Fighting Style:**
Gorillas are known for their incredible strength and intelligence. They often use their size to intimidate opponents before engaging in direct confrontations. When fighting, they may use their powerful arms to swing and their fists to deliver bone-crushing blows. Gorillas usually fight alone or in defense of their group, using both aggression and agility to outmaneuver their rivals.

### The Great White Shark

The great white shark is one of the most recognizable and feared predators in the ocean. Found in coastal waters worldwide, these carnivorous giants are known for their impressive hunting skills. With a sleek body and powerful tail, they are built for speed and agility, making them highly effective hunters.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Over 2,400 pounds!
- **Length / Height:** Up to 20 feet long! 
- **Top Speed:** Can swim up to 25 miles per hour! That's faster than a speeding car!

**Weapons:**
- Jaws with 300 sharp teeth, each up to 3 inches long!
- Powerful tail
- Speedy body

**Fighting Style:**
Great white sharks are ambush predators that rely on stealth and speed to capture their prey. They often employ a tactic called “breaching,” where they leap out of the water to surprise seals and other animals. In a fight, they use their powerful jaws to deliver crushing bites, and their speed allows them to dart quickly away from danger or chase down fleeing prey.
