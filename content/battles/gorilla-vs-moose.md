## Meet the Fighters

### The Gorilla

The gorilla, known scientifically as *Gorilla gorilla*, is one of the largest primates on Earth. These incredible creatures inhabit the tropical forests of Africa, where they roam in small family groups, showcasing their intelligence and strong social bonds. With their imposing size and powerful build, gorillas are often regarded as gentle giants, but they can be fierce when threatened.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Up to 400 pounds
- **Length / Height:** Up to 6 feet tall and 12 feet long when standing on all fours! That's as tall as a door!
- **Top Speed:** Can run up to 20 miles per hour! That's as fast as a speeding car in a school zone!

**Weapons:**
- Strong arms with a reach of 8 feet
- Powerful jaws
- Intimidating chest thump

**Fighting Style:**
Gorillas typically rely on their strength and intelligence during confrontations. They use their long arms to reach out and strike at opponents and can deliver powerful bites if necessary. When threatened, a gorilla may puff up its chest and thump loudly to intimidate rivals, often preferring to avoid conflict if possible.

### The Moose

The moose, known scientifically as *Alces alces*, is the largest member of the deer family. These magnificent animals are found in the forests and wetlands of North America, Europe, and parts of Asia. They are renowned for their impressive antlers and hefty size, making them a sight to behold in the wild. Moose are generally peaceful, grazing on leaves, twigs, and aquatic plants, but they can be quite aggressive when defending themselves or their territory.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Up to 1,500 pounds
- **Length / Height:** 6 to 7.5 feet tall at the shoulder! That's as tall as a DOORWAY!
- **Top Speed:** Can run up to 35 mph! That's as fast as a speeding CAR!

**Weapons:**
- Antlers up to 6 feet wide!
- Strong legs
- Kicking power

**Fighting Style:**
Moose are powerful fighters, using their enormous size and strength to their advantage. They can kick with great force, using their legs to defend themselves against threats. When challenged, a moose may charge at its opponent, using its antlers as weapons to inflict damage.
