## Meet the Fighters

### The Tiger

The tiger, scientifically known as *Panthera tigris*, roams the tropical forests, grasslands, and swamps of Asia. As one of the largest and most powerful cats in the world, tigers are known for their strength and solitary hunting skills. With their striking orange coats and distinctive black stripes, they are not just beautiful but also fearsome predators.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** 220 to 660 pounds
- **Length / Height:** Up to 10 feet long including the tail! That's as long as a SCHOOL BUS!
- **Top Speed:** Can run up to 30 miles per hour! That's as fast as a speeding CAR!

**Weapons:**
- Powerful jaws with a bite force of 1,000 PSI
- Sharp claws up to 3 inches long
- Strong legs for pouncing

**Fighting Style:**
Tigers are ambush predators, relying on their camouflage and stealth to sneak up on their prey. They typically hunt alone, using their strength to overpower animals much larger than themselves. With their powerful limbs, they can pounce on their target with incredible speed and precision.

### The Eagle

The bald eagle, or *Haliaeetus leucocephalus*, is a majestic bird that lives near large bodies of water. With its impressive wingspan and keen eyesight, the eagle is a symbol of strength and freedom. Known for its hunting prowess, the bald eagle primarily hunts fish but will also target small mammals and other birds.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Approximately 3 to 6.3 kilograms (7 to 14 pounds)
- **Length / Height:** Wingspan of up to 7.5 feet! That's longer than a sedan (15 feet) laid on its side!
- **Top Speed:** Can dive at speeds of 30-40 mph! That's as fast as a car on the highway!

**Weapons:**
- Sharp talons (up to 2.5 inches long)
- Strong beak
- Keen eyesight

**Fighting Style:**
Eagles are aerial hunters that rely on their incredible vision and speed to catch their prey. They often dive from great heights, using gravity to increase their speed and strike with precision. Their sharp talons grab hold of their prey, making them a formidable foe in the air.
