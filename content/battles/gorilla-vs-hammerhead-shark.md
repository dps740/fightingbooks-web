## Meet the Fighters

### The Gorilla

The gorilla is an impressive creature, known for its massive size and gentle nature. These incredible animals inhabit the tropical forests of Africa, where they roam in family groups, munching on a diet rich in leaves, fruits, and flowers. Despite their intimidating appearance, gorillas are primarily herbivores and exhibit fascinating social behaviors.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Up to 400 pounds! 
- **Length / Height:** Up to 6 feet tall and 12 feet long when standing on all fours! That's as tall as a door!
- **Top Speed:** Can run up to 20 miles per hour! That's as fast as a speeding car in a school zone!

**Weapons:**
- Strong arms with a reach of 8 feet
- Powerful jaws
- Intimidating chest thump

**Fighting Style:**
Gorillas are known for their raw power and strength. They typically engage in direct confrontations, using their impressive size and muscular arms to intimidate threats. In a fight, a gorilla would likely stand its ground, utilizing its strength and agility to fend off any attacker.

### The Hammerhead Shark

The hammerhead shark is one of the ocean's most iconic predators, recognized for its unique hammer-shaped head. Found in warm tropical and temperate waters worldwide, these fish are agile hunters, often found near coral reefs or swimming in schools. Their impressive hunting skills and unique head shape give them an edge in spotting and capturing prey.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Up to 1,000 pounds!
- **Length / Size:** Up to 20 feet long — as long as a giraffe is tall!
- **Top Speed:** Can swim up to 25 mph in bursts — faster than most humans can run!

**Weapons:**
- Wide hammer head that pins prey to the ocean floor — like using a spatula!
- Rows of razor-sharp serrated teeth that constantly regrow
- Incredible speed bursts for ambush attacks

**Fighting Style:**
Hammerhead sharks are known for their ambush tactics. They utilize their speed and keen senses to surprise their prey. With excellent 360-degree vision, these sharks can spot potential threats or prey from all angles. In a confrontation, they would likely rely on their speed and agility to attack swiftly and efficiently.
