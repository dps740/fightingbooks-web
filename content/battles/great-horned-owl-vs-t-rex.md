## Meet the Fighters

### The Great Horned Owl

The Great Horned Owl, scientifically known as *Bubo virginianus*, is one of the most widespread owls in the Americas. You can find them in forests, deserts, swamps, and even bustling cities! With tufted “horns” that make it look like a fierce cat, this owl is a master of stealth and hunting.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Up to 5.5 pounds
- **Length / Height:** Body is 2 feet long, wingspan up to 5 feet
- **Top Speed:** Can dive at 40 mph and fly at 40 mph
- **Bite Force / Strength:** Talons grip with 300 PSI of crushing force

**Weapons:**
- Talons that provide a crushing grip
- Silent flight feathers that eliminate noise
- Ability to rotate head up to 270 degrees for spotting prey

**Fighting Style:**
The Great Horned Owl is a solitary hunter that strikes with precision. It prefers to ambush its prey, using its silent flight to swoop down unnoticed. With the ability to turn its head almost completely around, it can track movements without giving away its position, making it a masterful predator.

### The T-Rex

The Tyrannosaurus rex, a legendary dinosaur, roamed the Earth about 68 to 66 million years ago. This colossal carnivore, known as the "tyrant lizard king," was a top predator in its time, capable of taking down enormous prey with its sheer size and power.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Up to 9 tons (or about 18,000 pounds!)
- **Length / Height:** About 40 feet long and 12 feet tall at the hips
- **Top Speed:** Estimated to reach speeds of 20-25 mph
- **Bite Force / Strength:** One of the strongest bite forces of any land animal, estimated at 8,000 pounds of pressure

**Weapons:**
- Massive, powerful jaws with sharp, serrated teeth
- Strong, muscular legs for running
- Tiny but powerful arms with two sharp claws

**Fighting Style:**
T-Rex is a brute-force fighter that relies on its massive size and incredible bite strength. It can charge at speeds that, while modest compared to smaller animals, are still formidable for its size. T-Rex often uses its powerful jaws to crush bones and tear into flesh, making it an apex predator.

In the heart of the ancient forest, the mighty T-Rex stomps through the underbrush, its massive feet creating vibrations felt by every creature nearby. The Great Horned Owl regains its composure and lands on a low branch, observing the T-Rex as it stomps in frustration. The dinosaur, with its enormous size, struggles to pivot quickly. The owl, agile and cunning, realizes it can outmaneuver the T-Rex. It dives again, this time aiming for the T-Rex's eyes, a sensitive spot that could leave the giant predator vulnerable.

As the owl strikes, the T-Rex roars, shaking the ground. The owl's talons scrape across the tough skin but fail to penetrate deeply. The T-Rex retaliates by snapping its jaws, but the owl is already airborne again, its wings flapping furiously as it escapes.

This back-and-forth continues, with the owl using its speed and stealth to evade the T-Rex's powerful attacks, while the dinosaur's sheer size and strength intimidate the smaller predator. However, the owl's strategy is clear: avoid direct confrontation and strike from above, utilizing its ability to fly silently.

Finally, after what feels like an eternity of cat-and-mouse, the T-Rex, exhausted and frustrated, makes a desperate charge. The Great Horned Owl, sensing the impending danger, takes to the air one last time. It swoops down with incredible speed, targeting the T-Rex's eyes once more, but this time it strikes with precision, momentarily blinding one eye!

The T-Rex roars in pain, thrashing wildly, but it cannot reach the agile owl now circling above. With one final effort, the owl takes a high perch, safe from the T-Rex's reach, watching as the dinosaur stumbles, disoriented and in agony.
