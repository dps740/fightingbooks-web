## Meet the Fighters

### The Rhino

The white rhinoceros, known scientifically as *Ceratotherium simum*, roams the grasslands and savannas of Africa. These impressive creatures are known for their size and strength, with a reputation that makes them one of the most formidable animals on the continent.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Up to 5,000 pounds (2,300 kg)
- **Length / Height:** Up to 13 feet long!
- **Top Speed:** 30 mph
- **Bite Force / Strength:** Not specified

**Weapons:**
- **HORN:** Up to 3 feet long!
- **THICK SKIN**
- **POWERFUL LEGS**

**Fighting Style:**
Rhinos typically use their size and strength to their advantage. They charge at threats, utilizing their impressive speed to engage in direct confrontations. Their thick skin provides some defense, while their horns can deliver lethal blows to predators or rivals. Rhinos are usually solitary, but they can be aggressive if they feel threatened.

### The Anaconda

The green anaconda, or *Eunectes murinus*, is a master of the waterways in South America. Found in swamps and rivers, these snakes are the heaviest and one of the longest snake species in the world. Their stealth and strength make them exceptional hunters in their aquatic homes.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Over 500 pounds (approx.)
- **Length / Height:** Up to 30 feet long!
- **Top Speed:** 1.5 mph (in water)
- **Bite Force / Strength:** Not specified

**Weapons:**
- **POWERFUL MUSCLES:** 30 feet long
- **CAMOUFLAGE**
- **STRONG JAWS**

**Fighting Style:**
Anacondas are ambush predators that rely on their ability to blend into their environment. They use their strong muscles to constrict and subdue prey, often swallowing them whole. While they may not be fast on land, they are agile swimmers, using the water to their advantage. Anacondas are solitary hunters and are known for their patience.
