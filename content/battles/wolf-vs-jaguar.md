## Meet the Fighters

### The Wolf

The gray wolf, known scientifically as *Canis lupus*, roams the forests, tundras, and grasslands across North America and parts of Eurasia. Renowned for their incredible teamwork and social structure, wolves hunt in packs, making them efficient and strategic hunters. With an impressive reputation in folklore and popular culture, wolves are often seen as symbols of strength and loyalty.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Up to 110 pounds
- **Length / Height:** Up to 6.5 feet long (including tail) - ABOUT AS LONG AS A SCHOOL BUS!
- **Top Speed:** Can run up to 40 mph - FASTER THAN A SPRINTING HUMAN!

**Weapons:**
- Powerful jaws (up to 1,500 PSI)
- Sharp claws
- Keen sense of smell

**Fighting Style:**
Wolves are known for their pack mentality, often working together to bring down larger prey. They employ tactics like flanking and ambushing to outsmart their opponents. While they can fight solo if necessary, their strength lies in collaboration, allowing them to coordinate attacks and outmaneuver their foes.

### The Jaguar

The jaguar, or *Panthera onca*, prowls the rainforests, swamps, and grasslands of Central and South America. As the largest big cat in the Americas, jaguars are known for their incredible strength and agility. With a reputation for being solitary and stealthy hunters, they rely on their powerful physique and striking camouflage to stalk and ambush their prey.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Up to 250 pounds
- **Length / Height:** Up to 6 feet long! That's as long as a school bus is tall!
- **Top Speed:** Can run up to 50 miles per hour! That's faster than a speeding car in a school zone!

**Weapons:**
- Powerful jaws with a bite force of 1,500 PSI
- Sharp claws
- Stealthy approach

**Fighting Style:**
Jaguars are ambush predators, often hiding in the underbrush until the perfect moment to strike. They use their powerful jaws to deliver a fatal bite, often targeting the skull or spine of their prey. Their stealth and strength make them lethal hunters, especially in their dense jungle habitats.
