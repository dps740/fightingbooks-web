## Meet the Fighters

### The Tiger

The Tiger, scientifically known as *Panthera tigris*, is one of the largest and most powerful cats on the planet. Found in diverse habitats ranging from tropical forests to grasslands and swamps across Asia, Tigers are renowned for their strength and striking appearance, with their iconic orange coats and black stripes making them one of the most recognizable animals on Earth.

**Weapons:**
- Powerful jaws with a bite force of 1,000 PSI
- Sharp claws up to 3 inches long
- Strong legs for pouncing

**Fighting Style:**
Tigers are solitary hunters, relying on their stealth to ambush prey. When they strike, they can deliver a powerful pounce, using their strength and speed to take down animals much larger than themselves. Their camouflage allows them to blend into their surroundings, making them expert stalkers.

### The Jaguar

On the other side of the ring is the Jaguar, or *Panthera onca*. Found in the rainforests, swamps, and grasslands of Central and South America, Jaguars are the third largest big cats in the world. With their stunning rosettes and powerful build, they are not just beautiful but also incredibly skilled hunters.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Up to 250 pounds
- **Length / Height:** Up to 6 feet long! That's as long as a school bus is tall!
- **Top Speed:** Can run up to 50 miles per hour! That's faster than a speeding car in a school zone!
- **Bite Force / Strength:** 1,500 PSI

**Weapons:**
- Powerful jaws with a bite force of 1,500 PSI
- Sharp claws
- Stealthy approach

**Fighting Style:**
Jaguars are known for their stealth and strength, often ambushing prey from above or lurking in the water. With incredibly powerful jaws, they can crush the shells of turtles and the skulls of their prey, making them one of the most efficient predators in the wild. Their agility allows them to navigate swiftly through both water and land.
