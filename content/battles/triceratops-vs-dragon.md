## Meet the Fighters

### The Triceratops

The Triceratops, scientifically known as *Triceratops horridus*, roamed the open plains and lush forests of North America around 68 to 66 million years ago. This herbivorous dinosaur was a massive creature, known for its impressive three horns and large bony frill, making it one of the most recognizable dinosaurs. With its robust body and fierce appearance, the Triceratops was a formidable opponent against any predator.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** 12,000 pounds (about 12 tons)
- **Length / Height:** 30 feet long!
- **Top Speed:** 20 mph!

**Weapons:**
- Horns up to 3 feet long!
- Beak for biting
- Heavy body for charging

**Fighting Style:**
The Triceratops was primarily a defensive fighter. It relied on its impressive horns and heavy body to charge at enemies, using its sheer size to intimidate and fend off predators. When threatened, it could stand its ground, using its horns to defend against attackers. While it typically fought solo, a group of Triceratops could create a formidable wall of horns against any foe.

### The Dragon

The Fire-Breathing Dragon, scientifically dubbed *Draco Ignis*, is a legendary creature often depicted in folklore and mythology. These majestic beings are said to inhabit mountains and caves, where they hoard treasures and soar through the skies. Known for their fearsome fire breath and sharp claws, dragons are symbols of power and mystery in many cultures.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** 1,500 pounds
- **Length / Height:** 30 feet long (about the length of a school bus!)
- **Top Speed:** 60 mph!

**Weapons:**
- Fire Breath (up to 1,000°F)
- Sharp Claws (2 feet long)
- Tail Swipe (5 feet wide)

**Fighting Style:**
Dragons are aggressive and cunning fighters. They often attack from the air, using their ability to fly and breathe fire to their advantage. A dragon’s strategy typically involves swooping down to unleash torrents of fire and using its claws to strike at enemies. Their camouflage skills also allow them to blend into their rocky surroundings, making ambush attacks a common tactic.
