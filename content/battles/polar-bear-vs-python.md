## Meet the Fighters

### The Polar Bear

The polar bear, or **Ursus maritimus**, is the apex predator of the Arctic regions, thriving mainly on sea ice. Known for its incredible size and strength, this majestic creature is a master hunter, primarily feeding on seals, but it can also tackle a variety of prey. With its thick white fur and a keen sense of smell, the polar bear is perfectly adapted to its icy habitat.

- **Length / Height:** Up to 10 feet long
- **Top Speed:** Can run up to 25 miles per hour

### The Python

The green python, scientifically known as **Morelia viridis**, is a master of camouflage and stealth, often found in the tropical rainforests of New Guinea and Australia. These snakes are known for their vibrant green scales, which help them blend into the leaves and branches of trees. While they might not be as large as polar bears, their constriction technique makes them lethal hunters.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Up to 40 pounds (approx.)

**Fighting Style:**
Pythons are ambush predators, relying on their ability to remain undetected until it's time to strike. Once they have a hold of their prey, they use their muscular bodies to constrict and suffocate it. Their ability to climb and navigate through trees adds to their hunting prowess, allowing them to attack from above.
