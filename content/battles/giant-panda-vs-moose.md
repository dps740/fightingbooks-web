## Meet the Fighters

### The Giant Panda

The giant panda, scientifically known as *Ailuropoda melanoleuca*, roams the misty mountain bamboo forests of central China, particularly in the provinces of Sichuan, Shaanxi, and Gansu. These bears are known for their distinctive black-and-white fur and their diet, which consists almost entirely of bamboo. Despite their cuddly appearance, giant pandas are powerful animals with impressive physical capabilities!

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** 330 pounds
- **Length / Height:** Up to 6 feet long
- **Top Speed:** Can run up to 20 mph
- **Bite Force / Strength:** 292 PSI

**Weapons:**
- Jaw muscles and teeth strong enough to crush thick bamboo stalks
- Sharp claws on powerful forearms for climbing and swiping
- A special sixth finger (pseudo-thumb) made from a modified wrist bone

**Fighting Style:**
Giant pandas are generally solitary and non-aggressive, but when threatened, they can employ their strong jaws and claws in defense. They might use ambush tactics, climbing trees to gain a higher vantage point, or swiping at threats with powerful forearms.

### The Moose

The moose, or *Alces alces*, is the largest member of the deer family, found in forests and wetlands across North America and parts of Europe and Asia. Known for their impressive antlers and towering height, moose are herbivores that primarily feed on leaves, twigs, and aquatic plants. With their massive size and strength, they command respect in the wild!

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Up to 1,800 pounds
- **Length / Height:** 6 to 7.5 feet tall at the shoulder
- **Top Speed:** Can run up to 35 mph
- **Bite Force / Strength:** Not specified

**Weapons:**
- Antlers up to 6 feet wide
- Strong legs for kicking
- Powerful body for pushing through obstacles

**Fighting Style:**
Moose are not typically aggressive unless provoked, but when they feel threatened, they can use their huge size and powerful legs to kick or charge. Their antlers serve as formidable weapons, especially during mating season when males compete for dominance.
