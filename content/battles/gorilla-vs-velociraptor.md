## Meet the Fighters

### The Gorilla

The gorilla, scientifically known as *Gorilla gorilla*, is one of the largest primates on the planet. These magnificent creatures inhabit the tropical forests of Africa, where they are known for their strong social bonds and intelligence. With their imposing size and gentle demeanor, gorillas are often seen as symbols of strength and wisdom.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** 400 pounds
- **Length / Height:** Up to 6 feet tall and 12 feet long when standing on all fours! That's as tall as a door!
- **Top Speed:** Can run up to 20 miles per hour! That's as fast as a speeding car in a school zone!

**Weapons:**
- Strong arms with a reach of 8 feet
- Powerful jaws
- Intimidating chest thump

**Fighting Style:**
Gorillas are generally peaceful but can become fierce when threatened. They rely on their massive size and strength to intimidate opponents. When fighting, a gorilla may charge at an enemy, using its powerful arms to throw punches or grapple. They are known to thump their chests as a display of dominance, making them a formidable opponent.

### The Velociraptor

The velociraptor, or *Velociraptor mongoliensis*, lived during the late Cretaceous period and is often depicted as a cunning hunter. These small but deadly dinosaurs thrived in dry, open areas like deserts and plains. With their sharp claws and keen intelligence, velociraptors are often portrayed as pack hunters, making them efficient predators.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Approx. 33 pounds
- **Length / Height:** About 6.5 feet long! That's like a small car! (A sedan is ~15 feet)!
- **Top Speed:** Top speed of 24 mph! That's faster than a human sprinter!

**Weapons:**
- Clawed toes (up to 3 inches long)
- Sharp teeth
- Speed and agility

**Fighting Style:**
Velociraptors are known for their speed and agility. They often hunt in packs, using coordinated tactics to take down larger prey. In a fight, a velociraptor would likely dart in and out, using its sharp claws and teeth to deliver quick, lethal strikes. Their quick reflexes allow them to dodge larger opponents and strike with precision.
