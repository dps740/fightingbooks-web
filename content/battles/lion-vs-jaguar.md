## Meet the Fighters

### The Lion

Lions, known scientifically as *Panthera leo*, are majestic creatures that roam the grasslands, savannas, and open woodlands of Africa and India. Often called the "king of the jungle," lions are famed for their powerful roars and social behavior, living in groups called prides. These social structures play a vital role in their hunting strategies and protection against threats.

### The Jaguar

Jaguars, known as *Panthera onca*, are the largest big cats in the Americas and are renowned for their incredible strength and swimming abilities. These solitary hunters inhabit rainforests, swamps, and grasslands across Central and South America. With their stunning rosettes and powerful build, jaguars are often seen as symbols of beauty and ferocity.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Up to 250 pounds
- **Length / Height:** About 6 feet long
- **Top Speed:** 50 miles per hour
- **Bite Force / Strength:** 1,500 PSI

**Weapons:**
- Powerful jaws with a bite force of 1,500 PSI
- Sharp claws
- Stealthy approach

**Fighting Style:**
Jaguars are solitary hunters that rely on stealth and surprise. They often stalk their prey quietly and can leap great distances to ambush. Their powerful jaws allow them to crush bones, which is particularly useful when hunting armored animals like turtles or caimans.
