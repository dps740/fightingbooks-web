## Meet the Fighters

### The Lion

The lion (Panthera leo) reigns supreme in the grasslands and savannas of Africa and India. Known for their majestic manes and social behavior, lions live in groups called prides, which help them hunt and protect their territory. These big cats are not just beautiful to look at; they are fierce and strategic hunters, making them one of the most respected predators on land.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Up to 420 pounds
- **Length / Height:** About 8 to 10 feet long from nose to tail
- **Top Speed:** Up to 50 miles per hour for short bursts
- **Bite Force / Strength:** 650 PSI

**Weapons:**
- Powerful jaws with a bite force of 650 PSI
- Sharp claws up to 1.5 inches long
- Strong legs for pouncing

**Fighting Style:**
Lions are known for their tactical hunting abilities, often coordinating attacks as a pride. They use their speed and strength to ambush prey, employing a pouncing technique from a hidden position. In a fight, a lion would likely use its powerful forelimbs to strike and its sharp claws to inflict damage while trying to outmaneuver its opponent.

### The Alligator

The American alligator (Alligator mississippiensis) is a formidable predator found in the swamps, rivers, and lakes of the southeastern United States. With their tough, armored skin and impressive size, alligators are not only experts at swimming but also stealthy hunters on land. They can lie in wait for hours, ready to strike at any unsuspecting prey that comes too close.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Up to 1,000 pounds
- **Length / Height:** Up to 13 feet long
- **Top Speed:** Can run up to 20 miles per hour on land
- **Bite Force / Strength:** 2,125 PSI

**Weapons:**
- Powerful jaws with a bite force of 2,125 PSI
- Sharp teeth
- Long tail for swimming

**Fighting Style:**
Alligators are ambush predators, often using their camouflage to blend into the water or vegetation. They strike with incredible speed and power, grabbing their prey with a crushing bite. In a confrontation, an alligator would likely use its massive jaws to clamp down on its opponent, using its strength to drag them underwater if possible.
