## Meet the Fighters

### The Tiger

The tiger, known scientifically as *Panthera tigris*, is one of the most iconic big cats in the world. These majestic creatures thrive in tropical forests, grasslands, and swamps across Asia. Renowned for their beauty and power, tigers are solitary hunters, relying on their keen senses and stealth to take down prey.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Up to 660 pounds
- **Length / Height:** Up to 10 feet long including the tail! That's as long as a SCHOOL BUS!
- **Top Speed:** Can run up to 30 miles per hour! That's as fast as a speeding CAR!

**Weapons:**
- Powerful jaws with a bite force of 1,000 PSI
- Sharp claws up to 3 inches long
- Strong legs for pouncing

**Fighting Style:**
Tigers are ambush predators that prefer to stalk their prey silently, using their camouflage to blend into the environment. Once close enough, they utilize their incredible speed and strength to pounce, often delivering a lethal bite to the neck or throat of their victim.

### The Anaconda

The green anaconda, scientifically known as *Eunectes murinus*, is the largest snake in the world by weight and one of the longest, reaching impressive lengths. These incredible reptiles inhabit swamps and rivers in South America, where they are expert ambush predators. Their reputation as formidable hunters comes from their ability to constrict and swallow prey whole.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Can weigh over 500 pounds
- **Length / Height:** Up to 30 feet long! That's longer than a SCHOOL BUS!
- **Top Speed:** Can swim at 1.5 mph, like a slow jog!

**Weapons:**
- Powerful muscles (30 feet long)
- Camouflage
- Strong jaws

**Fighting Style:**
Anacondas use their enormous size and strength to overpower prey. They are ambush predators, lying in wait for unsuspecting animals to come close before launching themselves with incredible speed from the water. Once they have a grip on their prey, they coil around it, using their powerful muscles to constrict and suffocate.
