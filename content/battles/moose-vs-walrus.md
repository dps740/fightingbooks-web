## Meet the Fighters

### The Moose

The moose, scientifically known as *Alces alces*, roams the forests and wetlands of North America and parts of Europe and Asia. These magnificent creatures are known for their impressive size and unique features, such as long legs and broad antlers. With a diet primarily consisting of leaves and aquatic plants, moose are gentle giants—unless provoked!

- **Weight:** Approximately 1,200 pounds
- **Length / Height:** 6 to 7.5 feet tall at the shoulder! That's as tall as a DOORWAY!
- **Top Speed:** Can run up to 35 mph! That's as fast as a speeding CAR!

**Weapons:**
- Antlers up to 6 feet wide!
- Strong legs
- Kicking power

**Fighting Style:**
Moose are solitary fighters, relying on their size and strength to intimidate opponents. When threatened, they can charge at a target or kick with their powerful legs, delivering a forceful blow. They are also known to use their massive antlers as a weapon, swinging them to fend off attackers.

### The Walrus

The walrus, known scientifically as *Odobenus rosmarus*, is a unique marine mammal found in the icy waters of the Arctic and sub-Arctic regions. With their distinctive tusks and blubbery bodies, walruses are not only impressive but also highly social animals. They can often be seen lounging on ice floes and diving for clams and mollusks.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Up to 2,000 pounds
- **Length / Height:** Up to 12 feet long!
- **Top Speed:** Can swim up to 35 mph! That's like a speeding car on the highway!

**Weapons:**
- Tusks up to 3 feet long!
- Strong flippers
- Massive body weight

**Fighting Style:**
Walruses are not typically aggressive, but they can be fierce when defending themselves or their young. They use their impressive tusks for combat, either to stab or to push opponents away. Their massive bodies also give them a weight advantage, allowing them to roll over opponents or crush them if necessary.
