## Meet the Fighters

### The Jaguar

The jaguar, scientifically known as *Panthera onca*, is one of the most formidable big cats roaming the rainforests, swamps, and grasslands of Central and South America. Renowned for its strength and agility, this apex predator is often referred to as the “king of the jungle.” Jaguars are solitary hunters, stalking their prey with a blend of stealth and power.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Up to 250 pounds
- **Length / Height:** Up to 6 feet long!
- **Top Speed:** Can run up to 50 miles per hour!
- **Bite Force / Strength:** 1,500 PSI

**Weapons:**
- Powerful jaws with a bite force of 1,500 PSI
- Sharp claws
- Stealthy approach

**Fighting Style:**
Jaguars are known for their ambush tactics. They rely on their incredible camouflage to blend into the jungle, waiting for the perfect moment to strike. With their immense strength, they take down prey quickly and efficiently, often using their powerful jaws to deliver a swift fatal bite.

### The Great Horned Owl

The great horned owl, or *Bubo virginianus*, is one of the most widespread owls in the Americas, found in forests, deserts, swamps, and even urban areas. Known for its distinctive tufted “horns” and fierce hunting skills, this owl is a master of stealth. Great horned owls are known to be fearless hunters, often taking on prey much larger than themselves.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Up to 5.5 pounds
- **Length / Height:** Body is 2 feet long, wingspan up to 5 feet wide!
- **Top Speed:** Can dive at 40 mph and fly at 40 mph!
- **Bite Force / Strength:** 300 PSI

**Weapons:**
- Talons that grip with 300 PSI of crushing force
- Silent flight feathers with serrated edges
- Can rotate head up to 270 degrees

**Fighting Style:**
Great horned owls are expert ambush predators. They fly silently, using their incredible vision to spot prey in low light. With their powerful talons, they can swoop down and catch unsuspecting animals, delivering a crushing grip that immobilizes them instantly.
