## Meet the Fighters

### The Hammerhead Shark

The hammerhead shark, known scientifically as *Sphyrna mokarran*, is one of the most fascinating marine creatures roaming the warm tropical and temperate oceans worldwide. Renowned for its distinctive hammer-shaped head, this shark is a formidable predator, primarily feasting on stingrays, fish, and even other sharks!

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Up to 1,000 pounds!
- **Length / Height:** Up to 20 feet long!
- **Top Speed:** 25 mph in bursts!

**Weapons:**
- Wide hammer head pins prey to the ocean floor — like using a spatula!
- Rows of razor-sharp serrated teeth that constantly regrow.
- Incredible speed bursts for ambush attacks.

**Fighting Style:**
Hammerhead sharks are known for their stealth and ambush tactics. They often hunt alone or in small groups, using their wide heads to corner prey and their powerful bodies to strike with speed and precision. With 360-degree vision, they can spot predators or prey from all angles, making them astute hunters.

### The Leopard

The leopard, or *Panthera pardus*, is a magnificent big cat that thrives in various habitats, including savannas, forests, and grasslands. These agile and powerful hunters are known for their striking spotted fur and incredible climbing ability. With a diet that includes deer, antelope, and monkeys, leopards are skilled hunters that can adapt to many environments.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Between 66 to 176 pounds.
- **Length / Height:** Up to 6.25 feet long!
- **Top Speed:** Can run up to 36 mph!

**Weapons:**
- Powerful jaws (up to 2 inches long).
- Sharp claws.
- Agile body.

**Fighting Style:**
Leopards are masters of stealth, often stalking their prey before launching a surprise attack. They use their sharp claws and powerful jaws to overpower their victims. Known for their climbing skills, leopards can take their prey into trees, keeping it safe from scavengers while they feast.
