## Meet the Fighters

### The Wolf

The Gray Wolf (Canis lupus) roams the forests, tundras, and grasslands, renowned for its intelligence and social structure. These animals are not just solitary hunters; they thrive in packs, which allows them to tackle larger prey and defend their territory effectively.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** 50-110 pounds
- **Length / Height:** Up to 6.5 feet long (including tail) - ABOUT AS LONG AS A SCHOOL BUS!
- **Top Speed:** Can run up to 40 mph - FASTER THAN A SPRINTING HUMAN!

**Weapons:**
- Powerful jaws (up to 1,500 PSI)
- Sharp claws
- Keen sense of smell

**Fighting Style:**
Wolves are strategic fighters, often employing teamwork when hunting. They use their speed and endurance to tire out their prey, striking when the opportunity arises. Their powerful jaws can deliver devastating bites, and they often rely on their keen sense of smell to track down their target.

### The Eagle

The Bald Eagle (Haliaeetus leucocephalus) is a symbol of strength and freedom, typically found near large bodies of open water. Known for its impressive wingspan and keen eyesight, this bird of prey is a skillful hunter, primarily targeting fish but also taking down smaller mammals and birds.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** 3-6.3 kilograms (approx. 6.6-14 pounds)
- **Length / Size:** Wingspan of up to 7.5 feet! That's longer than a sedan (15 feet) laid on its side!
- **Top Speed:** Can dive at speeds of 30-40 mph! That's as fast as a car on the highway!

**Weapons:**
- Sharp talons (up to 2.5 inches long)
- Strong beak
- Keen eyesight

**Fighting Style:**
Eagles are aerial aces, relying on their remarkable vision to spot prey from great heights. They swoop down with incredible speed, using their talons to grab and immobilize their target. Their ability to fly allows them to escape danger quickly and strike from an advantageous position.
