## Meet the Fighters

### The Lion

The lion, known scientifically as *Panthera leo*, roams the grasslands, savannas, and open woodlands of Africa and India. Often dubbed the "king of the jungle" (even though they actually live in grasslands!), lions are social animals that live in prides, making them one of the most recognizable and revered big cats in the animal kingdom.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** 420 pounds
- **Length / Height:** About 8 to 10 feet long from nose to tail
- **Top Speed:** 50 miles per hour for short bursts
- **Bite Force / Strength:** 650 PSI

**Weapons:**
- Powerful jaws with a bite force of 650 PSI
- Sharp claws up to 1.5 inches long
- Strong legs for pouncing

**Fighting Style:**
Lions are strategic fighters. They often use teamwork to hunt, working together in groups to take down prey. Individual lions can also fight solo, employing ambush tactics to surprise their opponents. With their powerful legs and keen senses, they can pounce on their target from a distance, delivering a devastating blow.

### The T-Rex

The Tyrannosaurus rex, or T-Rex for short, was a massive carnivorous dinosaur that roamed the Earth approximately 68 to 66 million years ago. Known for its impressive size and fearsome reputation, this apex predator was the ruler of its domain, striking terror into the hearts of its prey with its sheer strength and ferocity.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** 9 tons (approximately 18,000 pounds)
- **Length / Height:** About 40 feet long and 12 feet tall at the hips
- **Top Speed:** 20 miles per hour (approx.)
- **Bite Force / Strength:** 12,800 PSI

**Weapons:**
- Massive jaws filled with sharp teeth
- Strong legs used for running and stability
- Powerful tail for balance and striking

**Fighting Style:**
The T-Rex was a formidable fighter, relying on its immense size and strength to overpower opponents. It used its powerful jaws to crush bone and tear flesh, making short work of its prey. While it may not have been the fastest dinosaur, its sheer mass and crushing bite force made it a fearsome predator in any confrontation.
