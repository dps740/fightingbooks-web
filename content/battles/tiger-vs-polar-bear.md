## Meet the Fighters

### The Tiger

The tiger (scientific name: *Panthera tigris*) roams the tropical forests, grasslands, and swamps of Asia. With its stunning orange coat and distinctive black stripes, it’s not just beautiful; it’s a master of camouflage. Tigers are solitary hunters, often stalking their prey with incredible stealth before launching a powerful attack.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** 660 pounds
- **Length / Height:** Up to 10 feet long (including the tail!)
- **Top Speed:** Can run up to 30 miles per hour
- **Bite Force / Strength:** 1,000 PSI

**Weapons:**
- Powerful jaws with a bite force of 1,000 PSI
- Sharp claws up to 3 inches long
- Strong legs for pouncing

**Fighting Style:**
Tigers are ambush hunters, relying on their camouflage to sneak up on prey. They use their powerful legs to leap great distances and their sharp claws to grip and hold onto their catch. In a fight, they would use their speed and agility to dodge attacks and strike with precision.

### The Polar Bear

The polar bear (scientific name: *Ursus maritimus*) is the largest land carnivore and is most commonly found in the Arctic regions, primarily on sea ice. With its thick layer of blubber and transparent fur, it’s perfectly adapted to the cold environment. Polar bears are known for their exceptional swimming abilities and keen sense of smell, which they use to hunt seals and other marine life.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Up to 1,500 pounds
- **Length / Height:** Up to 10 feet long
- **Top Speed:** Can run up to 25 miles per hour
- **Bite Force / Strength:** 1,200 PSI

**Weapons:**
- Powerful jaws with a bite force of 1,200 PSI
- Sharp claws up to 2 inches long
- Strong legs for powerful swimming

**Fighting Style:**
Polar bears are formidable fighters, using their immense strength to overpower prey. They rely on their powerful limbs to deliver crushing blows and can swim swiftly to escape or pursue. In combat, they would use their size and brute force to dominate an opponent.
