## Meet the Fighters

### The Polar Bear

The polar bear, scientifically known as *Ursus maritimus*, is a colossal carnivore that thrives in the icy habitats of the Arctic. Known for its impressive size and striking white coat, this powerful predator is perfectly adapted to hunting seals on sea ice.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Up to 1,500 pounds
- **Length / Height:** Up to 10 feet long
- **Top Speed:** Can run up to 25 miles per hour
- **Bite Force / Strength:** 1,200 PSI

**Weapons:**
- Powerful jaws with a bite force of 1,200 PSI
- Sharp claws up to 2 inches long
- Strong legs for powerful swimming

**Fighting Style:**
Polar bears are solitary hunters that rely on their strength and stealth to capture prey. They often use their keen sense of smell to locate seals hidden beneath the ice, then ambush them with explosive speed. When threatened, they can defend themselves fiercely, using a combination of their powerful jaws and sharp claws.

### The Crocodile

The saltwater crocodile, or *Crocodylus porosus*, is the largest living reptile, famed for its ferocity and cunning. Found in coastal areas, rivers, and swamps, this apex predator is feared by many due to its powerful bite and stealthy hunting tactics.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Up to 2,200 pounds
- **Length / Height:** Up to 23 feet long
- **Top Speed:** Can swim up to 20 miles per hour
- **Bite Force / Strength:** 3,700 pounds

**Weapons:**
- Powerful jaws with a bite force of 3,700 pounds
- Sharp teeth
- Long tail

**Fighting Style:**
Crocodiles are ambush predators, waiting patiently for hours to strike at their unsuspecting prey. They often drag their victims into the water to drown them. In combat, they use their powerful jaws and swift, agile movements to overpower opponents, making them formidable in any fight.
