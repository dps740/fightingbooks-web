## Meet the Fighters

### The Wolf

The Gray Wolf, also known scientifically as *Canis lupus*, is a remarkable and adaptable predator found in various habitats like forests, tundras, and grasslands. With their strong pack mentality, they are known for working together to hunt and defend their territory. These social creatures are skilled hunters, taking down prey much larger than themselves!

**Weapons:**

**Fighting Style:**
Wolves often hunt in packs, using teamwork to their advantage. They employ strategies like flanking and ambushing their prey, focusing on the weak or isolated individuals. In a one-on-one fight, a wolf's agility and speed would help it evade attacks while delivering quick bites.

### The Cape Buffalo

The Cape Buffalo, scientifically known as *Syncerus caffer*, roams the grasslands and savannas of Africa. Often referred to as "Black Death" due to their reputation for being tough and dangerous, these herbivores can weigh as much as a small car and are known for their fierce protective nature. They live in herds, relying on each other for safety against predators.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Up to 2,000 pounds
- **Length / Height:** Up to 11.5 feet long! That's like 1/3 of a school bus! They stand about 5.5 feet tall at the shoulder.
- **Top Speed:** Can run up to 35 mph! That's faster than a galloping horse!

**Weapons:**
- Horns up to 3.5 feet long!
- Powerful legs
- Thick skin

**Fighting Style:**
Cape Buffalos are known for their aggressive behavior when threatened. They charge at predators with incredible speed, using their massive horns to deliver powerful blows. When in a herd, they form a protective circle around the young or injured, making it difficult for predators to attack.
