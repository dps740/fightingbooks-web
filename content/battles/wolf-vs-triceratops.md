## Meet the Fighters

### The Wolf

The gray wolf, scientifically known as *Canis lupus*, roams the forests, tundras, and grasslands of North America, Europe, and Asia. These social animals are known for their pack mentality and impressive hunting skills, often working together to take down prey much larger than themselves.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** 50-110 pounds
- **Length / Height:** Up to 6.5 feet long (including tail) - ABOUT AS LONG AS A SCHOOL BUS!
- **Top Speed:** Can run up to 40 mph - FASTER THAN A SPRINTING HUMAN!

**Weapons:**
- Powerful jaws (up to 1,500 PSI)
- Sharp claws
- Keen sense of smell

**Fighting Style:**
Wolves are strategic hunters that often rely on teamwork. They use their speed and agility to outmaneuver prey, typically attacking from the sides or rear. While they can hunt solo, they prefer the efficiency of a pack, which allows them to take down larger animals like deer and elk.

### The Triceratops

The triceratops, or *Triceratops horridus*, was a herbivorous dinosaur that thrived in the open plains and forests of North America about 68 million years ago. With its iconic three horns and massive bony frill, this dinosaur is one of the most recognizable dinosaurs from the Late Cretaceous period.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** About 12,000 pounds (that's like 3 elephants!)
- **Length / Height:** 30 feet long - about as long as a school bus!
- **Top Speed:** Top speed of 20 mph! 

**Weapons:**
- Horns up to 3 feet long!
- Beak for biting
- Heavy body for charging

**Fighting Style:**
Triceratops were known for their defensive capabilities. When threatened, they would charge at predators using their enormous size and powerful horns as weapons. Their thick skull and bony frills offered protection against attacks, making them a formidable opponent in battle.
