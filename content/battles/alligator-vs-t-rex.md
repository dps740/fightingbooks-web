## Meet the Fighters

### The Alligator

The American alligator, known scientifically as *Alligator mississippiensis*, is a formidable predator found in the swamps, rivers, and lakes of the southeastern United States. **Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** 1,000 pounds
- **Length / Height:** Up to 13 feet long! That's about the length of a school bus!
- **Top Speed:** Can run up to 20 mph on land! That's as fast as a speeding bicycle!

**Weapons:**
- Powerful jaws with a bite force of 2,125 PSI
- Sharp teeth
- Long tail for swimming

**Fighting Style:**
The alligator is a master of ambush tactics, relying on its stealth and camouflage to surprise prey. It often lies in wait near the water's edge, launching a sudden, powerful attack when an unsuspecting animal comes close. Alligators are solitary hunters, using their impressive bite force and strong jaws to overpower their prey.

The Tyrannosaurus rex, one of the most famous dinosaurs, roamed the Earth about 68 to 66 million years ago. This colossal carnivore, often referred to as T-Rex, was a dominant predator in its ecosystem, towering over most of its contemporaries with its massive size and terrifying presence.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Approximately 9 tons (18,000 pounds)
- **Length / Height:** Up to 40 feet long!
- **Top Speed:** Estimated at about 12 mph

**Weapons:**
- Enormous, serrated teeth capable of crushing bone
- Powerful legs for running and attacking
- Strong, muscular arms with two claws

**Fighting Style:**
The T-Rex was a ferocious predator known for its brute strength and powerful bite. It likely used its keen sense of smell to track down prey and would charge at them with incredible force. Even though its arms were small, its massive jaws and weight made it a formidable opponent in any battle.
