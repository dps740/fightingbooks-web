## Meet the Fighters

### The Rhino

The white rhino, or **Ceratotherium simum**, roams the grasslands and savannas of Africa. Known for their gigantic size and formidable horn, these gentle giants are primarily herbivores, munching on grass and leaves. Despite their massive stature, they are surprisingly agile and can be quite aggressive when threatened.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** 5,000 pounds
- **Length / Height:** Up to 13 feet long
- **Top Speed:** 30 mph

**Weapons:**
- **HORN:** Up to 3 feet long!
- **THICK SKIN**
- **POWERFUL LEGS**

**Fighting Style:**
Rhinos are known for their direct and bold fighting style. They rely on their strength and size, charging at threats with their massive bodies. When feeling threatened, a rhino may lower its head and charge, using its horn to deliver powerful blows. They usually fight alone, using their brute force to intimidate and defend their territory.

### The Electric Eel

The electric eel, scientifically known as **Electrophorus electricus**, is not actually an eel but a type of fish! Found in the murky freshwater rivers and swamps of the Amazon and Orinoco basins in South America, this remarkable creature can grow up to 8 feet long and weigh around 44 pounds. Known for their stunning electrical abilities, electric eels are masters of surprise.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** 44 pounds
- **Length / Height:** Up to 8 feet long
- **Top Speed:** 3 mph

**Weapons:**
- **860 VOLTS of electricity!**
- **Three electric organs**
- **Electric pulse to reveal hidden prey**

**Fighting Style:**
Electric eels use their electricity as both offense and defense. When hunting, they can deliver a powerful shock to incapacitate prey, making it easier to consume. In a confrontation, they can unleash a series of shocks to disorient or injure an opponent. Their strategy often involves staying hidden and using their electric abilities from a distance.
