## Meet the Fighters

### The Gorilla

The gorilla, known scientifically as *Gorilla gorilla*, roams the tropical forests of Africa, where it is one of the most fascinating and intelligent animals on the planet. With their muscular builds and expressive faces, gorillas have earned a reputation as gentle giants, but they can be fierce when threatened.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Up to 400 pounds
- **Length / Height:** Up to 6 feet tall and 12 feet long when standing on all fours! That's as tall as a door!
- **Top Speed:** Can run up to 20 miles per hour! That's as fast as a speeding car in a school zone!

**Weapons:**
- Strong arms with a reach of 8 feet
- Powerful jaws
- Intimidating chest thump

**Fighting Style:**
Gorillas are primarily herbivores and typically avoid conflict, but when they do fight, they rely on their incredible strength and size. They often use their powerful arms to deliver devastating punches and may charge at opponents to intimidate them. When defending their territory, gorillas may thump their chests to assert dominance before resorting to physical confrontation.

### The Elephant

The African elephant, or *Loxodonta africana*, is the largest land mammal on Earth, thriving in savannas, forests, and grasslands across Africa. Known for their incredible intelligence and strong social bonds, elephants are also formidable opponents when it comes to defending themselves and their families.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Up to 14,000 pounds
- **Length / Height:** Up to 13 feet tall at the shoulder and 24 feet long! That's as long as two school buses!
- **Top Speed:** Can run up to 25 mph! That's as fast as a running horse!

**Weapons:**
- Tusks, up to 10 feet long!
- Trunk
- Massive body

**Fighting Style:**
Elephants are known for their strategic intelligence in confrontations. They often use their tusks for charging and goring, while their large size can knock down trees and obstacles in their path. In a fight, an elephant may swing its trunk to defend itself or use its body weight to push opponents away. Elephants also communicate with each other to strategize during confrontations.

## FAQ

In a direct confrontation, it is highly unlikely that a gorilla could kill an elephant due to the latter's immense size and strength. While a gorilla can inflict serious damage with its powerful punch, the elephant’s thick skin and sheer weight would make it nearly impossible for a gorilla to win.

**Who is stronger, a Gorilla or an Elephant?**  
While both animals are incredibly strong in their own right, elephants possess a significant advantage in strength due to their size. An adult elephant can weigh as much as 35 times a gorilla, making it far stronger overall.

**What are the odds in a Gorilla vs Elephant fight?**  
In a battle between a gorilla and an elephant, the odds favor the elephant overwhelmingly. Statistically, the elephant would likely win 9 out of 10 fights, primarily due to its sheer size, strength, and experience in defending against threats.

However, in captivity, such encounters could occur, but they would be extremely rare and highly discouraged by animal care professionals.
