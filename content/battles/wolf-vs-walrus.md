## Meet the Fighters

### The Wolf

The gray wolf, known scientifically as *Canis lupus*, roams across forests, tundras, and grasslands. Renowned for its intelligence and social structure, wolves are exceptional pack hunters, often working together to take down larger prey. With their keen senses and agile bodies, they are well-equipped for survival in the wild.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** 50-110 pounds
- **Length / Height:** Up to 6.5 feet long (including tail)
- **Top Speed:** Can run up to 40 mph
- **Bite Force / Strength:** Up to 1,500 PSI

**Weapons:**
- Powerful jaws (up to 1,500 PSI)
- Sharp claws
- Keen sense of smell

**Fighting Style:**
Wolves are known for their strategic approach to hunting and fighting. They often rely on teamwork, using their speed and agility to outmaneuver opponents. When engaging in a fight, wolves may first assess their opponent, using their keen senses to find weaknesses before launching an attack, either solo or as a pack.

### The Walrus

Meet the walrus, scientifically named *Odobenus rosmarus*. This marine mammal thrives in the chilly Arctic and sub-Arctic regions, where it spends much of its time lounging on ice floes or diving deep for food. Known for their impressive tusks and blubbery bodies, walruses are powerful creatures that can intimidate many potential threats.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Can weigh up to 2,000 pounds
- **Length / Height:** Up to 12 feet long
- **Top Speed:** Can swim up to 35 mph
- **Bite Force / Strength:** Not specified (approx. strong based on size)

**Weapons:**
- Tusks up to 3 feet long
- Strong flippers
- Massive body weight

**Fighting Style:**
Walruses often rely on their sheer size and strength in confrontations. With their impressive tusks, they can deliver powerful jabs. When threatened, they use their blubber and tough skin as natural armor. In a fight, a walrus would likely use its weight to its advantage, trying to overpower its opponent rather than outmaneuver them.
