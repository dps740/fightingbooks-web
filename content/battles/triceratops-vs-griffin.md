## Meet the Fighters

### The Triceratops

The Triceratops, scientifically known as *Triceratops horridus*, roamed the open plains and forests of North America around 68 to 66 million years ago. This remarkable dinosaur is well-known for its impressive size and strong defensive features, making it one of the most recognizable dinosaurs. With its three massive horns and a large bony frill, the Triceratops is a formidable presence in the prehistoric world.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** 12 tons
- **Length / Height:** 30 feet long
- **Top Speed:** 20 mph

**Weapons:**
- Horns up to 3 feet long
- Beak for biting
- Heavy body for charging

**Fighting Style:**
Triceratops primarily relies on its strength and the formidable weapons it possesses. When threatened, it can charge directly at its opponent, using its heavy body and large horns to inflict damage. This dinosaur is a solitary fighter, often defending itself against predators in a straightforward manner.

### The Griffin

The Griffin is a mythological creature that has captured imaginations for centuries. With the body of a lion and the head and wings of an eagle, it’s a majestic guardian of treasures and a symbol of strength and courage. Griffins are often depicted as creatures of the mountains and ancient ruins, soaring high above the ground, ready to defend their territory.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** (approx.) 1,000 pounds
- **Length / Height:** About 13 feet long and 6 feet tall at the shoulder
- **Top Speed:** Can fly up to 30 mph

**Weapons:**
- Sharp claws (up to 6 inches long)
- Powerful beak
- Talons for gripping

**Fighting Style:**
Griffins are aerial fighters that use their speed and agility to their advantage. They can swoop down from above, using their sharp claws and beak to attack their opponents. In battle, they tend to be evasive, relying on their flight capabilities to avoid direct confrontation and strike from unexpected angles.
