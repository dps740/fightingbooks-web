## Meet the Fighters

### The Jaguar

The jaguar, scientifically named *Panthera onca*, roams the lush rainforests, swamps, and grasslands of Central and South America. This big cat is renowned for its beautiful coat adorned with rosettes, which help it blend seamlessly into its surroundings. Jaguars are powerful predators, often considered the third largest big cat in the world.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** 250 lbs
- **Length / Height:** Up to 6 feet long! That's as long as a school bus is tall!
- **Top Speed:** Can run up to 50 miles per hour! That's faster than a speeding car in a school zone!
- **Bite Force / Strength:** 1,500 PSI

**Weapons:**
- Powerful jaws with a bite force of 1,500 PSI
- Sharp claws
- Stealthy approach

**Fighting Style:**
The jaguar is a master ambush predator, often stalking its prey silently before launching a powerful attack. With its incredible speed and strength, it prefers to fight solo rather than in packs, relying on its stealth and sharp claws to outmaneuver its opponents.

### The Giant Panda

The giant panda, known scientifically as *Ailuropoda melanoleuca*, inhabits the misty mountain bamboo forests of central China, particularly in the provinces of Sichuan, Shaanxi, and Gansu. With its adorable black-and-white markings, this bear is often perceived as cuddly. However, beneath that cute exterior lies a powerful animal that is incredibly well-adapted to its environment.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** 330 lbs
- **Length / Height:** Up to 6 feet long
- **Top Speed:** Can run up to 20 mph in short bursts
- **Bite Force / Strength:** 292 PSI

**Weapons:**
- Jaw muscles and teeth strong enough to crush thick bamboo stalks
- Sharp claws on powerful forearms for climbing and swiping
- A special sixth finger (pseudo-thumb) made from a modified wrist bone

**Fighting Style:**
While giant pandas are primarily herbivorous, they can defend themselves if threatened. Their fighting style combines their size and strength, using powerful swipes with their sharp claws, and they can climb trees quickly to escape danger. Though they are not aggressive by nature, they can be surprisingly fierce when provoked.
