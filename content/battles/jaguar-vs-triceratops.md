## Meet the Fighters

### The Jaguar

The jaguar, scientifically known as *Panthera onca*, is a powerful big cat that roams the rainforests, swamps, and grasslands of Central and South America. Renowned for their strength and agility, these magnificent felines are apex predators, meaning they're at the top of their food chain. Jaguars are known for their beautiful rosettes and impressive hunting skills, allowing them to take down prey many times their size!

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Up to 250 pounds
- **Length / Height:** Up to 6 feet long
- **Top Speed:** Can run up to 50 miles per hour
- **Bite Force / Strength:** 1,500 PSI

**Weapons:**
- Powerful jaws with a bite force of 1,500 PSI
- Sharp claws
- Stealthy approach

**Fighting Style:**
Jaguars are solitary hunters who often use stealth and ambush tactics to catch their prey by surprise. Their incredible speed and agility allow them to stalk and pounce, delivering powerful bites to critical areas. They are known to take down large animals, including deer and even caimans, showing their adaptability in various environments.

### The Triceratops

The triceratops, or *Triceratops horridus*, was a massive dinosaur that roamed the open plains and forests of North America during the Late Cretaceous period. With its impressive size and distinctive three horns, this herbivore was more than just a gentle giant; it was a formidable opponent for any predator. Triceratops were social creatures, often found in herds, and used their horns for defense against threats.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Around 12,000 pounds (or 12 tons)
- **Length / Height:** About 30 feet long
- **Top Speed:** 20 miles per hour

**Weapons:**
- Horns up to 3 feet long
- Beak for biting
- Heavy body for charging

**Fighting Style:**
Triceratops were not just passive grazers; they were equipped with powerful weapons! They could charge at predators with their massive weight and utilize their three sharp horns for defense. When threatened, they would likely stand their ground, forming a defensive posture with their bony frill and horns ready to attack.
