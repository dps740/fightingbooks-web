## Meet the Fighters

### The Cheetah

- **Length / Height:** Up to 4.5 feet tall at the shoulder and 3.5 to 4.5 feet long. That's about the length of a small sedan!

- **Bite Force / Strength:** Not provided

**Weapons:**
- Sharp claws (up to 2 inches long)
- Powerful jaws
- Speedy legs

**Fighting Style:**
Cheetahs use their extraordinary speed and stealth to ambush small to medium-sized animals, such as gazelles and impalas. While they usually hunt alone, they are capable of making calculated moves to outmaneuver their prey.

Meet the Spinosaurus, or *Spinosaurus aegypticus*, one of the largest carnivorous dinosaurs that ever roamed the Earth. This dinosaur lived in rivers and wetlands in North Africa, showcasing a fascinating blend of aquatic and terrestrial adaptations. With its massive body and distinctive sail, the Spinosaurus is a true giant of the prehistoric world.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Estimated 7-20 tons
- **Length / Height:** Up to 50-59 feet long! That's like TWO SCHOOL BUSES!
- **Top Speed:** Could move at about 12 mph! That's about as fast as a human sprinter — pretty quick for a 7-ton dinosaur!
- **Bite Force / Strength:** Not provided

**Weapons:**

- Powerful claws
- Huge sail on its back

**Fighting Style:**
The Spinosaurus is believed to have been an ambush predator, utilizing its size and strength to overpower prey. It likely hunted fish and other dinosaurs, using its long, crocodile-like snout to snatch its meals from the water. In a fight, the Spinosaurus would rely on its massive size and powerful claws to dominate its opponents.
