## Meet the Fighters

### The Wolf

The Gray Wolf, known scientifically as *Canis lupus*, roams the forests, tundras, and grasslands of North America, Europe, and Asia. These majestic animals are not just beautiful; they are also highly social creatures that live and hunt in packs, making them formidable predators. Known for their intelligence and adaptability, wolves have a reputation that strikes both fear and admiration in the hearts of many.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** 50-110 pounds
- **Length / Height:** Up to 6.5 feet long (including tail) - ABOUT AS LONG AS A SCHOOL BUS!
- **Top Speed:** Can run up to 40 mph - FASTER THAN A SPRINTING HUMAN!

**Weapons:**
- Powerful jaws (up to 1,500 PSI)
- Sharp claws
- Keen sense of smell

**Fighting Style:**
Wolves are strategic hunters that often use teamwork to take down larger prey. They rely on their speed and endurance, using pack tactics to outmaneuver and exhaust their targets. When attacking, they may ambush their prey or chase it down relentlessly, showcasing their stamina and agility.

### The Electric Eel

Despite its name, the Electric Eel (*Electrophorus electricus*) is actually a fish found in the murky freshwater rivers and swamps of the Amazon and Orinoco basins in South America. This fascinating creature has a reputation for its astonishing ability to generate electricity, which it uses to hunt and defend itself. With a sleek, elongated body, the electric eel can grow up to an impressive length, making it a noteworthy inhabitant of its aquatic environment.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Up to 44 pounds
- **Length / Height:** Up to 8 FEET long and 44 pounds — as long as a couch!
- **Top Speed:** Slow swimmers at about 3 mph

**Weapons:**
- Can discharge up to 860 VOLTS — enough to stun a horse or knock a human unconscious!
- Three separate electric organs make up 80% of their body — they're basically a living BATTERY!
- Can pulse electricity to make hidden prey twitch, revealing their hiding spots — like sonar but with LIGHTNING!

**Fighting Style:**
Electric eels utilize their electric shocks primarily for hunting and self-defense. They can deliver powerful bursts of electricity to stun prey, making it easier to swallow. When threatened, they can discharge their electricity to deter potential predators, effectively using their shocking abilities as both an offensive and defensive tactic.
