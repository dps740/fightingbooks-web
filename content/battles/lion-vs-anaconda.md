## Meet the Fighters

### The Lion

The lion, scientifically known as **Panthera leo**, reigns as one of the most iconic animals on the planet. Found primarily in the grasslands, savannas, and open woodlands of Africa and India, lions are celebrated for their majestic appearance and social structures. Living in prides, these big cats are not only formidable hunters but also symbols of strength and courage.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** 420 pounds
- **Length / Height:** 8 to 10 feet long from nose to tail
- **Top Speed:** 50 miles per hour (for short bursts)
- **Bite Force / Strength:** 650 PSI

**Weapons:**
- Powerful jaws with a bite force of 650 PSI
- Sharp claws up to 1.5 inches long
- Strong legs for pouncing

**Fighting Style:**
Lions are strategic hunters. They often work together in packs, using teamwork to ambush their prey. When it comes to fighting, a lion uses its strength and speed to pounce on an opponent, delivering powerful bites and sharp swipes with its claws. While they can fight solo, teamwork is their secret weapon, allowing them to take down much larger prey.

### The Anaconda

The **Green Anaconda**, or **Eunectes murinus**, is a true giant of the snake world! This massive reptile lives in the swamps and rivers of South America, thriving in its watery habitat and often hiding in plain sight thanks to its excellent camouflage. Anacondas are renowned for their strength and size, making them one of the largest snakes in the world.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** 500 pounds (or more)
- **Length / Height:** Up to 30 feet long
- **Top Speed:** 1.5 miles per hour (swimming)
- **Bite Force / Strength:** Not specified (but relies on constriction)

**Weapons:**
- Powerful muscles (30 feet long)
- Camouflage for sneaky attacks
- Strong jaws

**Fighting Style:**
Anacondas are ambush predators. They lie in wait, blending into their surroundings until they spot potential prey. When the moment is right, they strike with lightning speed, wrapping around their target and constricting it with immense strength. Anacondas prefer to fight in the water, where their size and power give them a considerable advantage.
