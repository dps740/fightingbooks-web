## Meet the Fighters

### The Hammerhead Shark

The hammerhead shark, scientifically known as *Sphyrna mokarran*, is one of the most distinctive sharks in the ocean. Known for its iconic hammer-shaped head, this shark can be found in warm tropical and temperate oceans worldwide, from vibrant coral reefs to the deep, mysterious open sea. Hammerheads are not just fascinating to look at; they are formidable predators, earning a reputation as ruthless hunters!

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Up to 1,000 pounds
- **Length / Height:** Up to 20 feet long
- **Top Speed:** 25 mph

**Weapons:**
- Wide hammer head that pins prey to the ocean floor—like using a spatula!
- Rows of razor-sharp serrated teeth that constantly regrow
- Incredible speed bursts for ambush attacks

**Fighting Style:**
Hammerhead sharks are known for their cunning hunting tactics. They often hunt alone or in small groups, using their wide heads to pin down prey like stingrays and then delivering a swift, powerful bite. Their 360-degree vision ensures that nothing sneaks up on them, making them both effective hunters and skilled fighters.

### The Electric Eel

Despite its name, the electric eel, or *Electrophorus electricus*, is actually a fish! Found in the murky freshwater rivers and swamps of the Amazon and Orinoco basins in South America, the electric eel is famous for its shocking abilities. This slippery predator can reach impressive lengths and has a reputation for stunning its prey before devouring them whole.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Up to 44 pounds
- **Length / Height:** Up to 8 feet long
- **Top Speed:** Approximately 3 mph

**Weapons:**
- Can discharge up to 860 volts—enough to stun a horse!
- Three separate electric organs make up 80% of their body—basically a living battery!
- Can pulse electricity to reveal hidden prey—like sonar but with lightning!

**Fighting Style:**
Electric eels are masters of ambush. They can electrocute their prey to incapacitate them, making it easy to catch fish, amphibians, and even small mammals. When it comes to defense, their thick mucus coating protects them against parasites, while their ability to breathe air allows them to survive in oxygen-poor environments.
