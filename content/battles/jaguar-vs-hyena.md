## Meet the Fighters

### The Jaguar

The jaguar, scientifically known as *Panthera onca*, prowls the lush rainforests, swamps, and grasslands of Central and South America. Recognized as the third largest big cat globally, these magnificent creatures are not just beautiful but also powerful hunters. With their iconic rosettes that help them blend into the dappled sunlight of the jungle, jaguars are formidable predators.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Up to 250 pounds
- **Length / Height:** Up to 6 feet long! That's as long as a school bus is tall!
- **Top Speed:** Can run up to 50 miles per hour! That's faster than a speeding car in a school zone!
- **Bite Force / Strength:** 1,500 PSI

**Weapons:**
- Powerful jaws with a bite force of 1,500 PSI
- Sharp claws
- Stealthy approach

**Fighting Style:**
Jaguars are known for their ambush tactics. They often stalk their prey silently before launching a powerful attack. Their incredible strength allows them to take down large animals like deer and caimans. When it comes to fighting, they rely on their stealth, surprise, and brute force to overpower opponents, making them a solitary but lethal force.

### The Hyena

Meet the spotted hyena, or *Crocuta crocuta*, a clever and resilient predator found in the savannas, grasslands, and woodlands of Africa. Often misunderstood due to their scavenging habits, hyenas are skilled hunters and are known for their complex social structures. A clan of hyenas can have up to 80 members, showcasing their cooperative hunting prowess.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Up to 190 pounds
- **Length / Height:** Up to 5.5 to 6.5 feet long! That's about as long as a small sedan!
- **Top Speed:** Can run up to 37 miles per hour! That's faster than a speeding car in a school zone!
- **Bite Force / Strength:** 1,100 PSI

**Weapons:**
- Powerful jaws with a bite force of 1,100 PSI!
- Sharp claws
- Strong legs for running

**Fighting Style:**
Hyenas are known for their pack behavior, which gives them an advantage in combat situations. While they can hunt alone, they often work together to take down larger prey. Their intimidating laugh can scare off rivals and is part of their unique communication strategy. When faced with danger, they may use their numbers to outsmart and overpower their opponents.
