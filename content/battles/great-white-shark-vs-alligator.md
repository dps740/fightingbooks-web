## Meet the Fighters

### The Great White Shark

The Great White Shark, scientifically known as *Carcharodon carcharias*, is one of the ocean's most formidable predators. Found in oceans worldwide, especially in coastal waters, these sharks are known for their powerful build and fearsome reputation. With a diet that includes seals, fish, and even other sharks, the Great White is a carnivorous powerhouse!

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Over 2,400 pounds
- **Length / Height:** Up to 20 feet long!
- **Top Speed:** Can swim up to 25 miles per hour!
- **Bite Force / Strength:** Stronger than a T-Rex!

**Weapons:**
- Jaws with 300 sharp teeth, each up to 3 inches long!
- Powerful tail for swift movement
- Speedy body for quick strikes

**Fighting Style:**
Great White Sharks are ambush hunters. They often stalk their prey from below and use their speed to launch surprise attacks. Their powerful jaws and sharp teeth allow them to inflict devastating bites, making them effective solo fighters in the vast ocean.

### The Alligator

Meet the American Alligator, or *Alligator mississippiensis*! This cold-blooded reptile thrives in the swamps, rivers, and lakes of the southeastern USA. Recognized for their armored skin and sharp teeth, alligators are skilled predators that command respect in their habitats. With a diet that includes fish, birds, and even deer, they have a BIG appetite!

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Up to 1,000 pounds
- **Length / Height:** Up to 13 feet long! 
- **Top Speed:** Can run up to 20 mph on land!
- **Bite Force / Strength:** 2,125 PSI

**Weapons:**
- Powerful jaws with a bite force of 2,125 PSI
- Sharp teeth for gripping prey
- Long tail for powerful swimming and balance

**Fighting Style:**
Alligators are ambush predators as well, using their camouflage to blend into their surroundings. They strike quickly with their powerful jaws, often dragging prey underwater to drown it. They are equally effective in water and on land, making them versatile fighters.
