## Meet the Fighters

### The Crocodile

The Saltwater Crocodile, known scientifically as *Crocodylus porosus*, is a formidable predator found in coastal areas, rivers, and swamps. Renowned for its fierce reputation, this reptile can be found basking in the sun or lurking just beneath the water's surface, waiting for its next meal.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Up to 2,200 pounds
- **Length / Height:** Up to 23 feet long
- **Top Speed:** Can swim up to 20 mph! That's as fast as a running dog!

**Weapons:**
- Powerful jaws with a bite force of 3,700 pounds!
- Sharp teeth
- Long tail

**Fighting Style:**
Crocodiles are expert ambush predators. They rely on stealth, camouflaging themselves in water or on riverbanks to catch unsuspecting prey. When attacking, they use their powerful jaws to clamp down on their victim with incredible force, often dragging them underwater to drown. Crocodiles usually hunt alone, showcasing their solitary hunting skills.

### The Eagle

The Bald Eagle, or *Haliaeetus leucocephalus*, is a majestic bird of prey often seen near large bodies of open water. With its impressive wingspan and sharp eyesight, this bird commands respect in its habitat. The Bald Eagle primarily hunts fish but is also known to take down small mammals and other birds.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Up to 14 pounds
- **Length / Height:** About 3.5 feet long (wingspan of up to 7.5 feet!)
- **Top Speed:** Can dive at speeds of 30-40 mph! That's as fast as a car on the highway!

**Weapons:**
- Sharp talons (up to 2.5 inches long)
- Strong beak
- Keen eyesight

**Fighting Style:**
Eagles are known for their incredible aerial skills. They soar high in the sky, using their keen eyesight to spot potential prey from great distances. When they dive, they use their sharp talons to grab their target with precision. Bald Eagles typically hunt alone, relying on their sight and speed to catch their meals.
