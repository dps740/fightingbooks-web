## Meet the Fighters

### The Gorilla

The gorilla, scientifically known as *Gorilla gorilla*, roams the tropical forests of Africa. These gentle giants are known for their impressive strength and social behavior, often living in groups led by a dominant male. Gorillas are herbivores, munching on a variety of leaves, stems, fruit, and flowers, making them peaceful yet powerful inhabitants of their habitat.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Up to 400 pounds!
- **Length / Height:** Up to 6 feet tall and 12 feet long when standing on all fours! That's as tall as a door!
- **Top Speed:** Can run up to 20 miles per hour! That's as fast as a speeding car in a school zone!

**Weapons:**
- Strong arms with a reach of 8 feet
- Powerful jaws
- Intimidating chest thump

**Fighting Style:**
Gorillas are known for their brute strength. They typically fight using their powerful arms, delivering heavy punches or grappling with opponents. Though they are social animals, a lone gorilla will stand its ground if threatened. Their chest thumping is a display of dominance, often used to intimidate rivals without physical confrontation.

### The Python

The Green Python, or *Morelia viridis*, is native to the tropical rainforests of New Guinea and Australia. Renowned for its beautiful green scales, this snake is a master of camouflage, often blending seamlessly into the foliage. As a carnivore, the python's diet consists primarily of small mammals and birds, which it captures using its impressive constriction technique.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Up to 50 pounds (approx.)
- **Length / Height:** Up to 13 feet long!
- **Top Speed:** Can strike at speeds of 3 mph! That's like running as fast as a slow jogger!

**Weapons:**
- Strong muscles for constriction (up to 1 foot in diameter)
- Sharp teeth
- Camouflage

**Fighting Style:**
Pythons are ambush predators, often lying in wait for unsuspecting prey to pass by. Once they strike, they use their strong muscles to constrict and suffocate their catch. Their silent movement and ability to blend into their environment make them formidable opponents, even against larger animals.
