## Meet the Fighters

### The Elephant

With its impressive size and intelligence, this gentle giant has earned a reputation as one of the most powerful animals on land. Elephants are herbivores, consuming a staggering amount of food every day, which fuels their incredible strength and massive body.

**Fighting Style:**

### The Octopus

The common octopus, or *Octopus vulgaris*, is a fascinating creature that inhabits oceans and seas around the world. Renowned for its intelligence and adaptability, this cephalopod is a master of disguise, capable of changing its color and texture to blend in with its surroundings. As a carnivorous hunter, the octopus preys on crabs, fish, and mollusks, using its exceptional skills to outsmart and capture its next meal.

**Physical Stats:**
- **Weight:** Up to 22 pounds
- **Length / Height:** Up to 3.5 feet long! That's about the length of a SCHOOL BUS! (35 feet)!
- **Top Speed:** Can jet through water at 25 mph! That's faster than most people can swim — by A LOT!

**Weapons:**
- Beak (1 inch)
- Ink cloud
- Camouflage

**Fighting Style:**
Octopuses rely on stealth and intelligence in battles. They can use their ink cloud to create a smokescreen and escape from predators. With their ability to camouflage, they can ambush unsuspecting prey or hide from threats. If they engage in direct confrontation, they can use their beak to bite and their strong arms to grapple with opponents.
