## Content Strategy

### Battle Pages (Primary Traffic Driver)

**URL Structure:** `/battles/[animal1]-vs-[animal2]`

**Page Template:**
1. **Hero Section**
   - Title: "[Animal 1] vs [Animal 2]: Who Would Win?"
   - Quick verdict teaser
   - CTA: "Generate Your Custom Battle Book"

2. **Tale of the Tape**
   - Side-by-side comparison table
   - Size, weight, speed, bite force, weapons, habitat

3. **Animal 1 Deep Dive** (300-500 words)
   - Physical characteristics
   - Hunting/fighting abilities
   - Real-world examples of combat

4. **Animal 2 Deep Dive** (300-500 words)
   - Same structure

5. **The Battle Analysis** (400-600 words)
   - Scenario breakdown
   - Advantages/disadvantages each side
   - Expert opinions (cite sources)

6. **The Verdict** (200-300 words)
   - Clear winner declaration
   - Reasoning summary
   - Edge cases ("In water, the crocodile wins, but on land...")

7. **CTA Section**
   - "Want to see this battle illustrated?"
   - Generate book button
   - Preview of what the book looks like

8. **Related Battles**
   - Internal links to 4-6 related matchups
   - "If you liked Lion vs Tiger, try..."

9. **FAQ Section** (for featured snippets)
   - "Who would win in a fight, lion or tiger?"
   - "Are tigers stronger than lions?"
   - "Has a lion ever fought a tiger?"

10. **Sources**
    - Cite National Geographic, scientific papers, zoo studies
    - Builds credibility and E-E-A-T

**Word Count Target:** 1,800-2,500 words per page

   - Hippo vs Rhino, Elephant vs Rhino, Lion vs Hyena

## Technical SEO

### On-Page
- [ ] Title tags: "[Animal 1] vs [Animal 2]: Who Would Win? [2026]"
- [ ] Meta descriptions: Compelling, include verdict teaser, 150-160 chars
- [ ] H1: Primary keyword
- [ ] H2s: Secondary keywords, questions
- [ ] Image alt text: Descriptive, include keywords
- [ ] Internal linking: Every page links to 4-6 related battles
- [ ] Schema markup: FAQ, Article, BreadcrumbList

### Technical
- [ ] Google Search Console verified
- [ ] Sitemap.xml submitted
- [ ] Robots.txt configured
- [ ] Page speed <3s (Vercel = good)
- [ ] Mobile responsive (Next.js = good)
- [ ] HTTPS (Vercel = automatic)

### Indexing Priority
1. Homepage
2. Top 5 battle pages (highest volume keywords)
3. Book list reference page
4. Hub pages
5. Remaining battle pages
